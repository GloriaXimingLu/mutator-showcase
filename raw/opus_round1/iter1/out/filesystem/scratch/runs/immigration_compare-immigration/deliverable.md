       I. Purpose and Scope  
      This memorandum reviews the certified Application for Permanent Employment Certification (ETA Form 9089, PERM Case No. A-18247-63910, filed March 15, 2025; certified May 22, 2025) against the beneficiary’s supporting credentials to identify any gaps between (a) the minimum requirements and attestations stated on the application and (b) the education, experience, and certifications actually documented in the file. This review is undertaken in connection with the employer’s June 4, 2025 request that counsel confirm         “    whether there are any issues with the credentials matching the PERM requirements    ”         and identify any missing or supplemental documentation.  
          Important context. The credential gaps identified below are therefore most consequential for (i) PERM audit/revocation exposure and (ii) the forthcoming I-140 petition. The concurrently planned H-1B (I-129) petition is a separate filing governed by a distinct         “    specialty occupation    ”         standard and is not the subject of this analysis, although several of the same documents will be reused.

           B. Critical Findings  
       Finding 1 — Required AWS certification not held (J.B.5)  
      Section J.B.5 states that the         “    AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification or equivalent [is] required,    ”         and the addendum to J.B.5 reiterates this as a mandatory minimum. The certifications compilation documents only two credentials:  
              AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner         — foundational level, issued January 18, 2022,             expired January 18, 2025         (i.e., expired             before         the March 15, 2025 filing and not renewed); and  
              TensorFlow Developer Certificate         — issued March 10, 2023 by             Google    , no expiration, framework-specific to TensorFlow.  
      The compilation expressly states:             “        Counsel specifically inquired whether the beneficiary holds the AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification or any other AWS specialty-level certification; the beneficiary confirmed she does not hold any such certification.        ”  
          Analysis.         Neither held credential is an         “    equivalent    ”         to the AWS ML – Specialty certification:  
          The AWS Cloud Practitioner is a             foundational         credential covering general cloud literacy; it does not test machine-learning specialization, SageMaker, or model deployment, and it is             expired    . At the priority date the beneficiary therefore held             no valid AWS certification of any kind    .  
          The TensorFlow Developer Certificate is issued by             Google    , is             framework-specific         (TensorFlow model building), and does not cover AWS ML services, deployment, or ML operations that the AWS ML – Specialty tests and that the position itself requires (AWS SageMaker deployment per J.B.5(d)). It is not a functional substitute.  
      Because the certification is framed as a mandatory minimum (    “    required    ”    ), the beneficiary does not meet a stated minimum special requirement as of the priority date.  
          Risk.         (i)             PERM revocation         — DOL may revoke a certification within one year of filing for         “    cause    ”         including misrepresentation or a material error in the application (20 CFR 656.32). (ii)             I-140 denial         — USCIS verifies that the beneficiary possessed the minimum requirements (including special requirements) as of the priority date; a missing mandatory certification is a common basis for denial. (iii)             Business-necessity/consistency exposure         — if the certification is not in fact a pre-hire minimum, its inclusion as a         “    required    ”         minimum is itself problematic (it must be job-related, consistent with business necessity under 20 CFR 656.17(h), and applied uniformly to U.S. workers).  
        Finding 2 — Attestation inconsistency (K.3 / K.4)  
      Sections K.3 and K.4 attest         “    Yes    ”         that the beneficiary         “    possess[es] the special skills and other requirements stated in Section J.B.5    ”         and         “    is qualified for the job opportunity.    ”         Because J.B.5 includes the AWS ML – Specialty certification as a required minimum, and the beneficiary does not hold it (Finding 1), these attestations are             not supported by the credential file         and are potentially inaccurate. This is the legal consequence of Finding 1 and should be treated as a single, integrated issue for remediation.  
         C. Medium Findings  
       Finding 3 —         “    Proficiency in R    ”         not corroborated (J.B.5(e))  
      J.B.5(e) requires         “    proficiency in Python and R programming languages.    ”         The DataBridge experience letter — the principal document supporting the qualifying experience — states that the beneficiary         “    Used Python as the primary programming language,             with some use of R for statistical reporting and ad hoc data analysis    .    ”         The résumé lists R as         “        intermediate    ”         (versus Python         “    expert    ”    ). The NC State transcript shows one relevant course,             ST 558 — Statistical Computing with R (grade A-)    , which provides academic support but not work-experience corroboration.  
          Analysis.         The experience letter’s own language (    “    some use,    ”         “    ad hoc    ”    ) is             below         the         “    proficiency    ”         standard stated in J.B.5(e) and, if produced in an audit, could be read to             contradict         the requirement rather than satisfy it. Python proficiency is well supported; R proficiency is the weak point.  
        Finding 4 — Supervisory scope gap (J.B.5)  
      J.B.5 states the position         “    Will supervise a team of 3–5 ML engineers.    ”         The DataBridge letter and the résumé both document that the beneficiary         “        Led a team of 2 junior data scientists    .    ”  
          Analysis.         Two issues: (i)             quantitative         — 2 reports is below the 3–5 range stated; and (ii)             terminological         — the reports were         “    junior data scientists,    ”         not         “    ML engineers.    ”         If prior supervisory experience of a 3–5-person team is a true pre-hire minimum, the documented experience falls short. If supervision of 3–5 is a forward-looking job duty (the work to be performed at Prismex) rather than a pre-hire minimum, the beneficiary’s demonstrated supervisory capability (leading 2) may suffice, but the application should not be read to require prior 3–5-person supervisory experience that the file does not document.  
        Finding 5 — Missing NC State Research Assistant experience letter (J.C, Entry 2)  
      The application lists the NC State Research Assistant position (August 15, 2016 – May 15, 2018, 20 hrs/week) as Experience Entry 2 (    “    related occupation    ”    ). The HR email confirms             no experience verification letter         has been obtained (Professor Helen Tsai is on sabbatical and unresponsive). The transcript documents the appointment, supervisor, and NSF funding source (Grant No. IIS-1742956) but does             not         describe duties.  
          Analysis.         Because this experience was gained             during         the master’s program and is             part-time    , it is             not counted         toward the 5-year post-master’s minimum (K.2 correctly relies only on DataBridge). The impact is therefore limited. Nonetheless, it is a listed experience entry, and in an audit DOL expects each claimed experience entry to be documented; the transcript alone is a partial substitute.  
        Finding 6 — FEIN discrepancy (C.7 vs. PWD §1)  
      The PERM application (C.7) lists the employer FEIN as             84-3291756    . The Prevailing Wage Determination (§1) lists the employer FEIN as             84-3291047    . The employer name and address match across both documents, but the FEINs differ.  
          Analysis.         The FEIN is a key employer identifier that must be consistent across the PWD, the PERM, and the I-140. A mismatch suggests a clerical error on one document and must be reconciled before the I-140 is filed. If the PWD bears an incorrect FEIN, counsel should assess any impact on the validity of the prevailing-wage support for the certified PERM.

         VI. Remedial Recommendations  
       Priority 1 — Cure the certification gap (Findings 1 & 2)  
              Obtain the AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification immediately.         Schedule the beneficiary for the exam at the earliest available date. This is the most direct cure and should be completed             before         the I-140 is filed and before any audit response is required.  
              Reconcile the nature of the requirement.         Determine, on the record, whether the AWS ML – Specialty certification is (a) a             true pre-hire minimum         that the beneficiary must have held as of the priority date, or (b) a             hire/post-offer condition         to be satisfied before commencement. This determination drives the remediation path:  
              If a true pre-filing minimum:         the beneficiary did not possess it at the priority date, creating I-140 denial risk and PERM revocation exposure. Evaluate whether to (i) proceed to the I-140 with the certification now obtained, supported by a business-necessity justification and a prepared audit defense, or (ii) withdraw and             re-file a new PERM         with the certification obtained             before         the new filing and with consistent K.3/K.4 attestations. The latter is the cleaner but slower path and must be weighed against the beneficiary’s STEM-OPT expiration (September 15, 2025).  
              If a hire/post-offer condition:         it should not have been stated as a         “    required    ”         minimum in J.B.5, and the K.3/K.4 attestations are overstated. Document the employer’s actual hiring practice (i.e., that the certification is required before start, not before filing) and assess whether the discrepancy is curable by explanation or whether a new filing is warranted to avoid an inconsistency the DOL could challenge.  
              Renew or replace the expired AWS Cloud Practitioner    , or rely on the new specialty certification as the operative AWS credential. Do not present the expired Cloud Practitioner as a current qualification.  
              Build an audit-defense file         documenting (i) the business necessity of the certification requirement under 20 CFR 656.17(h) (job-relatedness to a Senior ML Engineer role deploying on AWS SageMaker), and (ii) the uniform application of the requirement to all applicants during recruitment.  
        Priority 2 — Corroborate R proficiency (Finding 3)  
              Obtain a revised or supplemental DataBridge experience letter         that accurately and specifically describes the beneficiary’s use of R — the types of statistical reporting/analyses performed, frequency, complexity, and independence — to support         “    proficiency.    ”         If R use was genuinely limited,             reassess whether                 “        proficiency in R        ”                 is a true minimum    ; if it is not, consider whether the requirement should be softened (e.g.,         “    working knowledge of R    ”    ) to avoid an inconsistency between the stated minimum and the experience letter. Any change to the stated minimum has PERM implications and must be evaluated carefully given the certification is already issued.  
              Supplement         with the ST 558 transcript entry and, if available, examples of R-based statistical reporting deliverables (redacted as needed).  
        Priority 3 — Address the supervisory scope (Finding 4)  
              Clarify whether prior supervision of a 3–5-person ML-engineer team is a true pre-hire minimum.         If it is a forward-looking job duty, document that the beneficiary’s demonstrated supervisory experience (leading 2 junior data scientists, plus mentorship/code review) satisfies the supervisory             capability         requirement. If prior 3–5-person supervision is a true minimum, obtain a supplemental letter detailing the full scope of her leadership responsibilities, or reassess the requirement as above.  
        Priority 4 — Document the Research Assistant experience (Finding 5)  
              Continue efforts to obtain an NC State experience letter.         Per the HR email, pursue the department administrator or university HR as a backup to Professor Tsai. If a letter cannot be obtained, rely on the transcript (which documents the appointment, supervisor, and NSF funding)             plus         a detailed sworn personal statement from the beneficiary describing her RA duties, corroborated by her two co-authored conference publications (EMNLP 2018; ACL 2017). Because this experience is not counted toward the 5-year post-master’s minimum, the residual risk is limited.  
        Priority 5 — Reconcile the FEIN (Finding 6)  
              Immediately verify the correct FEIN         against the employer’s IRS records/tax filings. Determine which document (PWD or PERM) bears the error. If the PWD is incorrect, assess whether a corrected PWD is needed and the impact, if any, on the certified PERM.             Ensure the I-140 uses the correct, consistent FEIN         and document the reconciliation in the file.  
        Priority 6 — Documentation clean-up (Findings 7–9)  
              State the experience duration accurately         (~6 years 9 months as of the priority date) in any audit response or I-140 support letter; do not repeat         “    approximately 7 years.    ”  
              Obtain/retain degree-conferral evidence dated on or before the priority date         (e.g., a prior transcript copy or the NC State degree-conferral verification) for audit readiness, in addition to the May 20, 2025 transcript.  
              Re-issue the certifications compilation         with a PERM-appropriate caption (and have the attorney of record, Craig Halloran, sign or co-sign) for consistency across the PERM/I-140 record.

             Recommended path.         The education and experience-quantity requirements are satisfied.         VIII. Conclusion  
      The PERM application is internally consistent on education, prevailing wage, and the broad experience quantity, and the beneficiary plainly exceeds the 5-year post-master’s experience threshold. However, the supporting credentials do             not         fully substantiate every stated minimum requirement. The single most serious gap is the             absence of the required AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification    , which is inconsistent with the K.3/K.4 attestations and creates material I-140 and revocation risk. Secondary gaps in R-proficiency corroboration, supervisory scope, the missing RA letter, and the FEIN discrepancy are readily curable with supplemental documentation and reconciliation. We recommend the remediation steps in Section VI be initiated immediately and completed before the I-140 is filed, with a decision on the certification requirement’s character (pre-filing minimum vs. hire condition) made this week.
