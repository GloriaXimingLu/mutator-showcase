       Our Company  
      Crestline Software, Inc. is an enterprise software-as-a-service (    “    SaaS    ”    ) company providing an AI-driven supply chain optimization platform to Fortune 1000 manufacturers and retailers. We were incorporated in the State of Delaware on March 14, 2017, and our principal executive offices are located at 4200 Innovation Drive, Suite 800, Austin, Texas 78759.  
      Our platform consists of the             Crestline Nexus         unified SaaS platform for end-to-end supply chain optimization and the             SupplyMind         AI engine, which provides proprietary machine learning models for demand forecasting, inventory optimization, and logistics routing. We serve customers across manufacturing, retail, logistics, and consumer packaged goods verticals. As of December 31, 2024, we had 1,247 employees and served 487 customers, including 34 customers contributing annual recurring revenue (    “    ARR    ”    ) in excess of $1.0 million.  
      We operate through our wholly-owned subsidiary, Crestline Software UK Ltd., incorporated in England and Wales, which supports our European operations. We also maintain offices in San Francisco, California; London, United Kingdom; and Bangalore, India.  
        Our Strengths  
              Leading AI-driven supply chain optimization platform.         Our proprietary SupplyMind AI engine combines machine learning, advanced statistical modeling, and real-time data integration to deliver predictive and prescriptive supply chain intelligence.  
              Strong revenue growth and highly recurring revenue model.         Total revenue grew 45.3% year-over-year, from $128.9 million in fiscal year 2023 to $187.3 million in fiscal year 2024. Subscription revenue accounted for 90.0% of total revenue in fiscal year 2024, and ARR was $211.6 million as of December 31, 2024.  
              Best-in-class net retention.         Our dollar-based net retention rate was 132% for fiscal year 2024, evidencing strong upsell and cross-sell dynamics within our customer base.  
              Robust intellectual property portfolio.         Our intellectual property portfolio includes 47 issued U.S. patents, 12 pending U.S. patent applications, and 8 issued foreign patents covering core algorithmic innovations and data processing methods. Material trademarks include         “    Crestline,    ”         “    Crestline Nexus,    ”         and         “    SupplyMind.    ”  
              Enterprise-grade security and compliance.         We achieved SOC 2 Type II certification in August 2024 and maintain compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (    “    GDPR    ”    ) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (    “    CCPA    ”    ).

            RISK FACTORS  
      An investment in our Class A common stock involves a high degree of risk. Before deciding whether to invest in our Class A common stock, you should carefully consider the specific risks set forth below, together with all of the other information contained in this prospectus, including our consolidated financial statements and the related notes. The occurrence of any of the following risks could materially and adversely affect our business, financial condition, results of operations, and prospects, in which case the trading price of our Class A common stock could decline, and you may lose all or part of your investment.  
       Risks Related to Our Business and Industry  
          We have a history of net losses, we may not achieve or maintain profitability, and we may not be able to generate sufficient cash to service our indebtedness.  
      We have incurred net losses since inception. We reported a net loss of $24.8 million and $27.4 million for the fiscal years ended December 31, 2024 and 2023, respectively. As of December 31, 2024, we had an accumulated deficit of $312.6 million. We expect to continue to incur operating expenses and may continue to incur net losses for the foreseeable future as we invest in research and development, sales and marketing, and international expansion. We may not achieve or maintain profitability in the future.  
          We are subject to a term loan facility that contains financial covenants and other restrictions, and our indebtedness could adversely affect our financial condition.  
      As of December 31, 2024, we had $40.0 million of outstanding indebtedness under our senior secured term loan facility with Kestridge West Credit Partners (the         “    Term Loan    ”    ), which matures on December 15, 2027 and bears interest at a rate per annum equal to the Secured Overnight Financing Rate (    “    SOFR    ”    ) plus 3.25%, subject to a SOFR floor of 1.00%. The Term Loan is secured by a first-priority security interest in substantially all of our assets, including our intellectual property, and is guaranteed by our UK subsidiary, subject to a cap of £5.0 million. The credit agreement governing the Term Loan contains financial covenants, including a minimum liquidity requirement of $30.0 million and an ARR-based leverage ratio not to exceed 2.0x. As of December 31, 2024, we were in compliance with all financial covenants, with liquidity of $137.0 million and a leverage ratio of approximately 0.19x. Our ability to comply with these covenants may be affected by events beyond our control, and a breach could result in an event of default and acceleration of our indebtedness.  
      The credit agreement also requires us to provide the lender with written notice not less than 30 days prior to the consummation of any initial public offering of our equity securities. If we fail to provide this notice on a timely basis, we could be in default under the credit agreement, which could result in acceleration of the Term Loan.  
          The credit agreement contains a change-of-control provision that could result in acceleration of our indebtedness.  
      The credit agreement provides that a         “    change of control,    ”         as defined therein, including the existing stockholders of the Company as of the closing date of the credit agreement ceasing to hold, directly or indirectly, at least 50% of the aggregate voting power of our then-outstanding capital stock (including following an initial public offering), would constitute an event of default permitting the lender to accelerate all outstanding obligations. Based on the dual-class capital structure to be adopted in connection with this offering, our co-founders are expected to retain approximately 71.8% of the aggregate voting power following this offering, and we do not believe this offering will trigger a change of control. However, future transactions or transfers of Class B common stock could cause the existing holders to fall below the 50% threshold, which could result in acceleration of the Term Loan.  
          We are party to pending litigation that could result in significant costs and liabilities, and an adverse judgment could trigger a default under our credit agreement.  
      On September 12, 2024, Optivance, Inc. filed a patent infringement lawsuit against us in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Texas (Case No. 6:24-cv-00831), alleging that certain features of our SupplyMind platform infringe U.S. Patent No. 11,284,107, relating to         “    machine-learning-based inventory forecasting.    ”         We believe the claims are without merit and intend to vigorously defend against them. Based on the advice of outside patent counsel, we estimate the potential range of loss, in the event of an adverse judgment, to be between $5.0 million and $20.0 million, although we consider an unfavorable outcome to have a low probability. The lawsuit is in the early stages, and no trial date has been set. In addition, under our credit agreement, one or more judgments for the payment of money in excess of $5.0 million in the aggregate that remain undischarged for 60 days would constitute an event of default. An adverse judgment at the upper end of the estimated range could therefore trigger a default under our credit agreement if not bonded, appealed, or otherwise stayed.  
          Our revenue is concentrated among a limited number of customers, and the loss of one or more of these customers could adversely affect our results of operations.  
      No single customer accounted for more than 10% of total revenue during fiscal year 2024 or 2023. However, our largest customer, Harmon Industries Inc., represented approximately 8.2% of our total revenue in fiscal year 2024, and our top 10 customers collectively represented approximately 31.7% of our total revenue in fiscal year 2024. The loss of, or a material reduction in purchases by, one or more of these customers could adversely affect our business, financial condition, and results of operations.  
          We depend on our key personnel, including our co-founders, and the loss of one or more of them could harm our business.  
      Our success depends substantially on the continued services and performance of our senior management, particularly our co-founders, Arun Ramaswamy, our Chief Executive Officer, and Katerina         “    Kate    ”         Litvanova, our Chief Technology Officer. The loss of the services of either co-founder could adversely affect our business. In addition, our credit agreement provides that the cessation of Arun Ramaswamy as Chief Executive Officer, unless a successor reasonably acceptable to the lender is appointed within 90 days, would constitute a change of control and an event of default.  
          If we fail to maintain an effective system of internal control over financial reporting, we may not be able to accurately report our financial results or prevent fraud.  
      As an emerging growth company, we are not required to include an auditor’s attestation report on our internal control over financial reporting in this prospectus. However, we will be required to comply with the internal control over financial reporting requirements of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2004 beginning with our second annual report. If we identify material weaknesses in our internal control over financial reporting, or if we fail to maintain effective internal controls, we may not be able to accurately report our financial results.  
          Our ability to use our net operating loss carryforwards and certain other tax attributes may be limited.  
      As of December 31, 2024, we had approximately $228.0 million of federal net operating loss (    “    NOL    ”    ) carryforwards and approximately $166.0 million of state NOL carryforwards. Our ability to use these NOL carryforwards may be limited under Section 382 of the Internal Revenue Code if we experience an         “    ownership change,    ”         which could occur in connection with this offering or other equity transactions. We have not yet completed a Section 382 study, and the limitation, if any, on our ability to use our NOL carryforwards has not been determined.  
          We face intense competition and may lack sufficient financial resources to maintain or improve our competitive position.  
      The market for supply chain optimization software is competitive and rapidly evolving. We compete with legacy enterprise resource planning vendors, point-solution providers, and other emerging AI-native platforms. Some of our competitors have greater financial, technical, and marketing resources than we do.  
          Our international operations subject us to additional risks.  
      We conduct operations in the United Kingdom and India through our subsidiary and offices located in those jurisdictions. Our international operations are subject to risks, including regulatory requirements (including GDPR), currency exchange rate fluctuations, political and economic instability, and compliance with foreign laws.  
          If we fail to protect our intellectual property, our competitive position could be impaired.  
      Our success depends in part on our ability to protect our intellectual property. We rely on a combination of patents, trademarks, trade secrets, and contractual rights. The steps we take to protect our intellectual property may be inadequate. In addition, we may be subject to claims that we have infringed the intellectual property rights of others, which could result in significant costs and could require us to redesign our products.  
        Risks Related to Our Class A Common Stock and This Offering  
          Because our co-founders will control a majority of the voting power of our outstanding capital stock, they will have the ability to control the outcome of matters submitted to our stockholders for approval.  
      Upon the completion of this offering, our co-founders will hold all of the outstanding shares of Class B common stock, representing approximately 71.8% of the total voting power of our outstanding capital stock. As a result, our co-founders will have the ability to control the outcome of matters submitted to our stockholders for approval, including the election of directors, amendments to our Restated Certificate, and approval of significant corporate transactions. This concentration of voting control will limit the ability of holders of Class A common stock to influence corporate matters for the foreseeable future.  
          Our Class B common stock has no sunset provision, and the dual-class structure may persist indefinitely.  
      Our Restated Certificate provides that shares of Class B common stock will convert into shares of Class A common stock only upon (i) transfer to a person or entity that is not a permitted transferee or (ii) the death or incapacity of the holder. There is no time-based or ownership-percentage sunset provision. As a result, the dual-class structure and the concentration of voting control with our co-founders may persist indefinitely. In addition, certain index providers may exclude companies with multi-class share structures from their indices, which could limit the demand for, and liquidity of, our Class A common stock.  
          We are a                 “        controlled company        ”                 within the meaning of the Nasdaq listing rules and may rely on exemptions from certain corporate governance requirements.  
      Because our co-founders will control a majority of the voting power of our outstanding capital stock following this offering, we will be a         “    controlled company    ”         within the meaning of the Nasdaq listing rules. As a controlled company, we may elect to rely on exemptions from certain Nasdaq corporate governance requirements, including the requirement that a majority of our board of directors consist of independent directors, the requirement that we have a fully independent compensation committee, and the requirement that we have a fully independent nominating committee. If we rely on these exemptions, our stockholders will not have the same protections afforded to stockholders of companies that are subject to these requirements.  
          Anti-takeover provisions in our Restated Certificate and amended and restated bylaws (the                 “        Restated Bylaws        ”        ) could make it more difficult for a third party to acquire us, even if doing so would be beneficial to our stockholders.  
      Our Restated Certificate and Restated Bylaws contain provisions that may delay, defer, or prevent a change of control, including a classified board of directors, the inability of stockholders to act by written consent, limitations on the ability of stockholders to call special meetings, advance notice requirements for stockholder proposals and nominations, supermajority voting requirements for certain amendments, and a board vacancy-filling provision. These provisions could limit the price that investors might be willing to pay in the future for our Class A common stock.  
          Our Restated Certificate designates certain courts as the exclusive forum for certain disputes, which could limit the ability of our stockholders to bring claims in certain judicial forums.  
      Our Restated Certificate provides that, unless we consent in writing to the selection of an alternative forum, the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware will be the sole and exclusive forum for certain state-law claims, and the federal district courts of the United States will be the sole and exclusive forum for claims arising under the Securities Act. These forum-selection provisions may limit the ability of our stockholders to bring a claim in a judicial forum that they find favorable for disputes with us or our directors, officers, or other employees, which may discourage such lawsuits.  
          You will experience immediate and substantial dilution in the pro forma net tangible book value per share of the Class A common stock you purchase in this offering.  
      The assumed initial public offering price of $24.00 per share (the midpoint of the estimated range) is substantially higher than the pro forma net tangible book value per share of our Class A common stock. As a result, you will experience immediate and substantial dilution of $19.85 per share. See         “    Dilution.    ”  
          Future sales of our Class A common stock, or the perception that such sales may occur, could cause the price of our Class A common stock to decline.  
      Substantial numbers of shares of our Class A common stock will be eligible for sale following the expiration of the 180-day lock-up period after this offering, including shares issuable upon the exercise of outstanding stock options and the vesting and settlement of outstanding RSUs. Sales of a substantial number of shares, or the perception that such sales may occur, could cause the market price of our Class A common stock to decline.  
          Holders of our redeemable convertible preferred stock have registration rights, and the exercise of those rights could cause the price of our Class A common stock to decline.  
      Holders of our redeemable convertible preferred stock, which will convert into Class A common stock in this offering, are parties to an Investor Rights Agreement that provides them with demand registration rights commencing 180 days after this offering and S-3 piggyback registration rights. The exercise of these registration rights could result in a substantial number of additional shares being sold into the market, which could cause the price of our Class A common stock to decline.  
          We do not anticipate paying any cash dividends on our capital stock in the foreseeable future.  
      We have never declared or paid any cash dividends on our capital stock. We do not anticipate paying any cash dividends in the foreseeable future. In addition, our credit agreement prohibits the payment of dividends and stock repurchases without the lender’s consent.

         VI. Governance and Charter/Bylaw Issues  
       Issue 22 — Dual-class structure has no sunset provision [Medium]  
          Description.         The Restated Certificate (Article IV, Section 4.3) provides that Class B common stock converts to Class A only upon (i) transfer to a non-permitted transferee or (ii) the death or incapacity of the holder. There is no time-based or ownership-percentage sunset. The dual-class structure and the co-founders’ 71.8% voting control may therefore persist indefinitely.  
          Why it matters.         Perpetual dual-class structures are disfavored by certain institutional investors and may cause exclusion from certain major equity indices (e.g., S&P and FTSE Russell eligibility rules), which could reduce demand for and liquidity of the Class A common stock.  
          Recommendation.         The Draft S-1 includes risk-factor disclosure regarding the absence of a sunset, the concentration of voting control, and potential index exclusion. Consider whether to adopt a sunset (e.g., time-based or ownership-based) as a matter of market practice, though this is a business decision for the Board.  
        Issue 23 — Federal forum-selection clause for Securities Act claims [Low]  
          Description.         The Restated Certificate (Article X, Section 10.2) designates the federal district courts as the exclusive forum for claims arising under the Securities Act. Sciabacucchi         (Del. 2020), but it limits the ability of stockholders to bring Section 11 claims in state court (which would otherwise be concurrently available under             Cyan Inc. v. Beaver County Employees Retirement Fund         (2018)).  
          Recommendation.         The Draft S-1 includes risk-factor disclosure regarding the forum-selection provisions. No change required, but confirm enforceability analysis is documented.
