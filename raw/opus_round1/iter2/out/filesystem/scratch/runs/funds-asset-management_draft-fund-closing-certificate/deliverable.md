       Issue 1 — ERISA / Benefit Plan Investor percentage is internally inconsistent; under one reading the 24.99% cap is exceeded (Section 11.2(5); Section 6.8)  
          Why it matters.         Section 11.2(5) requires the General Partner to determine that, after giving effect to the admission of all incoming Limited Partners at the Final Closing, the aggregate Capital Commitments of Benefit Plan Investors (    “    BPIs    ”    ) do not exceed 24.99% of total Capital Commitments. Pursuant to Section 11.3, this condition             may not be waived under any circumstances    . If the true BPI percentage exceeds 24.99%, the Final Closing cannot proceed.  
          What the documents say.         Three different figures appear:  
             GP Memo (Section V.C):         States that BPI commitments are         “    approximately $225,300,000    ”         and that         “    total Fund commitments will be approximately $1,140,000,000,    ”         yielding         “    approximately 19.8%.    ”         This calculation is wrong on two inputs: (a) the $225,300,000 numerator reflects only the             prior-closings         BPI commitments and             omits the $130,000,000 of Final Closing BPIs         (Meridian State Teachers’ Retirement System ($100,000,000) and Sentry Municipal Employees’ Pension Trust ($30,000,000)); and (b) the $1,140,000,000 denominator is inconsistent with the $1,615,000,000 total Fund commitments stated in the GP Memo’s own reconciliation table (Section III) and in every other Source Document. We could not derive $1,140,000,000 from any combination of the stated commitment figures.  
             Subscription Agreement (Section 2.3 and Schedule I) and Admin Checklist (ERISA Analysis tab and Closing Checklist item 19):         State total BPI commitments of $355,300,000 (= $225,300,000 prior + $130,000,000 Final Closing) against total Fund commitments of $1,615,000,000, yielding             22.00%    .         Issue 7 — Default interest rate conflict: LPA 12% vs. Subscription Agreement 18% (Section 3.3(b))  
          What the documents say.         The LPA, Section 3.3(b), provides that a Defaulting Partner curing within the Cure Period must pay interest         “    at a rate of             twelve percent (12%) per annum         from the original Contribution Date through the date of payment.    ”         The Subscription Agreement, Section 1.2, by contrast, states that a defaulting subscriber may be subject to         “    default interest at a rate of the             lesser of 18% per annum         and the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.    ”         CC #1 (Section V) and CC #2 (Section V) both cite 12% (consistent with the LPA), although CC #2 adds         “    compounding quarterly,    ”         which the LPA does not specify (the LPA’s interest framework is simple, non-compounded).  
          Recommendation.         The Subscription Agreement’s 18% default rate is inconsistent with the LPA’s 12% and should be conformed to the LPA (the LPA controls under the Subscription Agreement’s own terms and the capital-call notices). The         “    compounding quarterly    ”         language in CC #2 should also be conformed to the LPA (simple interest). Confirm the controlling rate with the General Partner and correct the Subscription Agreement form for future use.

       Issue 10 — CC #1 funding-date discrepancy (October 24 vs. October 25, 2024)

        Issue 11 —         “    Investment Manager    ”         undefined term in Amendment No. 1  
      Amendment No. 1, Section 2.2(c), refers to         “    the General Partner,             the Investment Manager    , or their respective key persons.    ”         The LPA does not define a separate         “    Investment Manager    ”    ; Whitmore Capital Management LLC serves as both the General Partner and the investment adviser.         Issue 12 — Equalization day-count convention should be confirmed  
      As detailed in Issue 2, the LPA’s         “    from and including … through and including    ”         language supports a both-inclusive day count (114 days for CC #1; 48 days for CC #2), while the Subscription Agreement uses 113/47 and the Admin Checklist uses 113/46. The dollar impact of the convention ranges across the figures in the Issue 2 table (a spread of approximately $12,493 in the aggregate total due).  
        Issue 13 — Governmental plans treated as Benefit Plan Investors — definitional tension  
      The LPA’s defined term         “    Benefit Plan Investor    ”         (Article I) is limited to (a) employee benefit plans subject to Title I of ERISA, (b) plans subject to Section 4975 of the Code, and (c) entities whose assets include         “    plan assets    ”         by reason of investment by the foregoing. Governmental plans (Section 3(32) of ERISA) and church plans (Section 3(33)) are generally excluded from that definition. The Subscription Agreement (Box C), however, states that governmental plans and church plans are         “    treated as benefit plan investors for purposes of the LPA Section 6.8 threshold calculation,    ”         and the Admin Checklist marks several governmental plans as         “    Y.    ”         This conservative overlay is not expressly supported by the LPA’s definition and contributes to the inconsistency in Issue 1. The treatment should be documented and applied consistently.  
        Issue 14 — Advisory Committee fifth seat vacant (post-closing obligation)  
      Pursuant to Section 9.1(b) of the LPA, one Advisory Committee seat remains vacant and is to be filled from among the Limited Partners admitted at the Final Closing. The GP Memo (Section XI) recommends approaching Meridian State Teachers’ Retirement System. This is a post-closing obligation (the General Partner must use commercially reasonable efforts to fill the vacancy within 30 days of the Final Closing) and is not a condition precedent under Section 11.2, but it should be tracked as a post-closing action item.

      The Fund is, on the whole, positioned to proceed with the Final Closing, and most of the Section 11.2 conditions are documented as satisfied.
