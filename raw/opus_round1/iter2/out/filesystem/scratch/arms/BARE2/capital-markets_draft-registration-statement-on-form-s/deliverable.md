       Risks Related to Our Business and Industry  
          We have a history of net losses, we may not achieve or sustain profitability, and we may not generate sufficient cash flow to fund our operations.  
      We have incurred net losses since inception. We reported a net loss of $24.8 million and $27.4 million for the fiscal years ended December 31, 2024 and 2023, respectively. As of December 31, 2024, we had an accumulated deficit of $312.6 million. We expect our operating expenses to increase as we continue to invest in research and development, sales and marketing, and the expansion of our operations. We may not achieve or sustain profitability in the future.  
          We have a significant concentration of revenue from a limited number of customers.  
      No single customer accounted for more than 10% of total revenue during fiscal year 2024 or 2023. However, our largest customer, Harmon Industries Inc., represented approximately 8.2% of our total revenue during fiscal year 2024, and our top 10 customers collectively represented approximately 31.7% of our total revenue during fiscal year 2024. The loss of, or a material reduction in purchases by, one or more of these customers could materially and adversely affect our revenue and results of operations.  
          We are subject to intellectual property litigation that could materially and adversely affect our business.  
      On September 12, 2024, Optivance, Inc. filed a patent infringement lawsuit against us in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Texas (Case No. 6:24-cv-00831), alleging that certain features of our supply chain optimization platform infringe U.S. Patent No. 11,284,107, relating to         “    machine-learning-based inventory forecasting.    ”         We believe the claims are without merit and intend to vigorously defend against them. Based on the advice of outside patent counsel, we estimate the potential range of loss, in the event of an adverse judgment, to be between $5.0 million and $20.0 million, although an unfavorable outcome is considered to have a low probability. Litigation is inherently uncertain, and an adverse judgment could materially and adversely affect our business, financial condition, and results of operations. In addition, an adverse judgment in excess of $5.0 million that remains undischarged for 60 days could constitute an event of default under our term loan facility. See         “    Business—Legal Proceedings    ”         and         “    Management’s Discussion and Analysis—Indebtedness.    ”  
          Our growth depends in part on the success of our AI/ML technology, which is subject to rapid technological change.  
      The market for AI-driven supply chain software is characterized by rapid technological change, frequent new product introductions, and evolving industry standards. If we are unable to keep pace with technological developments or if our SupplyMind AI engine fails to deliver accurate and reliable results, our competitive position and results of operations could be harmed.  
          We depend on our co-founders and key personnel, and the loss of one or more of them could harm our business.

        Risks Related to Our Common Stock and This Offering  
          Because our co-founders will control a majority of the voting power of our capital stock, they will have control over most matters submitted to our stockholders for approval.  
      Upon the closing of this offering, our co-founders will hold an aggregate of 20,800,000 shares of Class B common stock, representing approximately 71.8% of the total voting power of our outstanding capital stock. As a result, our co-founders will have the ability to control the outcome of matters submitted to our stockholders for approval, including the election of directors, amendments to our certificate of incorporation, and the approval of significant corporate transactions. This concentration of voting control will limit the ability of other stockholders to influence corporate matters and may have an adverse effect on the market price of our Class A common stock.  
          We are a                 “        controlled company        ”                 within the meaning of the Nasdaq listing rules and may rely on exemptions from certain corporate governance requirements.  
      Because our co-founders will control a majority of the voting power of our outstanding capital stock, we expect to qualify as a         “    controlled company    ”         under Nasdaq Listing Rule 5615(c). As a controlled company, we may elect to rely on exemptions from certain Nasdaq corporate governance requirements, including the requirement that a majority of our board of directors consist of independent directors, the requirement for a fully independent compensation committee, and the requirement for a fully independent nominating and corporate governance committee. If we rely on these exemptions, you will not have the same protections afforded to stockholders of companies that are subject to these requirements.  
          Our dual-class common stock structure has no sunset provision and may adversely affect the trading price of our Class A common stock.  
      Our Class B common stock will not automatically convert into Class A common stock based on the passage of time or upon the holders’ ownership falling below a specified threshold. Class B common stock will convert into Class A common stock only upon transfer to a non-permitted transferee or upon the death or incapacity of the holder. The absence of a sunset provision means that the concentration of voting control with our co-founders may persist indefinitely. In addition, certain index providers may exclude companies with multi-class share structures from their indices, which could limit the demand for, and adversely affect the trading price of, our Class A common stock.  
          You will experience immediate and substantial dilution in the net tangible book value per share of the Class A common stock you purchase.  
      The assumed initial public offering price of $24.00 per share (the midpoint of the estimated price range) is substantially higher than the pro forma net tangible book value per share of our Class A common stock immediately after this offering. As a result, you will experience immediate and substantial dilution of $19.85 per share. See         “    Dilution.    ”  
          Our amended and restated certificate of incorporation and bylaws contain provisions that may discourage acquisition proposals and limit the ability of stockholders to influence corporate matters.  
      Our amended and restated certificate of incorporation and bylaws will contain provisions, including a classified board, the elimination of the ability of stockholders to act by written consent, advance notice requirements for stockholder proposals and director nominations, and supermajority voting requirements for certain amendments, that could make it more difficult for a third party to acquire us or for stockholders to effect changes in management. These provisions could limit the price that investors might be willing to pay for our Class A common stock.  
          Our exclusive forum provisions may limit the ability of stockholders to bring certain claims.  
      Our amended and restated certificate of incorporation will provide that, unless we consent in writing to the selection of an alternative forum, the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware will be the sole and exclusive forum for certain state-law claims, and the federal district courts of the United States will be the sole and exclusive forum for claims arising under the Securities Act. These provisions may limit the ability of stockholders to bring claims in the judicial forums they prefer.

       Overview  
      We are an enterprise SaaS company providing an AI-driven supply chain optimization platform to Fortune 1000 manufacturers and retailers. We generate revenue primarily from subscription fees for access to our cloud-based platform, which we recognize ratably over the contractual term, and from professional services, which we recognize as services are delivered.  
      For the fiscal year ended December 31, 2024, total revenue was $187.3 million, representing an increase of 45.3% from $128.9 million for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2023. Subscription revenue accounted for 90.0% of total revenue in fiscal year 2024. As of December 31, 2024, ARR was $211.6 million, and our dollar-based net retention rate was 132%.  
      We reported a net loss of $24.8 million for fiscal year 2024, compared to a net loss of $27.4 million for fiscal year 2023. As of December 31, 2024, we had an accumulated deficit of $312.6 million.

            BUSINESS  
       Overview  
      Crestline Software, Inc. is an enterprise SaaS company providing an AI-driven supply chain optimization platform to Fortune 1000 manufacturers and retailers. Our platform, powered by the proprietary SupplyMind AI engine, combines machine learning, advanced statistical modeling, and real-time data integration to deliver predictive and prescriptive supply chain intelligence. We serve customers across manufacturing, retail, logistics, and consumer packaged goods, among other verticals.  
        Industry Background  
      Global supply chains are increasingly complex, fragmented, and vulnerable to disruption. Legacy planning tools rely on static models and manual inputs that fail in volatile environments. We believe the market for AI-driven supply chain software is undergoing a generational shift from deterministic to AI-driven probabilistic planning. We estimate that the total addressable market for AI-driven supply chain software will be approximately $28 billion by 2028.  
        Our Solution  
      Our Crestline Nexus platform is a unified SaaS platform for end-to-end supply chain optimization. The SupplyMind AI engine provides proprietary machine learning models for demand forecasting, inventory optimization, and logistics routing. Our platform features real-time data ingestion from enterprise resource planning, warehouse management, transportation management, and IoT sensor systems, with pre-built connectors for over 100 enterprise data sources. Our multi-tenant, cloud-native architecture enables global scalability.  
        Revenue Model  
      We generate revenue from two sources: (i) subscription revenue, consisting of fees for access to our cloud-based platform, which is recognized ratably over the contractual term (typically one to three years); and (ii) professional services revenue, consisting of implementation, configuration, training, and optimization consulting, which is recognized as services are delivered. Subscription revenue accounted for 90.0% of total revenue in fiscal year 2024.  
        Customers  
      As of December 31, 2024, we served 487 customers, including 34 customers contributing ARR in excess of $1.0 million. Our customers span manufacturing (38% of revenue), retail (24%), logistics (19%), and consumer packaged goods (12%), with the remaining 7% distributed across healthcare, financial services, and other industries. No single customer accounted for more than 10% of total revenue during fiscal year 2024 or 2023. Our largest customer represented approximately 8.2% of total revenue during fiscal year 2024, and our top 10 customers collectively represented approximately 31.7% of total revenue.  
        Intellectual Property  
      Our intellectual property portfolio includes 47 issued U.S. patents, 12 pending U.S. patent applications, and 8 issued foreign patents covering core algorithmic innovations and data processing methods. We have registered the trademarks         “    Crestline,    ”         “    Crestline Nexus,    ”         and         “    SupplyMind    ”         with the United States Patent and Trademark Office. We also rely on trade secrets and proprietary training data to protect our technology.  
        Competition  
      The market for supply chain software is competitive and fragmented. We compete with legacy enterprise resource planning vendors, point-solution providers, and emerging AI-native platforms. We believe our principal competitive advantages include our proprietary SupplyMind AI engine, our patent portfolio, our high net retention rate, and our Fortune 1000 customer base.  
        Employees  
      As of December 31, 2024, we had 1,247 employees. During the third quarter of 2024, we implemented a workforce reduction affecting approximately 46 employees and recorded restructuring charges of $1.2 million.  
        Properties  
      We lease office facilities in Austin, Texas (our headquarters), San Francisco, California, London, United Kingdom, and Bangalore, India. Our Austin headquarters lease expires in August 2029 and has annual base rent of approximately $3.8 million. Our San Francisco lease expires in March 2027 and has annual base rent of approximately $2.1 million. Our London lease expires in January 2028 and has annual base rent of approximately £680,000. Our Bangalore lease expires in June 2026 and has annual base rent of approximately INR 42,000,000. We believe our facilities are adequate for our current needs.  
        Legal Proceedings  
      On September 12, 2024, Optivance, Inc. filed a patent infringement lawsuit against us in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Texas (Case No. 6:24-cv-00831), alleging that certain features of our supply chain optimization platform infringe U.S. Patent No. 11,284,107, relating to         “    machine-learning-based inventory forecasting.    ”         We believe the claims are without merit and intend to vigorously defend against them. The lawsuit is in the early stages of litigation, and no trial date has been set. We have not recorded a loss contingency for this matter because management believes it is not probable that a loss has been incurred. Based on the advice of outside patent counsel, we estimate the potential range of loss, in the event of an adverse judgment, to be between $5.0 million and $20.0 million, although an unfavorable outcome is considered to have a low probability.        Issue 27 — Director Ages Not Provided [Low]  
          Description. Item 401 of Regulation S-K requires disclosure of the age of each director and executive officer.

          Recommendation.         Follow up with each director to confirm age, and complete the Management section (the draft Form S-1 includes placeholders for the missing ages).  
        Issue 28 — Lock-Up Coverage for Fund-Level Entities [Medium]  
          Description.         The director and officer questionnaire responses (Open Item 5) note that the Crestline shares held by Graymont Ventures and Ridgeline Growth Partners are held at the fund level (e.g., Ridgeline Growth Partners Fund V, L.P.), not in the individual names of Ms. Venkatesh and Mr. Kwon. The lock-up agreements must bind the fund-level entities that hold the shares, not merely the individual directors who serve as representatives on the board.  
          Risk.         If the lock-up agreements are executed only by the individual directors and not by the fund entities that hold the shares, the lock-up restrictions may not be enforceable against the shares held by the funds, undermining the lock-up arrangement that the underwriters expect.  
          Recommendation.         (i) Ensure that the lock-up agreements are executed by the fund-level entities (Graymont Ventures Fund III, L.P.; Graymont Ventures Fund IV, L.P.; Ridgeline Growth Partners Fund V, L.P.) and their general partners, in addition to the individual directors; (ii) cross-reference the lock-up letter forms with the underwriting agreement to ensure proper coverage; and (iii) confirm that all 5%+ stockholders and their holding entities execute lock-up agreements.  
        Issue 29 — Auditor Report Date and Subsequent-Events Date Not Finalized [Low]  
          Description.         The auditor’s report and the subsequent-events note (Note 17) use placeholder dates of “March __, 2025.” The auditor has served as the Company’s auditor since 2022, and the engagement partner is Robert C. Fenn.  
          Risk.         The auditor’s report date and the subsequent-events evaluation date must be finalized before the Registration Statement is filed. This is a routine completion item.  
          Recommendation.         Coordinate with Archer Pryce LLP to finalize the report date and the subsequent-events evaluation date, and update the Registration Statement accordingly.  
        Issue 30 — IPO Midpoint Price Equals Series D Price [Low]  
          Description.         The estimated IPO price range is $22.00 to $26.00 per share, with a midpoint of $24.00. The Series D redeemable convertible preferred stock was issued in June 2023 at $24.00 per share, implying a post-money equity valuation of approximately $1.92 billion. The IPO midpoint thus equals the Series D price, notwithstanding 45.3% revenue growth and significant operating progress between the Series D and the Offering.  
          Risk.         While not a disclosure inconsistency, pricing the Offering at the same per-share price as the Series D may raise questions from investors and the SEC regarding valuation progression. If the Offering is priced below $24.00, it could be perceived as a down-round relative to the Series D, which may negatively affect market perception. This is primarily a commercial consideration, but counsel should be aware of it.  
          Recommendation.         (i) Confirm the final price range with the underwriters; (ii) ensure the dilution and use-of-proceeds disclosures are updated for the final price; and (iii) be prepared to address valuation questions during the marketing process.
