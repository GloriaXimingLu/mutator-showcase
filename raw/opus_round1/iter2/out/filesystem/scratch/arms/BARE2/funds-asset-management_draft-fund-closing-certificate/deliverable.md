        III. Critical Issues  
       Issue 1 — AML/KYC Completion Email Does Not Cover Cascade Re Insurance Ltd.  
          Condition affected:         Section 11.2(3) (AML/KYC Compliance) —             non-waivable         under Section 11.3.  
          Finding:         The AML Email from Jonathan C. Prewitt of Oakmere Fund Services LLC, dated January 24, 2025 — which is the supporting document for satisfaction of the AML/KYC condition — expressly lists AML/KYC verification results for only             five (5) of the six (6)         Final Closing Limited Partners:  
          Meridian State Teachers’ Retirement System;  
          Haverfield Family Office LLC;  
          Northbridge Endowment Fund;  
          Sentry Municipal Employees’ Pension Trust; and  
          Kingsgate Ventures Fund-of-Funds III, LP.  
          Cascade Re Insurance Ltd.         (a Bermuda-domiciled Class 3B reinsurer with a $35,000,000 Capital Commitment) is             not listed         in the AML Email. The email states that         “    the results for the final closing investors are as follows    ”         and then enumerates only five investors, omitting Cascade Re.  
          Significance:         Section 11.2(3) requires satisfactory AML/KYC documentation for             each incoming Limited Partner    , and Section 11.3 expressly provides that the AML/KYC condition         “    may not be waived under any circumstances.    ”         Cascade Re, as a non-U.S. entity organized in Bermuda, would ordinarily warrant enhanced due diligence (including, e.g., verification of source of funds, sanctions screening against non-U.S. lists, and beneficial ownership analysis). The Final Closing Certificate cannot certify Condition 11.2(3) as satisfied for all six incoming Limited Partners on the basis of the AML Email alone.  
          Recommendation:         Obtain a supplemental written confirmation from the Fund Administrator that AML/KYC verification (including enhanced due diligence appropriate to a Bermuda-domiciled reinsurer) has been completed for Cascade Re Insurance Ltd. with no adverse findings, and that all supporting documentation is on file, before the Final Closing Certificate is executed in final form. The certificate has been drafted to qualify the Condition 3 certification accordingly.

The treatment of governmental plans should be confirmed as a matter of interpretation; doing so conservatively (i.e., counting governmental plans) is the more protective approach and is the basis for the figures above.

Specifically, confirm whether Commonwealth Public Employees’ Retirement Fund and Ashford Healthcare System Retirement Plan are properly classified as Benefit Plan Investors, and correct either the summary or the detailed schedule so that the two agree. If, after reconciliation, the Benefit Plan Investor percentage exceeds 24.99%, the General Partner must reduce or decline one or more Benefit Plan Investor commitments under Section 6.8 before admitting such investors. The Final Closing Certificate has been drafted to qualify the Condition 5 certification to disclose this reconciliation item.  
      
        Issue 3 — Equalization Interest Day-Count Discrepancy (Capital Call No. 2)  
          Condition affected:         Section 11.2(2) (Capital Contributions, including Equalization Interest under Section 3.2(c)).  
          Finding:         The equalization interest calculations for the Final Closing Limited Partners appear in two places and             do not reconcile    :  
          The             Checklist         (Equalization Calc tab) calculates equalization interest for Capital Call No. 2 using a day count of             46 days         (December 15, 2024 through January 31, 2025), yielding:  
          Aggregate equalization interest:             $849,534.24  
          Total amount due from Final Closing LPs:             $43,599,534.24  
          The             Subscription Agreements         (Section 1.3 and Schedule I) calculate equalization interest for Capital Call No. 2 using a day count of             47 days    , yielding:  
          Aggregate equalization interest (per-LP sum):             $852,657.53  
          Aggregate equalization interest (aggregate LPA methodology):             $852,661.64  
          Total amount due from Final Closing LPs:             $43,602,661.64  
      The difference of approximately             $3,127.40         is attributable to the one-day difference in the Capital Call No. 2 day count. Notably,             both         documents use             113 days         for Capital Call No. 1 (October 10, 2024 through January 31, 2025), which is the correct date difference for that period. The date difference from December 15, 2024 to January 31, 2025 is likewise             47 days    , so the Checklist’s 46-day count for Capital Call No. 2 appears to be an off-by-one error that is internally inconsistent with its own Capital Call No. 1 calculation.  
      The Subscription Agreements provide that the aggregate figure of             $43,602,661.64         “    shall be the controlling amount for purposes of the Final Closing.    ”  
          Significance:         The discrepancy affects the precise amount of capital contributions due at the Final Closing (Condition 11.2(2)) and the equalization interest to be distributed to existing Limited Partners under Section 3.2(c). While the dollar difference is modest, the closing statement, capital account records, and the Final Closing Certificate should all reflect a single, internally consistent figure.  
          Recommendation:         Conform the Checklist’s Equalization Calc tab to use 47 days for Capital Call No. 2 (consistent with the Subscription Agreements and with the 113-day count used for Capital Call No. 1), and use the controlling aggregate amount of $43,602,661.64 in the closing statement and certificate. Confirm that the per-investor equalization interest amounts to be distributed to existing Limited Partners are calculated on the same basis.

        Issue 8 — Target Fund Size Advisory Committee Notification Omitted from Action Items  
          Condition affected:         Section 3.1(d) — post-closing notification obligation.  
          Finding:         Aggregate Capital Commitments ($1,615,000,000) exceed the Target Fund Size ($1,500,000,000) for the first time at the Final Closing. Section 3.1(d) requires the General Partner to notify the Advisory Committee in writing of such excess, including the amount by which the Target Fund Size has been exceeded and the identities and Capital Commitments of the Partners admitted at the applicable Closing,             not later than five (5) Business Days following the Closing         at which the Target Fund Size is first exceeded.  
      Neither the GP Memo’s outstanding action items (Section XII) nor the Checklist lists this Advisory Committee notification. The Subscription Agreements (preamble and Section 5.1) acknowledge the notification requirement but characterize it as         “    a condition to the acceptance of commitments above the Fund Target,    ”         which is arguably inconsistent with the LPA — Section 3.1(d) requires only notification (not Advisory Committee consent) and does not make the notification a condition to closing; exceeding the Target does not require Advisory Committee consent provided the Hard Cap is not exceeded.  
          Recommendation:         Calendar the Advisory Committee notification (due within five Business Days following the Final Closing, i.e., by approximately February 7, 2025) and add it to the post-closing action item list. Reconcile the Subscription Agreements’ characterization of the notification as a         “    condition    ”         with the LPA’s notification-only framework.

         V. Lower-Priority and Clerical Issues  
       Issue 11 — GP Memo ERISA Figure (19.8%) Is Arithmetically Erroneous  
      The GP Memo (Section V.C) calculates the Benefit Plan Investor percentage as         “    approximately 19.8% ($225,300,000 / $1,140,000,000).    ”         This calculation is erroneous on two grounds: (i) the denominator $1,140,000,000 is not the total Fund commitments (which are $1,615,000,000); and (ii) the numerator omits the Final Closing Benefit Plan Investors (Meridian $100,000,000 and Sentry $30,000,000). The corrected figure, per the Checklist and Subscription Agreements, is 22.00% ($355,300,000 / $1,615,000,000). The GP Memo should be corrected. (Separately, the $225,300,000 prior-closings figure itself does not tie to the detailed Commitment Schedule — see Issue 2.)  
        Issue 12 — Amendment No. 1 Misidentifies the Waterfall Section  
      Amendment No. 1, Section 1.2, states that it corrects a technical error in         “    Section 8.3(b)    ”         relating to the General Partner’s         “    catch-up    ”         allocation, and labels the catch-up the         “    second    ”         tier of the waterfall. In the LPA, however, Section 8.3(b) is the             Preferred Return         (the second tier, paid to the Limited Partners), and the             GP Catch-Up         is Section 8.3(c) (the third tier). The amendment’s section reference and tier numbering do not align with the LPA’s waterfall structure. The substantive correction (conforming the catch-up to a true 100% catch-up to 20% of the first two tiers) appears sound, but the cross-references in Amendment No. 1 should be clarified to avoid ambiguity regarding which section was amended.

          (c) Equalization Interest Reconciliation (Condition 2).         The equalization interest calculations prepared by the Fund Administrator reflect a one-day difference in the day count for Capital Call Notice No. 2 (December 15, 2024 through January 31, 2025) between the administrative closing checklist (which uses 46 days, yielding aggregate equalization interest of $849,534.24 and a total amount due of $43,599,534.24) and the Subscription Agreements (which use 47 days, yielding aggregate equalization interest of $852,661.64 and a total amount due of $43,602,661.64). The closing statement and this Certificate reflect the controlling aggregate amount, and the administrative checklist should be conformed.

The Benefit Plan Investor percentage of 22.00% ($355,300,000 / $1,615,000,000) is based on $225,300,000 of Benefit Plan Investor commitments from prior closings plus $130,000,000 from the Final Closing. If the detailed schedule’s classifications are accurate, the Benefit Plan Investor percentage would be approximately 28.81%, which would exceed the 24.99% cap.
