        Finding B — Unauthorized Timekeepers (Billing Guidelines §4.2; Staffing Plan §§2.3, 2.4, 4) — $33,400.00

        Finding C — Contract Attorney Rate Overcharge (§5.1; Staffing Plan §§2.5, 6) — $3,862.50  
      Marcus Tate (contract attorney, sourced through Redwood Document Solutions) was billed at             $210/hr    . Sections 4.4 and 8.4 require contract-attorney and vendor costs to be passed through at actual cost with no markup. The lower of the two governing figures — the             $185/hr         approved pass-through rate — controls.  
          Line-item hours: 154.5. Overcharge per hour: $210 − $185 = $25.  
              Reduction: 154.5 × $25 = $3,862.50         (Tate’s fees reduced from $32,445.00 to $28,582.50).

The $185 approved rate is recommended because it is the rate Pinnacle actually approved and because §4.4/§8.4 prohibit any markup. The Firm should also be required to substantiate that $185 remains the vendor’s actual cost.

        Finding E — Block Billing (§5.4) — $26,693.75 (43 entries)  
      Section 5.4 prohibits combining two or more discrete tasks in a single line item and mandates an             automatic 25% reduction         to each block-billed entry. Block billing is pervasive in this invoice: 43 of the entries by authorized timekeepers combine distinct, independently timeable activities — most commonly         “    draft X; research Y,    ”         “    review/revise X; conference with Z,    ”         and entries stacking three or more tasks. (Examples: Entry 2 —         “    Draft responses to Interrogatories Nos. 12–18; research Georgia Trade Secrets Act preemption…    ”    ; Entry 20 —         “    Review and revise draft motion to compel; analyze MedCore privilege log…; confer with D. Hargrove…    ”    ; Entry 92 —         “    Prepare for and conduct strategy session…; draft outline for expert discovery plan. Review and edit interrogatory responses…    ”    .)  
      The 25% reduction is applied to each block-billed entry’s fee (after any rate adjustment). Entries already fully rejected under Findings B, D, F, or G are excluded to avoid double-counting. The full schedule of 43 entries and the per-entry 25% amounts is set forth in             Appendix A    ; the aggregate reduction is             $26,693.75    .  
      Under §5.4, the Firm may avoid this reduction by resubmitting each block-billed entry as separate, individually-timed line items within 15 days of notice. Pinnacle should so advise the Firm.  
        Finding F — Excess Conference Attendance (§4.3) — $937.50  
      Section 4.3 caps billing timekeepers for any single internal meeting or conference call at             three (3)    , even for Substantive Case-Strategy Sessions, and requires advance designation when more than three are expected. On             May 8, 2025    , five timekeepers billed the same team strategy call (Entries 22–26: Hargrove, Cho, Pettersson, Barros, and Webb). No advance designation was made.  
      After removing Ms. Barros (fully rejected under Finding B), four authorized timekeepers remain — one over the cap. The three timekeepers most central to a discovery-strategy session (Hargrove, Cho, and Pettersson) are retained; the fourth, junior associate Tyler Webb (Entry 26, 2.5h), is the excess.             Reduction: 2.5 × $375 = $937.50         (Entry 26 written off in full).  
        Finding G — Excess Deposition Staffing (§4.3) — $2,375.00  
      Section 4.3 provides that depositions are             not         Court Appearances and that more than             two (2)         billing timekeepers at a deposition requires advance approval requested no later than five business days before the deposition; absent approval,         “    Pinnacle reserves the right to reduce the time of the additional timekeepers in their entirety.    ”  
      On             May 19, 2025    , three attorneys billed the Dr. Rebecca Liu deposition (Entries 60, 61, 62: Hargrove, Cho, and Pettersson), and the Case Status Report confirms all three were         “    present for the full duration.    ”         No advance approval for a third attorney was sought. The two justified billing attorneys are Karen Cho (who conducted the direct examination) and David Hargrove (who provided real-time strategic guidance and credibility assessment on the case’s most critical witness). James Pettersson’s role — managing exhibit presentation, taking notes, and preparing a testimony summary — is supportive/clerical and constitutes the unapproved third timekeeper.             Reduction: Entry 62 written off in full — 5.0 × $475 = $2,375.00.  
      (Note: the Case Status Report states all three attorneys were present for the deposition’s full ~6.5-hour duration, yet Mr. Hargrove billed only 4.0 hours and Mr. Pettersson 5.0 hours for that day. This narrative/hours inconsistency should be resolved when the Firm corrects the invoice, but it does not increase the payable.)  
        Finding H — Budget Overage Without Pre-Approval; Missing Reconciliation (§§7.2, 7.3, 9.1(g))  
      The Approved Staffing Plan establishes a monthly attorney-fee range of             $95,000–$165,000    . Under §7.3, fees exceeding             115% of the high end — $189,750         — require prior written pre-approval, and Pinnacle may reduce any unapproved excess to that ceiling. Attorney fees here exceed $189,750             under either measure         of the invoice ($193,372.50 as stated; $215,680.00 per the line items), and             no pre-approval was sought.         The April 28/May 1 correspondence shows Ms. Dominguez specifically wanted to         “    check where things stand against the current budget    ”         before approving any staffing increase — yet the Firm proceeded without that review.  
      Because the entry-level reductions in Findings B–G bring the compliant fee to             $143,121.25    , which is             below         the $189,750 ceiling, the budget cap does not further reduce the payable. The overage nonetheless remains a             standalone compliance violation    : the Firm exceeded the pre-approval threshold without notice and must obtain pre-approval before any future month that is projected to exceed $189,750.  
      In addition, the invoice             omits the budget-to-actual reconciliation         required by §7.2 and §9.1(g) (approved range, actual fees/expenses, cumulative figures, percentage of phase budget consumed, and narrative explanation of any variance >10% from the midpoint). This must be supplied before payment.

        Finding J — Other Observations (no separate dollar reduction)
