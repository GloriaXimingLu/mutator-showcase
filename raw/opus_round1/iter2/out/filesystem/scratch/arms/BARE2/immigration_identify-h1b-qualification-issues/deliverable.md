        IV. Critical Issues  
       C-1. Field-of-study qualification gap — Beneficiary’s degree is in Electronics & Communication Engineering, not the required specialty  
      The proffered position requires a bachelor’s degree in             Computer Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, or a closely related field         (Support Letter §3; LCA §G.7). The Beneficiary holds a             B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE)    .  
          The credential evaluation (Westbridge, §5) equates the degree to a             U.S. B.S. in Electronics and Communication Engineering         — i.e., it confirms the             level         of the degree but             not         its             relatedness         to the proffered position. Section 7 of the evaluation expressly states:             “        This evaluation does not opine on whether the applicant’s field of study satisfies the requirements of any particular position or petition.        ”  
          Per the evaluation (§4) and the transcript, computer-science/IT coursework totals only             ~12 credit hours out of ~160 (≈7.5%)    : C Programming (CS101), Advanced C Programming (CS201), Data Structures (CS401), and Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (EC704, an ECE elective). The remaining ~92.5% of the curriculum is core ECE (signals, communications, VLSI, electromagnetics, microwave, radar, etc.).  
          The Support Letter (§4) argues the curriculum         “    included coursework in programming, data structures, and artificial intelligence, providing a strong technical foundation,    ”         but this overstates the relevance of a 7.5% CS component and is not supported by any expert opinion on relatedness.  
          Risk.         Whether ECE is         “    closely related    ”         to CS/ML/AI is a case-by-case adjudicator judgment. Without an expert opinion addressing relatedness — and with the evaluation expressly disclaiming it — the primary qualification theory is exposed to an RFE or denial on the         “    qualifies to perform services in the specialty occupation    ”         element (INA § 214(i)(2); 8 CFR 214.2(h)(4)(iii)).  
          Recommendation.         Obtain a supplemental expert opinion / detailed credential evaluation that expressly opines that the Beneficiary’s ECE coursework (including signal processing, linear algebra, probability, and the AI/programming electives) constitutes the theoretical foundation for machine learning engineering and that ECE is a         “    closely related    ”         field. Alternatively, lean on the alternative-qualification theory (see C-2) and document it fully.  
        C-2. Undocumented three-year         “    Independent Machine Learning Consultant    ”         period — the linchpin of the alternative qualification theory  
      The Support Letter (§3) offers an             alternative qualification    : a bachelor’s degree in             any         engineering discipline             plus 5 years of progressive experience in machine learning    . The Beneficiary’s ECE degree satisfies the         “    any engineering discipline    ”         prong, so eligibility under this alternative turns on             5 years of progressive ML experience    .  
          The package contains             no documentation         for the             August 2020 – August 2023         independent-consulting period (no client letters, 1099s, contracts, invoices, tax returns, or statement of self-employment). This is a self-attested, three-year block.  
          Counting only             documented         ML experience: NovaStar (Apr. 2017 – Jul. 2020 ≈ 3.3 years) + Brightfield (Sept. 2023 – present ≈ 1.6 years) ≈             4.9 years         — just short of the 5-year threshold.  
          With the consultant period credited (3 years), total ML experience ≈ 7.9 years, satisfying the alternative.             The alternative qualification theory therefore depends entirely on crediting an undocumented, self-attested period.  
          Risk.         USCIS routinely declines to credit undocumented or self-attested experience. If the consultant period is not credited, the Beneficiary fails the alternative theory; and because the primary theory is weak (C-1), the petition’s qualification theory could collapse on both fronts.  
          Recommendation.         Corroborate the consultant period with at least two or three client letters (on client letterhead, describing ML/NLP duties and dates), 1099/1040 tax evidence, and/or contracts/SOWs. If corroboration is not available, restructure the qualification theory to rely on the primary path with a strong relatedness opinion (C-1) and the documented NovaStar + Brightfield experience.  
        C-3. LCA posting period is shorter than the 10 business days required by regulation  
      20 CFR § 655.734(b)(1)(ii) requires the LCA notice to be posted for             10 consecutive business days    . The certified LCA (§J.2) and the Posting Notice both state posting from             March 10, 2025 through March 19, 2025    .  
      Counting business days (excluding the March 15–16 weekend): March 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 17, 18, 19 =             8 business days    . To satisfy the 10-business-day rule, posting would have had to run through at least             March 21, 2025    .  
          Risk.         A deficient posting is a violation of the employer’s notice obligation independent of DOL certification of the LCA. It is enforceable by DOL’s Wage and Hour Division (back wages, civil money penalties) and may be raised by a complainant. While USCIS does not typically police posting in adjudication, the deficiency creates a compliance exposure and a weak Public Access File record.  
          Recommendation.         Re-post the notice for a full 10 consecutive business days, document both posting locations with dated photographs/affidavits, and update the Public Access File. Confirm that the re-posting and PAF documentation are completed before the Beneficiary enters H-1B status on October 1, 2025.  
      
         V. Significant Issues  
       S-1. Wage-level / job-duty inconsistency (Level 2 vs. supervisory, independent-judgment duties)  
      The PWD and LCA set the prevailing wage at             Level 2         ($113,734). The DOL four-tier framework describes Level 2 as workers who         “    perform moderately complex tasks requiring             limited judgment    ”         and does             not         include supervisory responsibility. Level 3 covers workers who         “    may exercise             independent judgment         and             may have supervisory responsibilities    ”    ; Level 4 covers workers who         “    supervise and train other workers    ”         and         “    exercise broad latitude.    ”  
      The position as described in the Support Letter and LCA includes:  
          “    Leading and mentoring a team of 3 junior data scientists    ”         (Support Letter duty 4; 15% of time);  
          “    lead and mentor a team of data scientists    ”         (LCA §G.5; PWD job-duty summary);  
          “    exercises             independent judgment         in the design and implementation of machine learning solutions    ”         (Support Letter §2);  
          a         “    Senior    ”         title requiring 3+ years of experience, held by a Beneficiary with 8+ years.  
      These duties map to             Level 3 (or arguably Level 4)    , not Level 2. The offered wage of             $128,000         exceeds Level 2 ($113,734) but is             below Level 3 ($137,946)         and Level 4 ($162,157).  
          Safe-harbor nuance.         Because the Petitioner used an NPWC-issued PWD and disclosed the         “    lead a team    ”         duty in the PWD request, the prevailing-wage safe harbor of 20 CFR § 655.731(a)(1) likely protects prevailing-wage compliance for the LCA as certified.             However    , the petition’s narrative is internally inconsistent: the same supervisory/independent-judgment language used to bolster the specialty-occupation argument undercuts the Level 2 wage. If the position were re-evaluated at Level 3 (e.g., on DOL audit or a redetermination), the offered wage would be             insufficient    , and the LCA wage attestation (INA § 212(n)(1)(A)) would be violated.  
          Recommendation.         Reconcile the wage level with the job duties. Either (a) increase the offered wage to at least             $137,946         (Level 3) to eliminate any exposure if the level is revisited; or (b) if maintaining Level 2, ensure the Support Letter does not overstate supervisory/independent-judgment authority in a way that contradicts Level 2. The current draft does both at once and should not be filed as-is.

## IV. Critical Issues

### C-1. Field-of-study qualification gap — Beneficiary's degree is in Electronics & Communication Engineering, not the required specialty

The proffered position requires a bachelor's degree in **Computer Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, or a closely related field** (Support Letter §3; LCA §G.7). The Beneficiary holds a **B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE)**.

- The credential evaluation (Westbridge, §5) equates the degree to a **U.S. B.S. in Electronics and Communication Engineering** — i.e., it confirms the *level* of the degree but **not** its *relatedness* to the proffered position. Section 7 of the evaluation expressly states: *"This evaluation does not opine on whether the applicant's field of study satisfies the requirements of any particular position or petition."*
- Per the evaluation (§4) and the transcript, computer-science/IT coursework totals only **~12 credit hours out of ~160 (≈7.5%)**: C Programming (CS101), Advanced C Programming (CS201), Data Structures (CS401), and Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (EC704, an ECE elective). The remaining ~92.5% of the curriculum is core ECE (signals, communications, VLSI, electromagnetics, microwave, radar, etc.).
- The Support Letter (§4) argues the curriculum "included coursework in programming, data structures, and artificial intelligence, providing a strong technical foundation," but this overstates the relevance of a 7.5% CS component and is not supported by any expert opinion on relatedness.

**Risk.** Whether ECE is "closely related" to CS/ML/AI is a case-by-case adjudicator judgment. Without an expert opinion addressing relatedness — and with the evaluation expressly disclaiming it — the primary qualification theory is exposed to an RFE or denial on the "qualifies to perform services in the specialty occupation" element (INA § 214(i)(2); 8 CFR 214.2(h)(4)(iii)).

**Recommendation.** Obtain a supplemental expert opinion / detailed credential evaluation that expressly opines that the Beneficiary's ECE coursework (including signal processing, linear algebra, probability, and the AI/programming electives) constitutes the theoretical foundation for machine learning engineering and that ECE is a "closely related" field. Alternatively, lean on the alternative-qualification theory (see C-2) and document it fully.

### C-2. Undocumented three-year "Independent Machine Learning Consultant" period — the linchpin of the alternative qualification theory

The Support Letter (§3) offers an **alternative qualification**: a bachelor's degree in *any* engineering discipline **plus 5 years of progressive experience in machine learning**. The Beneficiary's ECE degree satisfies the "any engineering discipline" prong, so eligibility under this alternative turns on **5 years of progressive ML experience**.

- The package contains **no documentation** for the **August 2020 – August 2023** independent-consulting period (no client letters, 1099s, contracts, invoices, tax returns, or statement of self-employment). This is a self-attested, three-year block.
- Counting only *documented* ML experience: NovaStar (Apr. 2017 – Jul. 2020 ≈ 3.3 years) + Brightfield (Sept. 2023 – present ≈ 1.6 years) ≈ **4.9 years** — just short of the 5-year threshold.
- With the consultant period credited (3 years), total ML experience ≈ 7.9 years, satisfying the alternative. **The alternative qualification theory therefore depends entirely on crediting an undocumented, self-attested period.**

**Risk.** USCIS routinely declines to credit undocumented or self-attested experience. If the consultant period is not credited, the Beneficiary fails the alternative theory; and because the primary theory is weak (C-1), the petition's qualification theory could collapse on both fronts.

**Recommendation.** Corroborate the consultant period with at least two or three client letters (on client letterhead, describing ML/NLP duties and dates), 1099/1040 tax evidence, and/or contracts/SOWs. If corroboration is not available, restructure the qualification theory to rely on the primary path with a strong relatedness opinion (C-1) and the documented NovaStar + Brightfield experience.

### C-3. LCA posting period is shorter than the 10 business days required by regulation

20 CFR § 655.734(b)(1)(ii) requires the LCA notice to be posted for **10 consecutive business days**. The certified LCA (§J.2) and the Posting Notice both state posting from **March 10, 2025 through March 19, 2025**.

Counting business days (excluding the March 15–16 weekend): March 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 17, 18, 19 = **8 business days**. To satisfy the 10-business-day rule, posting would have had to run through at least **March 21, 2025**.

**Risk.** A deficient posting is a violation of the employer's notice obligation independent of DOL certification of the LCA. It is enforceable by DOL's Wage and Hour Division (back wages, civil money penalties) and may be raised by a complainant. While USCIS does not typically police posting in adjudication, the deficiency creates a compliance exposure and a weak Public Access File record.

**Recommendation.** Re-post the notice for a full 10 consecutive business days, document both posting locations with dated photographs/affidavits, and update the Public Access File. Confirm that the re-posting and PAF documentation are completed before the Beneficiary enters H-1B status on October 1, 2025.

## V. Significant Issues

### S-1. Wage-level / job-duty inconsistency (Level 2 vs. supervisory, independent-judgment duties)

The PWD and LCA set the prevailing wage at **Level 2** ($113,734). The DOL four-tier framework describes Level 2 as workers who "perform moderately complex tasks requiring **limited judgment**" and does **not** include supervisory responsibility. Level 3 covers workers who "may exercise **independent judgment** and **may have supervisory responsibilities**"; Level 4 covers workers who "supervise and train other workers" and "exercise broad latitude."

The position as described in the Support Letter and LCA includes:

- "Leading and mentoring a team of 3 junior data scientists" (Support Letter duty 4; 15% of time);
- "lead and mentor a team of data scientists" (LCA §G.5; PWD job-duty summary);
- "exercises **independent judgment** in the design and implementation of machine learning solutions" (Support Letter §2);
- a "Senior" title requiring 3+ years of experience, held by a Beneficiary with 8+ years.

These duties map to **Level 3 (or arguably Level 4)**, not Level 2. The offered wage of **$128,000** exceeds Level 2 ($113,734) but is **below Level 3 ($137,946)** and Level 4 ($162,157).

**Safe-harbor nuance.** Because the Petitioner used an NPWC-issued PWD and disclosed the "lead a team" duty in the PWD request, the prevailing-wage safe harbor of 20 CFR § 655.731(a)(1) likely protects prevailing-wage compliance for the LCA as certified. **However**, the petition's narrative is internally inconsistent: the same supervisory/independent-judgment language used to bolster the specialty-occupation argument undercuts the Level 2 wage. If the position were re-evaluated at Level 3 (e.g., on DOL audit or a redetermination), the offered wage would be **insufficient**, and the LCA wage attestation (INA § 212(n)(1)(A)) would be violated.

**Recommendation.** Reconcile the wage level with the job duties. Either (a) increase the offered wage to at least **$137,946** (Level 3) to eliminate any exposure if the level is revisited; or (b) if maintaining Level 2, ensure the Support Letter does not overstate supervisory/independent-judgment authority in a way that contradicts Level 2. The current draft does both at once and should not be filed as-is.
