       I. Introduction and Scope  
      This memorandum reviews the document production served by Respondent Darren Kwan through his counsel, Raymond Fossett of Hargrove Fossett & Yates LLP, in response to Petitioner Melinda Torres-Kwan’s First Request for Production of Documents, Nos. 1–22. The production consists of a cover/transmittal letter and seven (7) produced document sets (PROD-001 through PROD-007), together with notice of four (4) documents withheld on privilege (PRIV-001 through PRIV-004) and a stated intent to supplement entity-level NovaBridge records responsive to Request Nos. 16–22.  
      The purpose of this review is to (a) catalog the exhibits, (b) summarize the financial figures reported, (c) flag inconsistencies, omissions, and red flags between and within the produced documents, and (d) recommend follow-up discovery. All figures below were recomputed and footed against the source documents; internal arithmetic in the Financial Declaration, the 2023 tax return, the Schedule K-1, the Crestline statements, the Elm Street mortgage statements, and the NovaBridge expense reports was verified and ties, except as noted. Three of the four credit-card statements do not foot (see Issue No. 17).  
          Bottom line.         The production, on its face, materially understates Respondent’s income and liabilities and omits at least one real property, one mortgage, one financial account, and recurring transfers to an unidentified third party. The most serious items are: (1) reporting $174,000 of cash distributions as         “    K-1 income    ”         when the Schedule K-1 reports $367,200 of ordinary business income (a $193,200 understatement); (2) the complete omission of a solely-owned rental property at 1035 Elm Street, Unit C and its ~$241,281 mortgage; (3) an $85,000 outgoing wire on June 15, 2024 from the Crestline brokerage account to an undisclosed account at Harborstone Financial Group; and (4) a conflict in the date of separation (June 1, 2024 in the sworn declaration vs. August 1, 2024 in counsel’s own privilege log). These warrant targeted supplemental demands, third-party subpoenas, and a forensic accounting trace before any support or equitable-distribution numbers are relied upon.

         IV. Inconsistencies, Omissions, and Red Flags  
      Issues are grouped by theme and tagged by severity:             [HIGH]         bears directly on income, asset/liability disclosure, or dissipation;             [MODERATE]         requires explanation or corroboration;             [LOW]         is a production/housekeeping error.  
       A. Income reporting  
          1. [HIGH] K-1 cash distributions reported as                 “        income        ”                 instead of K-1 ordinary business income — $193,200 understatement.         The declaration (¶15–16) reports         “    $174,000    ”         as         “    K-1 Income / Distributions    ”         and totals income at $359,000. The Schedule K-1 (PROD-002, DK-PROD-0053) reports             Box 1 ordinary business income of $367,200    ; the $174,000 is             Box 19a cash distributions    . These are different concepts: the $367,200 is Respondent’s taxable share of NovaBridge’s 2023 profit; the $174,000 is the cash he withdrew. W-2 ($185,000) + K-1 ordinary business income ($367,200) =             $552,200    , not $359,000. For child-support and alimony purposes the pass-through income figure (subject to the court’s add-back/deduction analysis), not the cash distribution, is the relevant measure.             Cite:         Declaration ¶¶15–16; K-1 Box 1 vs. Box 19a.  
          2. [HIGH] Undisclosed rental income from 1035 Elm Street, Unit C.         Schedule E (PROD-002, DK-PROD-0050) reports $25,200 gross rents and $2,940 net rental income for 2023, with mortgage interest of $11,280 paid to Meridian. The FCB checking statements (PROD-004) show         “    ACH Deposit — Tenant Rent Payment    ”         of             $2,100/month         in April, May, June, and July 2024 (continuing the same $2,100/mo stream). The declaration nonetheless states Respondent has         “    no other sources of income, including … rental income …             None    ”         (¶19).             Cite:         Declaration ¶19; Schedule E; FCB statements 4/5, 5/3, 6/5, 7/3/2024.  
          3. [MODERATE] Undisclosed vehicle allowance.         W-2 #1 Box 14 reports a $9,600 vehicle allowance ($800/mo employer stipend) (PROD-002, DK-PROD-0048). This compensation is not mentioned in the declaration’s income section, even though the declaration separately claims auto-insurance and gasoline expenses.             Cite:         W-2 Box 14; Declaration ¶19.  
          4. [MODERATE] Pay frequency / deposit amount do not reconcile; no pay stubs produced.         The declaration states the $185,000 salary is         “    paid on a bi-monthly basis    ”         (¶14) — i.e., 24 semi-monthly pays, which would be $7,708.33 gross per pay. The FCB statements show direct deposits of             $7,115.38         on approximately the 1st and 15th of each month. $7,115.38 × 26 = $185,000 (bi-weekly), while $7,115.38 × 24 = $170,769.12 (not $185,000). The deposit amount corresponds to a bi-weekly gross, not a semi-monthly gross, so the stated frequency and the deposit amount are inconsistent. Pay stubs — which the declaration says         “    have been or will be produced    ”         (¶57) — were             not         in this production.             Cite:         Declaration ¶14, ¶57; FCB payroll deposits.
