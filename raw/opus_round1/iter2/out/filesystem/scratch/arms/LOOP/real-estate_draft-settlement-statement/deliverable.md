                3.  DISCREPANCIES FLAGGED  
             The following inconsistencies were identified among the closing documents. None is believed to affect the monetary reconciliation, but each should be reconciled or corrected on or before Closing.  
            Title commitment number and date.         PSA §5.1 references a title commitment "dated April 30, 2025, Commitment No. PCT-2025-08814." The title commitment actually delivered is Commitment No. TC-2025-07182, effective June 18, 2025 (issued by Pinnacle Abstract & Title LLC for Continental Fidelity/Hartleigh Title Insurance Company). The file number PCT-2025-08814 is used on the settlement statement as the file reference. Confirm that the June 18 commitment (TC-2025-07182) is the operative commitment and that the PSA reference is simply an earlier-filed or placeholder number.  
            Title underwriter name.         The title commitment refers to the underwriter as both "Continental Fidelity Title Insurance Company" (header) and "Continental Hartleigh Title Insurance Company" (body and Conditions & Stipulations). These appear to be two different names for the same entity. Confirm the correct legal name of the underwriter so that the owner's and lender's policies are issued in the proper name.  
            Closing-agent address.         The PSA and title commitment list Pinnacle Abstract & Title LLC at 88 Field Point Road, Greenwich, CT 06830. The lien payoff cover letter is addressed to the closing officer at 3100 Main Street, Suite 204, Bridgeport, CT 06604, and the tax-lien statement is addressed to 1200 Post Road East, Suite 214, Westport, CT 06880. Confirm the correct office and, critically, verify all wire instructions by independent verbal callback before disbursing (wire-fraud best practice).  
            Letters Testamentary date.         The Fiduciary's Certificate states Letters Testamentary were issued on October 11, 2024; the title commitment states "on or about October 14, 2024." Reconcile the exact issuance date. The certificate is dated June 25, 2025, which is within the Lender's 60-day and the Title Company's 60-day recency requirements for the July 15, 2025 Closing.  
            Commercial lease remaining term.         PSA Exhibit C states the remaining term as of Closing (7/15/2025) is "approximately 5 years, 5 months, and 16 days" (lease expires 12/31/2030). The Lender Commitment and the rent-roll Notes column both state "approximately four and one-half years remaining." From July 15, 2025 to December 31, 2030 is approximately 5 years, 5.5 months — not 4.5 years. The "4.5 years" figures appear to be errors. Confirm the correct remaining term, as it is relevant to the Lender's underwriting and the required Subordination, Non-Disturbance, and Attornment Agreement (SNDA).  
            Lender loan-to-value (LTV) figure.         The Lender Commitment states LTV is "approximately 67.7%" and then computes $2,640,000 ÷ $3,920,000 (appraised value) = 67.35%, "rounded to approximately 67.7%." However, 67.35% does not round to 67.7%; the 67.7% figure corresponds to $2,640,000 ÷ $3,900,000 (purchase price) = 67.69%. The commitment conflates the purchase-price-based and appraised-value-based LTV. Cosmetic, but confirm the Lender's intended LTV basis.  
            Payoff-letter date references.         Title Commitment Requirements 4 and 5 reference Harborstone payoff statements "dated June 10, 2025." The payoff letters actually enclosed (and used on the settlement statement) are dated July 8, 2025 and supersede any earlier statement. Confirm the July 8 figures ($687,412.33 and $148,219.56, good through 7/20/2025) are the operative payoffs.  
            Financing-contingency cross-reference.         The Lender Commitment states the financing contingency was waived "pursuant to Section 5.2 of the Purchase Agreement." In the PSA, §5.2 is "Permitted Exceptions"; the financing waiver is addressed in PSA §3.3 and §10.2. Minor cross-reference error in the commitment letter; the waiver itself is documented and effective as of June 10, 2025.  
            Occupied vs. collected rent distinction.         The rent roll reports "Total Monthly Rent (Occupied Units) $25,200.00" (which includes Unit 2E, occupied but delinquent) and "Total Collected July 2025 Rent $23,600.00" (which excludes 2E). The $1,600.00 difference equals Unit 2E's delinquent July rent. The settlement statement prorates only the $23,600.00 collected, consistent with PSA §7.3 (no credit for uncollected rents). No error, but the distinction should be understood by all parties.  
                4.  ASSUMPTIONS MADE  
             The following assumptions were used where figures were unavailable, not yet determined, or required estimation. Each is subject to adjustment, reproration, or confirmation as noted.  
            Earnest-money accrued interest (estimated $730.42).         No interest figure was provided in the delivered documents. The settlement statement estimates $730.42 assuming a 2.00% APY escrow rate: First Deposit $100,000 × 82 days (4/24/2025–7/15/2025) = $449.32; Second Deposit $95,000 × 54 days (5/22/2025–7/15/2025) = $281.10. The actual interest credit will be per the Escrow Agent's final accounting and will adjust Buyer's cash-to-close (and the sources/uses) by any difference. PSA §3.2(e) requires the interest be credited to Buyer.  
            Connecticut Real Estate Conveyance Tax (estimated $44,750.00 total; $22,375.00 per party).         Taken from the Title Commitment Schedule C estimate, split 50/50 per PSA §12.1 and §6.4. The Title Commitment notes this is an estimate using "the tiered rate structure applicable to the City of Bridgeport" and that the actual amount "will be confirmed upon preparation and filing of Form OP-236." The mixed-use character of the property (commercial ground floor + residential upper floors) may affect the rate computation. Subject to adjustment at Closing.  
            Next-fiscal-year (2025–2026) property tax (estimated at the current-year levy of $52,480.00).         The new mill rate has not been set and no bill has been issued (Tax Lien Statement §3). Per PSA §7.1(b), the current-year amount is used as the estimate. Seller is charged for 14 days (7/1–7/14) = $2,012.93; Buyer is credited and will pay the bill when issued. Subject to reproration under PSA §7.7 within 90 days after Closing.  
            Security-deposit accrued interest (excluded).         The $32,400.00 transferred reflects principal only (per the security-deposit schedule). Connecticut General Statutes §47a-21 may require interest on residential security deposits held in escrow. Any accrued interest is not quantified in the delivered records and is excluded from the settlement statement. To be confirmed with Bayshore Management Co. and added to the credit to Buyer if applicable.  
            Gas and electric utilities (no proration reflected).         No final bills, meter readings, or account balances were provided for gas or electric service. Per PSA §7.5(d), utility deposits are retained by Seller and Buyer establishes new accounts as of Closing. The settlement statement reflects no gas/electric proration. If final landlord-paid gas/electric charges for Seller's period are later determined, they will be handled by reproration under PSA §7.7. (The commercial space is served by a separate HVAC system; residential/common areas use the oil-fired boiler; residential electric is likely tenant-metered.)  
            Water/sewer treatment — PSA §7.5(a) interpretation.         PSA §7.5(a) states that where a utility billing period ends on or before the Closing Date, "the full unpaid balance shall be credited to Buyer on the settlement statement." The WPCA account statement and Tax Lien Statement, however, require that the $1,847.60 water/sewer balance "be satisfied at or before closing." The settlement statement treats the $1,847.60 as a charge to Seller paid from Seller's proceeds directly to the City of Bridgeport at Closing (consistent with the WPCA's closing requirement and the Tax Lien Statement payoff summary). This is economically equivalent to the PSA's allocation (Seller bears the full cost); the difference is only mechanical (direct disbursement vs. credit to Buyer). If the parties prefer to follow the PSA's "credit to Buyer" language literally, the entry would be adjusted to Debit Seller / Credit Buyer $1,847.60, with Buyer remitting payment to the WPCA by the 8/1/2025 due date — but this would conflict with the WPCA's closing-satisfaction requirement.  
            Repair Escrow treatment.         Per PSA §8.4(c), the $45,000.00 Repair Escrow is a third-party holdback funded solely from Seller's proceeds. It is NOT a credit to Buyer and NOT part of the Purchase Price. It appears as a charge to Seller (disbursement to the Escrow Agent) and is held in a segregated, interest-bearing account. The pre-closing inspection (7/14/2025) confirms the roof deficiencies remain unrepaired; the escrow is therefore required at Closing. Any unused balance plus interest reverts to Seller after 12 months (PSA §8.4(e)).  
            Estate tax / FIRPTA.         The Estate of Gerald T. Whitford is below the Connecticut estate-tax threshold; CT estate tax due is $0.00, and the CT DRS estate-tax lien requires only a recorded release (no payoff). The settlement statement reflects $0.00 for the DRS lien; the $73.00 release-recording fee is a Seller charge. The PSA requires a FIRPTA affidavit or estate-equivalent certification; no federal or state withholding is anticipated.  
            Appraisal fee — Paid Outside of Closing (POC).         The $4,500.00 appraisal fee was paid by Buyer directly to Ashford Valuation Associates LLC on 5/5/2025, outside of Closing. Per the Lender Commitment's explicit and repeated instructions, it is disclosed on the settlement statement as POC with $0.00 impact on Buyer's cash-to-close and is NOT included in any debit/credit computation.  
            Payoff good-through dates.         All lien payoffs (Harborstone first mortgage $687,412.33; HELOC $148,219.56) and the delinquent-tax payoff ($28,602.80) are good through July 20, 2025. Closing on July 15, 2025 falls within the good-through period, so no per-diem interest adjustment is required. Assumption: Closing occurs on or before July 20, 2025. If delayed, per-diem interest applies ($112.67/day first mortgage; ~$27.93/day HELOC; $13.12/day delinquent tax) and updated payoff statements must be obtained.
