       I. Executive Summary  
      Respondent Darren Kwan produced seven (7) document sets (PROD-001 through PROD-007) in response to Petitioner’s First Request for Production, Nos. 1–22, together with a cover letter identifying four (4) documents withheld on privilege (PRIV-001 through PRIV-004). The production is facially responsive but, on cross-referencing the exhibits against one another and against the Sworn Financial Declaration (PROD-001), reveals             material omissions and internal inconsistencies         that, in our preliminary assessment, materially understate Respondent’s income, assets, and liabilities, and that raise concerns about post-separation dissipation and incomplete disclosure.  
      The most significant findings are:  
             Undisclosed real property.         The 2023 tax return (PROD-002) and the produced mortgage statements (PROD-005) both reflect a rental condominium at             1035 Elm Street, Unit C    , titled in Respondent’s name alone, carrying a mortgage of approximately             $241,281         and generating             $2,100/month         in rent. This property, its mortgage, and its rental income are             entirely omitted         from the Sworn Financial Declaration, which affirmatively states that Respondent         “    does not hold any ownership interest … in any other real property    ”         and that he has         “    no … rental income.    ”  
             Income understatement.         The Declaration reports 2023         “    K-1 income    ”         of             $174,000    , which is the             cash distribution         figure. The K-1 (PROD-002) reports             ordinary business income         of             $367,200         — a difference of             $193,200    . The Declaration also omits interest, dividend, and capital-gain income reflected on the joint return, and omits the rental income noted above.  
             $85,000 post-separation wire to an undisclosed account.         On             June 15, 2024         — two weeks after the Declaration’s stated separation date of June 1, 2024 — Respondent wired             $85,000         from the Crestline brokerage account (PROD-003) to             Harborstone Financial Group, account ending 2291    , an account that appears             nowhere         in the Declaration.  
             Recurring transfers to an unidentified third party.         The bank statements (PROD-004) show             $14,600         in Venmo transfers to         “    Kiera M.    ”         over March–July 2024 (~$2,920/month), none of which is disclosed or explained.  
             Separation-date conflict.         The Declaration fixes separation at             June 1, 2024    , while Respondent’s own cover-letter privilege log repeatedly references a separation date of             August 1, 2024    . The date of separation is dispositive to the marital-estate cutoff and to characterization of the June 15 wire and other transactions.  
             Valuation-date misrepresentation.         The Declaration represents all balances as of the June 1, 2024 separation date, but the figures reported for the individual checking, savings, and brokerage accounts in fact match the             August 1, 2024         statement values — obscuring the post-separation decline in the brokerage account (approximately             $81,245    , driven largely by the $85,000 wire).  
      A detailed exhibit catalog, figure summary, itemized inconsistency log, and recommended follow-up discovery follow.

         IV. Flagged Inconsistencies and Red Flags  
       A. Undisclosed Rental Property — 1035 Elm Street, Unit C (HIGH)  
      The Sworn Financial Declaration (¶20–23) lists only the marital residence at 418 Birchwood Lane and states that Respondent         “    does not hold any ownership interest, whether sole or joint, in any other real property.    ”         Three other exhibits contradict this:  
              PROD-002 (tax return), Schedule E, Part I:         reports a rental condominium at 1035 Elm Street, Unit C, Fairview, Columbia 20147,         “    Property titled in the name of Darren Kwan,    ”         rented 365 days, generating             $25,200         in 2023 rent ($2,100/month) and             $2,940         net rental income, with             $11,280         in mortgage interest paid to Meridian Home Lending.  
              PROD-005 (mortgage statements):         three monthly statements for loan 7842-0163-ELM, borrower Darren Kwan (sole, no co-borrower), property 1035 Elm Street, Unit C, original loan $279,000 dated Oct. 8, 2019, current principal balance             $241,281.19         as of Aug. 1, 2024, PITI             $1,680/month    .  
              PROD-004 (bank statements):         recurring         “    ACH Deposit — Tenant Rent Payment    ”         of             $2,100/month         (April 5, May 3, June 5, July 3, 2024) and a recurring         “    ACH Payment — Meridian Home Lending    ”         of             $1,680/month         (the Elm Street mortgage), distinct from the $1,842 Birchwood mortgage payment.  
          Impact:         The Declaration omits (i) a marital asset (the Elm Street condominium, acquired and financed during the marriage in October 2019), (ii) a liability of approximately             $241,281    , and (iii) rental income of             $2,100/month    . The property was acquired during the marriage and is presumptively marital notwithstanding sole title. Notably, Respondent produced mortgage statements for the             undisclosed         Elm Street property but produced             no         mortgage statements for the             disclosed         Birchwood marital residence.  
        B. Income Understatement (HIGH)  
      As detailed in Part III.A, the Declaration reports $174,000 of         “    K-1 Income / Distributions,    ”         conflating the cash distribution (K-1 Box 19a) with taxable pass-through income (K-1 Box 1 = $367,200). It also affirmatively denies interest, dividend, rental, and capital-gain income that the joint return reports. The understatement of Respondent-attributable 2023 income is at least             $196,140         and is directly material to child-support and alimony calculations.  
        C. $85,000 Wire Transfer to Undisclosed Account (HIGH — Dissipation Concern)  
      PROD-003 (Crestline, June 2024 statement) shows an outgoing wire transfer of             $85,000         on             June 15, 2024    , from account 9956 to             Harborstone Financial Group, account ending 2291         (reference WT-2024-061524-0087), initiated by the account holder with verbal telephone authorization. The Harborstone account is not listed anywhere in the Declaration’s account schedule (¶24–33). The wire occurred two weeks after the Declaration’s stated June 1, 2024 separation date. The Crestline year-to-date summary confirms total withdrawals of $97,300 through Aug. 1, 2024, of which $85,000 is this single wire (the remainder being advisory fees and a $10,550 IRS estimated-tax payment). This transfer is a prime candidate for a dissipation-of-marital-assets claim and for a tracing/subpoena of the Harborstone account.
