       I. Introduction and Scope of Review  
      This memorandum catalogs, summarizes, and evaluates the document production served by Respondent Darren Kwan through his counsel, Raymond Fossett of Hargrove Fossett & Yates LLP, in response to Petitioner Melinda Torres-Kwan’s First Request for Production of Documents, Nos. 1–22 (served September 10, 2024). The production transmittal (cover email from R. Fossett to S. Whitfield) indexes seven (7) produced document sets (PROD-001 through PROD-007) and a four-item privilege log (PRIV-001 through PRIV-004).  
      We have reviewed each produced exhibit against (a) the Respondent’s Sworn Financial Declaration (PROD-001) and (b) the internally generated figures in the supporting records.             The production reveals several material omissions and inconsistencies that, in our preliminary assessment, materially understate Respondent’s income, assets, and liabilities, and that warrant targeted follow-up discovery.         The most significant findings are (1) an entirely undisclosed investment real-property interest at 1035 Elm Street, Unit C; (2) an $85,000 wire transfer from the Crestline brokerage account to an undisclosed account at Harborstone Financial Group executed two weeks after the declared separation date; and (3) a substantial understatement of pass-through business income ($174,000 in cash distributions reported in lieu of $367,200 in K-1 ordinary business income).  
      This memorandum is work product. It is intended to guide discovery strategy and should not be produced or disclosed.

         IV. Flagged Inconsistencies and Areas of Concern  
      Findings are ordered by materiality.  
       A. Undisclosed Investment Real Property — 1035 Elm Street, Unit C  
      The financial declaration states, under penalty of perjury, that         “    Respondent does not hold any ownership interest, whether sole or joint, in any other real property    ”         (¶ 23) beyond 418 Birchwood Lane. This is directly contradicted by three produced exhibits:  
              Tax Return (PROD-002), Schedule E, Part I:         reports a rental condominium at 1035 Elm Street, Unit C, Fairview, Columbia 20147,         “    titled in the name of Darren Kwan,    ”         with $25,200 in rents received and $2,940 net rental income for 2023.  
              Mortgage Statements (PROD-005):         Meridian Home Lending loan No. 7842-0163-ELM, borrower Darren Kwan (sole; no co-borrower), original loan $279,000 dated October 8, 2019, current principal balance             $241,281.19         as of 8/1/2024, monthly PITI             $1,680.00    .  
              Bank Statements (PROD-004):         recurring         “    ACH Deposit — Tenant Rent Payment    ”         of             $2,100/month         (April–July 2024) and a recurring         “    ACH Payment — Meridian Home Lending    ”         of             $1,680/month         (the Elm Street mortgage), distinct from the $1,842 Birchwood Lane mortgage payment.  
          Impact:         The Elm Street property is a marital asset (acquired during the marriage, October 2019) that is wholly omitted from the asset schedule, and its ~$241,281 mortgage is omitted from the liability schedule. The $2,100/month rental income ($25,200/year) and the $1,680/month mortgage expense are omitted from the income and expense schedules, respectively. The declaration’s affirmative representation that no other real property is owned is false on its face. This is the single largest omission in the production.

WT-2024-061524-0087) from account ending 9956 to             Harborstone Financial Group, account ending 2291    , authorized by the account holder via recorded telephone call.

Either way, the marital brokerage asset is understated by approximately $81,000, and the destination and current balance of the $85,000 must be traced.  
        C. Understatement of Pass-Through Business Income  
      As detailed in Part III.A, the declaration reports $174,000 in         “    K-1 Income / Distributions    ”         rather than the $367,200 in K-1 ordinary business income reported on the tax return (Schedule K-1 Box 1; Schedule E Part II). The $193,200 difference was retained at the entity level and increased Respondent’s capital account to $675,800. For child-support and alimony calculations, taxable pass-through income—not cash distributions—is the relevant measure. The declaration also omits rental, interest, dividend, and capital-gains income despite affirmatively representing         “    None    ”         (¶ 19).  
        D. Date-of-Separation Discrepancy
