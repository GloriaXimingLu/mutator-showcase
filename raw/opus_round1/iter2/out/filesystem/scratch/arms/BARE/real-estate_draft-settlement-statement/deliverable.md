                SECTION A — DISCREPANCIES AND INCONSISTENCIES IN THE CLOSING FILE  
             A1.            Earnest Money Interest Not Quantified.           The PSA (§3.2(d)–(e)) requires all interest earned on the $195,000.00 earnest money (held in an interest-bearing account by the Escrow Agent) to be credited to Buyer at Closing. The closing file does not contain an interest statement from Pinnacle Abstract & Title LLC quantifying the accrued interest. The Settlement Statement reflects the $195,000.00 principal only (line 200). When the Escrow Agent confirms the accrued interest, it must be added as an additional credit to Buyer, reducing the cash required from Buyer at closing (currently $1,085,412.12).  
            •  First Deposit $100,000.00 deposited 4/24/2025; Second Deposit $95,000.00 deposited 5/22/2025; held through 7/15/2025.  
            •  Impact: Buyer's cash-to-close is overstated by the unconfirmed interest amount until the Escrow Agent's statement is received.  
             A2.            Residential Security Deposit Interest Not Quantified.           The PSA (§7.4) requires transfer to Buyer of all security deposits plus interest accrued "as required by applicable law." Under CGS §47a-21, residential security deposits in buildings with five or more units must be held in an interest-bearing escrow account. The security deposit schedule (Exhibit B) shows principal amounts only ($18,000.00 residential; $32,400.00 total). Accrued interest on the residential deposits is not quantified in the closing file. The Settlement Statement reflects principal only (line 205 / 432).  
            •  The commercial deposit ($14,400.00, Coastal Provisions Market LLC) is held in a non-interest-bearing account per the lease — no interest is due on that item.  
            •  Impact: When confirmed, residential deposit interest is an additional debit to Seller and credit to Buyer.  
             A3.            Connecticut Conveyance Tax Is an Estimate and Does Not Reconcile to Standard Rates.           The Title Commitment (Schedule C) estimates the total conveyance tax at $44,750.00 ($22,375.00 each, split 50/50 per PSA §12.1). This figure does not reconcile to a straightforward application of the Connecticut state conveyance rate (0.75% residential / 1.00% non-residential) plus the City of Bridgeport municipal rate (up to 0.50% as a designated municipality) to the $3,900,000.00 purchase price. The Property is mixed-use, which may involve apportionment between residential and non-residential components. The actual amount will be confirmed upon preparation and filing of Form OP-236 by the Closing Agent.  
            •  If the actual tax differs from $44,750.00, the parties' 50/50 shares and the reconciliation must be adjusted. The PSA (§12.1) delegates final computation to the Closing Agent.  
            •  Action: Closing Agent to confirm the computation basis and final amount before recording.  
             A4.            Title Commitment Number and Underwriter Name Inconsistencies.           The PSA (§5.1) references Title Commitment No. PCT-2025-08814, while the actual Title Commitment in the file is No. TC-2025-07182 (effective June 18, 2025). Separately, the Title Commitment body refers to the underwriter as both "Continental Fiducity Title Insurance Company" (cover page) and "Continental Hartleigh Title Insurance Company" (Conditions & Stipulations) — an internal inconsistency. The correct commitment number and underwriter name must be confirmed for the title policies and the closing file.  
             A5.            Address and Contact Inconsistencies Across Documents.           The closing file contains conflicting addresses and telephone numbers for the same parties, which creates wire-instruction and notice-delivery risk:  
            •  Pinnacle Abstract & Title LLC: 88 Field Point Road, Greenwich (PSA) vs. 1200 Post Road East, Westport (tax lien statement) vs. 3100 Main Street, Bridgeport (lien-payoff cover letter).  
            •  Ridgeline Law Group PLLC: 915 Chapel Street, New Haven (PSA / lender commitment) vs. 55 Railroad Avenue, Greenwich (lien-payoff cover letter).  
            •  Ashford, Clement & Paige LLP: telephone (203) 325-6600 (PSA) vs. (203) 547-8100 (lien-payoff letters) vs. (203) 555-4100 (probate certificate).  
            •  Action: Verify all wire instructions by telephone using a known, pre-existing number (not one from the closing documents) before transmitting funds. Do not rely on addresses/numbers appearing in a single document.  
             A6.            Water/Sewer "Credited to Buyer" Contract Language — Treatment Confirmed.           PSA §7.5(a) states that where a utility billing period ends on or before the Closing Date, the unpaid balance "shall be credited to Buyer on the settlement statement." The water/sewer bill ($1,847.60, billing period 5/15/2025–7/14/2025) ends July 14 — one day before closing — and is unpaid. The Settlement Statement treats this as a debit to Seller, paid from Seller's proceeds to the City of Bridgeport WPCA (line 412). This interpretation gives effect to the principle that the entire amount is Seller's sole responsibility and ensures the municipal lien is satisfied at closing. The "credited to Buyer" phrasing is read to mean Buyer is not charged. If the parties intend Buyer to pay the bill and receive a credit, the entry should be restructured; confirm with counsel.  
             A7.            Lien Payoff Good-Through Dates Relative to the Closing Date.           All three monetary lien payoffs and the delinquent-tax payoff are good through July 20, 2025. The scheduled closing (July 15, 2025) falls within each good-through period, so no per-diem interest adjustment is required. However, if closing is delayed beyond July 20, 2025, the following adjustments and updated payoffs will be required:  
            •  Harborstone first mortgage: +$112.67/day after 7/20/2025.  
            •  Harborstone HELOC: +~$27.93/day after 7/20/2025 (variable rate; subject to Prime adjustment).  
            •  Delinquent real estate tax: +$13.12/day after 7/20/2025 (per Tax Collector's certificate).  
            •  Northbridge mechanic's lien settlement ($31,000.00) is contingent on closing by August 31, 2025; Northbridge may withdraw the settlement and pursue the full $37,500.00 lien if closing occurs later.  
             A8.            Repair Escrow Adequacy — Roof Deficiencies Remain Unrepaired.           The pre-closing inspection (July 14, 2025) confirms that the roof deficiencies identified during due diligence remain unrepaired: ~400 sq ft of blistering/delamination, compromised parapet flashing, ponding-water evidence at two drains, and EPDM seam separation over the mechanical room. The inspector estimates the modified-bitumen sections have only 1–2 years of remaining useful life without intervention and recommends Buyer obtain competitive bids to confirm the $45,000.00 escrow is adequate for the full scope (membrane replacement, flashing repair, drainage correction, EPDM seam repair). The escrow is funded from Seller's proceeds and is NOT a credit to Buyer (PSA §8.4(c)); Buyer has 12 months to complete repairs and submit invoices, with any unused balance returned to Seller. Flag: the escrow may be insufficient; Buyer should verify promptly after closing.  
             A9.            Unit 2E Delinquent Rent — No Closing Credit.           Unit 2E's July 2025 rent ($1,600.00) is delinquent and is excluded entirely from the rent proration per PSA §7.3. Buyer receives no closing credit for this amount. After closing, Buyer may collect it (but is not obligated to litigate or evict); any amount collected is applied first to current rent, with excess remitted to Seller within 30 days (§7.3(b)). Seller may not commence or continue eviction/collection post-closing without Buyer's consent (§7.3(c)). The delinquent balance is a post-closing collection matter, not a settlement-statement adjustment.  
             A10.            Unit 3C Vacancy — No Rent or Security Deposit.           Unit 3C has been vacant since 11/01/2024; no July rent is prorated and no security deposit is held or transferred (the prior tenant's deposit was returned on lease termination). The estimated market rent of $1,650.00/month is informational only. The unit is in turnkey condition per the pre-closing inspection and ready for leasing.  
             A11.            Multiple Month-to-Month Residential Tenancies.           Six residential units (2B, 2C, 2E, 3B, 3D, 3F) are on month-to-month holdover status (original lease terms expired). Buyer assumes these tenancies at closing. This is not a settlement discrepancy, but Buyer should be aware of the rollover/lease-stabilization exposure post-closing. The rent proration reflects current in-place rents for these units.  
                SECTION B — ASSUMPTIONS AND METHODOLOGY  
              The Settlement Statement was prepared based on the following assumptions and methodology. Each is supported by the cited provision of the PSA or the cited closing document.  
             B1.                     Closing occurs on July 15, 2025, as scheduled, within all payoff good-through periods (July 20, 2025). (PSA §6.1.)  
             B2.                     Prorations are calculated as of 12:01 a.m. ET on the Closing Date; the day of closing (July 15) is allocated to Buyer. (PSA, Article 7.)  
             B3.                     A 365-day year is used for real-estate-tax prorations; the actual number of days in the month of closing (31 for July) is used for rent prorations. (PSA §7.1, §7.2.)  
             B4.                     Rent proration uses the individual-lease method — calculated separately for each lease, then aggregated — per PSA §7.2(d). Fractional cents are rounded to the nearest cent, with any remaining balance allocated to Buyer per §7.2(c).  
             B5.                     Only July 2025 rents actually collected are prorated. Delinquent rent (Unit 2E, $1,600.00) and the vacant unit (3C) are excluded entirely. (PSA §7.3; Rent Roll, Exhibit B.)  
             B6.                     The Connecticut conveyance tax is estimated at $44,750.00 total ($22,375.00 each) per the Title Commitment (Schedule C), split 50/50 per PSA §12.1. Subject to confirmation on Form OP-236. (See Discrepancy A3.)  
             B7.                     The FY 2025–2026 (July 1, 2025 – June 30, 2026) property tax is estimated at the current year's $52,480.00 because the new mill rate has not been set. Reproration required within 90 days post-closing when the actual bill issues. (PSA §7.1(b), §7.7; Tax Collector's certificate.)  
             B8.                     Security deposits are transferred at principal amounts ($32,400.00 total). Accrued interest on residential deposits is to be added when confirmed. (PSA §7.4; Security Deposit schedule. See Discrepancy A2.)  
             B9.                     Earnest money is credited at $195,000.00 principal. Accrued interest is to be added when confirmed by the Escrow Agent. (PSA §3.2. See Discrepancy A1.)  
             B10.                     Heating oil is valued at 180 gallons × $3.85/gallon = $693.00, based on the gauge reading at the pre-closing inspection (July 14, 2025) and Seller's last delivery receipt (Shoreline Fuel & Oil Co., June 2, 2025). (PSA §7.6; Pre-Closing Inspection Report.)  
             B11.                     The water/sewer charge of $1,847.60 is Seller's sole responsibility (entire billing period precedes closing) and is paid from Seller's proceeds. (PSA §7.5(a); Utility Account Statement. See Discrepancy A6.)  
             B12.                     The Repair Escrow of $45,000.00 is funded solely from Seller's proceeds and held by Pinnacle in a segregated account; it is a third-party holdback and is NOT a credit to Buyer. (PSA §8.4.)  
             B13.                     The appraisal fee of $4,500.00 is Paid Outside of Closing (POC, paid May 5, 2025) and is excluded from Buyer's cash-to-close per the Lender's express instructions. (Lender Commitment §4.2, §6.2.)  
             B14.                     No brokerage commissions, finder's fees, or similar compensation are due. (PSA §15.7.)  
             B15.                     All lien releases (Harborstone first mortgage, HELOC, Northbridge mechanic's lien, CT DRS estate tax lien) are to be recorded at or promptly after closing; recording fees are allocated per PSA §6.4.  
             B16.                     The CT DRS estate tax lien requires no monetary payoff ($0.00); only the $73.00 recording fee for the release is charged to Seller. (PSA §5.3(d); Probate Fiduciary Certificate, §E.)  
             B17.                     This is a probate sale. The Executrix (Claudia Whitford-Barnes) is authorized to convey by the Fiduciary's Certificate (dated June 25, 2025) and Letters Testamentary (issued October 11, 2024); no further court order is required. (Probate Fiduciary Certificate, §2.)  
             B18.                     The loan proceeds of $2,640,000.00 from Tidewater Savings Bank are credited to Buyer at closing. (Lender Commitment §2; PSA §3.3.)

                SECTION E — POST-CLOSING AND SURVIVAL ITEMS  
             E1.                     Proration reproration (90 days). Any proration that could not be finally determined at closing — principally the FY 2025–2026 property tax and any estimated utility charges — will be readjusted within 90 days after closing when final figures are available. Amounts owed are payable within 10 business days of demand with supporting documentation. (PSA §7.7, §15.1.)  
             E2.                     Repair Escrow (12 months). Buyer has 12 months from closing to complete the roof repairs and submit paid invoices (with contractor lien waivers) to the Escrow Agent for reimbursement. Any unused balance, plus accrued interest, returns to Seller after 12 months. Buyer must obtain at least two competitive bids before commencing work. (PSA §8.4.)  
             E3.                     Delinquent rent collection (Unit 2E). Buyer may collect the $1,600.00 delinquent July rent (and any prior delinquencies) post-closing, applying collections first to current rent and remitting any excess to Seller within 30 days of receipt, with an accounting on request. (PSA §7.3(b)–(c).)  
             E4.                     Tenant notification (15 days). Buyer must notify all tenants of the ownership change, new rent-payment instructions, and the new security-deposit holder within 15 days after closing, in compliance with Connecticut law. (PSA §11.3.)  
             E5.                     Lien-release recording (60 days). The Closing Agent must confirm that all lien releases/satisfactions are recorded in the Bridgeport Land Records within 60 days after closing. (Lender Commitment §6.3.)  
             E6.                     Reps & warranties (12 months). Seller's representations and warranties survive closing for 12 months; no claim may be brought after that period. (PSA §15.1.)  
             E7.                     Bayshore final accounting (15 days). Bayshore Management Co. must deliver a final accounting of all income, expenses, and deposit balances within 15 days of the July 15, 2025 termination date, and transfer all security deposits (with accrued residential interest) to the Closing Agent or Buyer. (Management Agreement §11.3; Termination Notice.)  
             E8.                     Post-closing deliverables to Lender (10 business days). The Closing Agent must deliver the recorded mortgage, final title policy/marked-up commitment, recorded documents, signed settlement statement, original promissory note, and lease assignments/SNDAs to Tidewater Savings Bank within 10 business days. (Lender Commitment §6.5.)
