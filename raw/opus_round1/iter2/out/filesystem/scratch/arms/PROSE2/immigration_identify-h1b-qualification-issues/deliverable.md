        IV. Issues Identified  
      Issues are grouped by subject matter. Each issue states the finding, the source, why it matters, and a recommended fix. Severity is marked             [HIGH]    ,             [MEDIUM]    , or             [LOW]    .  
       A. Beneficiary Qualifications & Specialty Occupation  
          Issue 1 — Field-of-study mismatch between the beneficiary’s degree and the required major. [HIGH]  
              Finding:         The position requires a bachelor’s degree in         “    Computer Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, or a closely related field    ”         (LCA § G.7; Support Letter § 3). The beneficiary’s degree is a B.Tech in             Electronics and Communication Engineering         (Resume; Credential Evaluation § 1; Transcript; Support Letter § 4). The credential evaluation (WCS-2025-03187, § 5) equates the degree to a U.S. B.S. in             Electronics and Communication Engineering         — not in CS/ML/AI — and expressly states in § 7 that         “    [t]his evaluation does not opine on whether the applicant’s field of study satisfies the requirements of any particular position or petition.    ”         The evaluation’s own program analysis (§ 4) concludes that         “    computer science and information technology-related coursework constitutes approximately 12 credit hours out of the total approximately 160 credit hours … or approximately 7.5% of the overall curriculum.    ”  
              Why it matters:         Whether ECE is         “    closely related    ”         to CS/ML/AI is the central qualification question. The record contains no expert opinion bridging that gap; the only support is the Support Letter’s conclusory statement (§ 4) that coursework in         “    programming, data structures, and artificial intelligence    ”         provides         “    a strong technical foundation.    ”         With only ~6.5–7.5% of the curriculum being computer-related (and the evaluator declining to opine on fit), USCIS may issue an RFE or deny on the basis that the beneficiary does not hold a degree in the required specialty. See 8 CFR 214.2(h)(4)(iii)(A) (degree must be in the specialty or a closely related field).  
              Recommendation:         Obtain a supplemental, position-specific expert opinion (preferably from a university professor or a NACES evaluator willing to opine on fit) establishing that the ECE coursework — including Computer Architecture (EC602), Microprocessors (EC503), Digital Logic Design (EC402), Digital Image Processing (EC604), Probability & Stochastic Processes (MT401), and the Intro to AI elective (EC704) — provides the theoretical foundation for machine learning engineering and that ECE is         “    closely related    ”         to the required field. Alternatively, rely squarely on the experience-equivalency path (Issue 12) and document it fully.  
          Issue 2 — Wage-level classification appears understated for the duties described. [HIGH]  
              Finding:         The LCA (§ F.4) and PWD (§ 4) classify the position at             Wage Level 2         ($113,734), described by the PWD as applying to workers who         “    perform moderately complex tasks requiring limited judgment.    ”         Yet the Support Letter (§ 2) describes a             “        Senior        ”         role that         “    exercises independent judgment in the design and implementation of machine learning solutions,    ”         and whose Duty 4 is         “    Leading and mentoring a team of 3 junior data scientists … providing technical guidance on model development, code review, and best practices.    ”         The PWD’s own level definitions place         “    independent judgment    ”         and         “    supervisory responsibilities    ”         at             Level 3         ($137,946) and         “    supervise and train other workers    ”         at             Level 4         ($162,157).  
              Why it matters:         The offered wage of $128,000             exceeds         the Level 2 prevailing wage ($113,734) by $14,266 but             falls below         Level 3 ($137,946) by $9,946 and below Level 4 ($162,157) by $34,157. If the correct level is 3 or 4, the wage offered is below the prevailing wage, the LCA wage attestation (INA § 212(n)(1)(A); LCA § K.1) would be false, and the petition is vulnerable to denial (and the employer to back-wage liability). The         “    senior    ”         title, the independent-judgment language, and the supervisory/mentoring duties are inconsistent with Level 2’s         “    limited judgment.    ”  
              Recommendation:         Re-assess the wage level against the DOL criteria. If the duties genuinely involve independent judgment and supervision, request a new PWD at Level 3 (or 4) and, if necessary, increase the proffered wage to at least $137,946 (Level 3) before filing, or re-file the LCA at the correct level. If Level 2 is defended, revise the Support Letter to remove or soften the         “    independent judgment    ”         and         “    lead/mentor a team    ”         language so the duties are consistent with Level 2’s         “    limited judgment    ”         — but note this creates tension with the specialty-occupation and         “    senior    ”         framing (see Issue 6).  
          Issue 3 — Three years of claimed progressive experience are undocumented. [HIGH]  
              Finding:         The Resume and Support Letter (§ 4) claim         “    Independent Machine Learning Consultant    ”         experience from August 2020 to August 2023 — three years during which the beneficiary allegedly         “    Designed and deployed end-to-end machine learning systems using TensorFlow, PyTorch, and AWS SageMaker    ”         and         “    Implemented large language model (LLM) fine-tuning workflows.    ”         No experience letter, client letter, 1099, contract, or business registration is included for this period. By contrast, the Kiran and NovaStar periods are each supported by a signed experience letter.  
              Why it matters:         This three-year window is the only documented period in which the specific tools required by the position — TensorFlow, PyTorch, AWS SageMaker, and LLM fine-tuning (Support Letter § 3) — were used (the NovaStar letter references only Python, scikit-learn, Tableau, SQL, and A/B testing). Without corroboration, USCIS may decline to credit this experience, undermining both the primary qualification showing and the alternative         “    5 years of progressive ML experience    ”         path (Issue 12). Self-employment is creditable only if documented.  
              Recommendation:         Obtain letters from at least two consulting clients (on client letterhead, signed by a supervisor/engagement lead, detailing projects, dates, tools, and the beneficiary’s role), plus 1099s or invoices and any business-registration evidence. If client contact is no longer possible, supplement with a detailed sworn declaration from the beneficiary describing each engagement, deliverables, and technologies, supported by artifacts (project repositories, model documentation, deployment records).  
          Issue 4 — Current title (        “        Data Scientist        ”        ) differs from the petitioned title (        “        Senior Machine Learning Engineer        ”        ). [MEDIUM]  
              Finding:         The Resume lists the beneficiary’s current Brightfield role as             “        Data Scientist        ”         (September 2023 – present). The petitioned position is             “        Senior Machine Learning Engineer.        ”         The Support Letter (§ 4) lists the Brightfield tenure but does not state the current title or explain the relationship between the current role and the petitioned role.  
              Why it matters:         Because the beneficiary is already employed by the petitioner (on F-1 OPT), USCIS may question whether the         “    Senior Machine Learning Engineer    ”         position is a bona fide new/senior role or merely a reclassification of the existing Data Scientist duties. An unexplained title change can also complicate the specialty-occupation and wage-level analysis (a         “    senior    ”         supervisory role vs. an individual-contributor Data Scientist role).  
              Recommendation:         Add a paragraph to the Support Letter explaining the relationship: whether the Senior ML Engineer role is a promotion/new position with materially different duties (supervision, infrastructure ownership, client-facing leadership) versus the current Data Scientist role, and confirm the beneficiary will move into the petitioned role on October 1, 2025. Consider including a current Brightfield experience/role letter.  
          Issue 5 — SOC classification (15-2051 Data Scientists) vs.         “        Engineer        ”                 title and software-engineering duties. [LOW/MEDIUM]  
              Finding:         The position is titled         “    Senior Machine Learning             Engineer    ”         and includes substantial software-engineering and cloud-infrastructure duties — Duty 2 (    “    Architecting and managing cloud-based ML infrastructure on AWS … SageMaker, EC2, S3, and Lambda    ”    ) and Duty 3 (    “    production Python codebases … TensorFlow, PyTorch, and scikit-learn    ”    ) — yet it is mapped to SOC 15-2051 (Data Scientists) rather than, e.g., 15-1252 (Software Developers) or 15-1221 (Computer and Information Research Scientists).  
              Why it matters:         15-2051 is defensible for an ML role, but the engineering/infrastructure emphasis creates a mismatch between the SOC and a portion of the duties. USCIS occasionally questions SOC selection where the title and a material portion of duties point elsewhere; the wage level and specialty-occupation analysis both depend on the SOC.  
              Recommendation:         Confirm 15-2051 is the best fit (it likely is, given the modeling/NLP/LLM core). Be prepared to defend the SOC against an RFE by emphasizing that the majority of duties (model design/deployment, NLP/LLM, analytics presentation) fall within Data Scientists, and that the infrastructure duties are in service of the ML function.  
        B. Wage & LCA Compliance  
          Issue 6 — Internal inconsistency between the                 “        senior/supervisory        ”                 duties and the Level 2 wage level. [HIGH]  
              Finding:         See Issue 2. The Support Letter’s         “    senior,    ”         “    independent judgment,    ”         and         “    lead and mentor a team    ”         framing is internally inconsistent with the Level 2 wage level the employer requested and the LCA certified. The employer cannot simultaneously assert Level 2 (    “    limited judgment,    ”         “    moderately complex tasks    ”    ) and a senior supervisory role with independent judgment.  
              Why it matters:         This inconsistency is the mechanism by which USCIS or DOL could challenge the wage level. It also cuts against the specialty-occupation narrative if the duties are later softened to defend Level 2.  
              Recommendation:         Reconcile the duties and the wage level as a single, coherent position description (see Issue 2). Do not leave the         “    senior/supervisory    ”         and         “    Level 2/limited judgment    ”         characterizations side by side.  
          Issue 7 — LCA posting ran only 8 business days; 10 consecutive business days required. [MEDIUM/HIGH]  
              Finding:         The LCA (§ J.2) and Posting Notice state the notice was posted March 10, 2025 and removed March 19, 2025. March 10, 2025 is a Monday; the inclusive period March 10–19, 2025 contains only             8 business days         (Mar 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 17, 18, 19). 20 CFR § 655.734(a)(1) requires posting         “    for a total of 10 consecutive business days.    ”         To satisfy the 10-business-day requirement beginning March 10, the notice would have had to remain posted through             Friday, March 21, 2025    .  
              Why it matters:         A posting shorter than 10 consecutive business days is a regulatory violation of the LCA notice attestation (INA § 212(n)(1)(A)(iv); 20 CFR § 655.734). It exposes the employer to DOL enforcement (back wages, civil money penalties) and undermines the LCA on which the petition depends.  
              Recommendation:         Re-post the notice for a full 10 consecutive business days in two conspicuous locations, document the dates and locations, and retain evidence (photos, signed posting log) in the Public Access File. If the LCA must be re-filed to support the petition, ensure the new posting precedes or coincides with the new filing and runs the full 10 business days.  
          Issue 8 — Only one posting location documented; two conspicuous locations required. [MEDIUM]  
              Finding:         The LCA (§ J.3) documents a single posting location —         “    Employee common area / break room, first floor.    ”         The Posting Notice itself states the notice         “    is being posted in two conspicuous locations at the place of employment,    ”         but only one location is identified anywhere in the package. 20 CFR § 655.734(a)(1) requires posting         “    in at least two conspicuous locations.    ”  
              Why it matters:         Documenting only one location is inconsistent with the regulation and with the Posting Notice’s own representation, and is a notice-attestation deficiency.  
              Recommendation:         Identify and document a second conspicuous posting location (e.g., a second break area, the main entrance bulletin board, or the HR office), and retain evidence of both postings in the Public Access File.  
          Issue 9 — Consulting business model vs. single-worksite LCA representation; possible itinerary need. [MEDIUM]  
              Finding:         Brightfield is a consulting firm (NAICS 541511,         “    Custom Computer Programming Services    ”    ) that         “    provides advanced data analytics, machine learning consulting, and AI-driven product development services to enterprise clients across healthcare, financial services, and technology sectors    ”         (Support Letter § 1). The LCA (§ H.2) nevertheless states         “    Additional Worksites: None (single worksite only),    ”         and the Support Letter fixes the work location at the Austin HQ. The company also maintains         “    a satellite office in Denver, Colorado, and a small distributed remote team    ”         (Support Letter § 1).  
              Why it matters:         If the beneficiary will perform any work at client sites, at the Denver office, or remotely (including from home), the LCA’s single-worksite representation is incomplete and additional LCA worksite coverage and an itinerary are required (8 CFR § 214.2(h)(2)(i)(B), (h)(11)(i)(A); 20 CFR § 655.733). An inaccurate worksite representation is an LCA violation and a petition vulnerability.  
              Recommendation:         Confirm in writing that the beneficiary will perform all work exclusively at the Austin HQ. If any off-site, client-site, Denver, or remote work is contemplated, file an additional LCA covering each worksite and include an itinerary with the petition.

        D. Document Completeness & Status
