## Key findings flagged in the memo

**High severity (could impede closing / non-waivable conditions at risk):**
- **AML/KYC email omits Cascade Re Insurance Ltd.** — the Fund Administrator's Jan. 24 confirmation enumerates only 5 of the 6 incoming LPs; Condition 11.2(3) is non-waivable.
- **ERISA / Benefit Plan Investor calculation is internally inconsistent** — individual LP "ERISA Plan Investor" flags sum to $465,300,000 (28.81%, **above** the 24.99% cap), while the summary/Subscription Agreement state $355,300,000 (22.00%); a $110,000,000 unexplained gap. The GP Memo's 19.8% figure is wrong (wrong numerator and denominator). Condition 11.2(5) is non-waivable.
- **Amendment No. 1 misidentifies the waterfall catch-up tier** — it amends "Section 8.3(b)" (which is the LPs' 8% preferred return) instead of Section 8.3(c) (the catch-up), which could eliminate the preferred return and contradicts the amendment's "no material adverse effect" representation.
- **Key Person certification omits Priya S. Mehta** — the GP Memo certifies only Whitmore; Condition 11.2(6)/Section 9.4(d) requires both Key Persons.

**Medium severity:** equalization interest day-count discrepancy (Subscription Agreement uses 47 days / $43,602,661.64 vs. administrator worksheet 46 days / $43,599,534.24); Subscription Agreement default interest (18%) conflicts with the LPA (12%); legal opinions still in draft and the limited-liability opinion required by 11.2(7)(iii) not expressly listed; Advisory Committee Target-Fund-Size notice (Section 3.1(d)) not flagged as an action item; escrow funding and Schedule A finalization pending.
