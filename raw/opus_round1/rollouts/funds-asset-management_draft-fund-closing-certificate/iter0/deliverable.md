## II. Critical Issues Requiring Resolution Before Closing

### A. ERISA / Benefit Plan Investor Calculation — Multiple Inconsistent Figures; Potential Cap Exceedance

**This is the most significant issue identified.** Condition 5 of Section 11.2 (ERISA Compliance) is **non-waivable** under Section 11.3 of the LPA. The Source Documents contain at least four different Benefit Plan Investor ("**BPI**") calculations, which do not reconcile with one another:

| Source | BPI Commitments | Denominator | BPI % | Status |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|-------|--------|
| GP Memo (§V.C) | \$225,300,000 (prior closings only) | \$1,140,000,000 | 19.8% | Wrong denominator; excludes final-closing BPIs |
| Sub Agreement (§2.3; Sch. I) | \$355,300,000 | \$1,615,000,000 | 22.00% | Below cap |
| Checklist — ERISA Analysis tab | \$355,300,000 | \$1,615,000,000 | 22.00% | Below cap; does not match Commitment Schedule "Y" markings |
| Checklist — Commitment Schedule "Y" markings | \$465,300,000 | \$1,615,000,000 | **28.81%** | **Exceeds 24.99% cap** |

**Specific problems:**

1. **GP Memo uses an incorrect denominator.** The GP Memo (§V.C) states that "total Fund commitments will be approximately \$1,140,000,000" and calculates the BPI percentage as \$225,300,000 / \$1,140,000,000 = 19.8%. The correct total Fund commitments figure is **\$1,615,000,000** (as stated in GP Memo §III, the Sub Agreement, and the Checklist). The \$1,140,000,000 figure appears nowhere else and is incorrect. Additionally, the GP Memo's numerator (\$225,300,000) reflects only prior-closing BPIs and **excludes the \$130,000,000 of final-closing BPIs** (Meridian and Sentry), which must be included when calculating the post-Final-Closing percentage.

2. **The Checklist's Commitment Schedule "Y" markings do not match its own ERISA Analysis.** The Commitment Schedule marks **nine (9) LPs** as "Y" (ERISA Plan Investor), totaling **\$465,300,000**, which equals **28.81%** of total commitments and **exceeds the 24.99% cap**. The ERISA Analysis tab, however, calculates only **\$355,300,000 (22.00%)**. The \$110,000,000 difference corresponds exactly to two LPs excluded from the ERISA Analysis: Commonwealth Public Employees' Retirement Fund (\$85,000,000) and Ashford Healthcare System Retirement Plan (\$25,000,000). There is no apparent rationale for excluding these two LPs while including the other seven "Y"-marked LPs.

3. **Treatment of governmental plans is inconsistent and not supported by the LPA.** Of the nine LPs marked "Y" for ERISA in the Commitment Schedule, **eight are governmental plans** (state pension funds, municipal pension trusts, and a teachers' retirement system), which are generally **exempt from Title I of ERISA** under Section 3(32) of ERISA. Only one — Ashford Healthcare System Retirement Plan (\$25,000,000) — is a true ERISA plan. The LPA's definition of "Benefit Plan Investor" (Article I) tracks the ERISA/Section 4975 plan-assets definition and **does not include governmental plans**. Nevertheless, the Sub Agreement (§2.3, Box C) states that governmental plans are "treated as benefit plan investors to the extent specified in the LPA" — but the LPA does not in fact specify such treatment. The GP Memo likewise labels Meridian and Sentry as "Benefit plan investor (ERISA plan investor)," which is technically imprecise for governmental plans.

**Implications and recommended resolution:**

- If governmental plans are **not** counted as BPIs (consistent with the LPA's Article I definition), only Ashford (\$25,000,000) is a true BPI, yielding **1.55%** — well below the cap, and the condition is satisfied.
- If governmental plans **are** counted as BPIs (as the Commitment Schedule "Y" markings and the Sub Agreement's Box C suggest), the total is **\$465,300,000 = 28.81%**, which **exceeds the 24.99% cap** — meaning the non-waivable ERISA condition would **not** be satisfied and the Final Closing could not lawfully proceed without reducing BPI commitments.
- The 22.00% figure used in the Sub Agreement and the Checklist's ERISA Analysis reflects an **inconsistent subset** of the "Y"-marked LPs and does not correspond to any single coherent methodology.

**Fund Counsel recommends** that the General Partner, in consultation with ERISA counsel, (i) confirm the correct interpretation of "Benefit Plan Investor" under the LPA, (ii) reconcile the Commitment Schedule "Y" markings with the ERISA Analysis, and (iii) document a single, internally consistent BPI calculation before the Final Closing Certificate is executed. The certificate should not certify Condition 5 until this reconciliation is complete.

### C. LPA Amendment No. 1 — Section 8.3(b) / 8.3(c) Catch-Up Correction Mislabeled

Amendment No. 1, Section 1.2, purports to correct a "technical error" in "**Section 8.3(b)**" of the LPA, which it describes as the "second tier of the distribution waterfall (the General Partner's 'catch-up' allocation)." However, in the LPA as executed:

- **Section 8.3(b)** is the **Preferred Return** tier (100% to the Partners until each Partner has received an 8% IRR preferred return);
- **Section 8.3(c)** is the **GP Catch-Up** tier (100% to the General Partner until the GP has received 20% of amounts distributed under Sections 8.3(b) and 8.3(c)).

The Amendment therefore misidentifies the section being corrected. The "original" language quoted in the Amendment (distributions "to the General Partner in an amount equal to 20% of the aggregate amount distributed pursuant to Section 8.3(a) and this Section 8.3(b)") **does not match** the actual text of Section 8.3(b) in the LPA (which is the preferred return, 100% to Partners). If the Amendment's replacement text were applied literally to Section 8.3(b), it would **delete the preferred return** and replace it with a second catch-up provision — an economically nonsensical result that would materially and adversely affect the Limited Partners.

The intended correction almost certainly targets **Section 8.3(c)** (the GP Catch-Up), not Section 8.3(b). Notably, the LPA's Section 8.3(c) already appears to contain a properly functioning catch-up mechanism, raising a further question as to whether the "technical error" the Amendment describes ever existed in the form stated.

**Fund Counsel recommends** that the Amendment be reviewed and, if necessary, corrected (via a further amendment or clarifying memorandum) to reference the correct section (8.3(c)) and to confirm that the preferred return in Section 8.3(b) remains intact. Because the Amendment was effected unilaterally by the General Partner under Section 14.1 of the LPA (see Section III.D below), any correction should be carefully documented.

## III. Significant Discrepancies in Source Documents

### A. Birchwood Insurance Co. — Capital Commitment Amount Inconsistent

The Amendment (Section 1.3(a)) states that Birchwood Insurance Co., a Second Closing Limited Partner, has a Capital Commitment of **\$75,000,000**. However:

- **CC#2** (Exhibit A) lists Birchwood at **\$60,000,000** (5% call = \$3,000,000, which is consistent with \$60,000,000);
- The **Checklist** (Commitment Schedule) lists Birchwood at **\$60,000,000**; and
- The Amendment's **own aggregate** for the Second Closing is \$375,000,000 for nine LPs. The nine Second Closing LPs per the Checklist sum to \$375,000,000 **only if** Birchwood is \$60,000,000; if Birchwood were \$75,000,000, the Second Closing total would be \$390,000,000, which contradicts the Amendment's stated aggregate.

The **\$75,000,000 figure in the Amendment is internally inconsistent** with the Amendment's own \$375,000,000 aggregate and is contradicted by CC#2 and the Checklist. The correct figure appears to be **\$60,000,000**. Fund Counsel recommends correcting the Amendment (or confirming the correct amount) and ensuring the Advisory Committee appointment of Thomas A. Riley (Birchwood's representative) references the correct commitment.

### B. Default Interest Rate — Three Different Rates Across Documents

The documents state three different default interest rates:

| Document | Default Interest Rate |
|----------|----------------------|
| LPA (§3.3(b)) | 12% per annum (cure-period interest; no compounding specified) |
| CC#1 (§V) | 12% per annum (or maximum rate permitted by law, if lower) |
| CC#2 (§V) | 12% per annum, **compounding quarterly** |
| Sub Agreement (§1.2) | **18% per annum** (lesser of 18% and maximum rate permitted by law) |

The **Sub Agreement's 18% rate is inconsistent with the LPA's 12% rate** and would overstate the default remedy available to the Fund. The **CC#2 "compounding quarterly"** specification is not found in the LPA and is inconsistent with CC#1 (which does not mention compounding). Fund Counsel recommends conforming the Sub Agreement and the capital call notices to the LPA's 12% per annum simple-interest default rate, or amending the LPA if a different rate is intended. The Final Closing Certificate should not be relied upon to cure this inconsistency.

### C. Equalization Interest — Day-Count Discrepancies

Section 3.2(c) of the LPA provides that equalization interest is calculated "from and including the date of each prior Capital Call through and including the date of the applicable Subsequent Closing." The day counts used in the Source Documents do not consistently follow this "inclusive both endpoints" methodology:

| Capital Call | LPA Method (inclusive both ends) | Sub Agreement | Checklist (Equalization Calc tab) |
|--------------|----------------------------------|---------------|-----------------------------------|
| CC#1 (Oct 10, 2024 → Jan 31, 2025) | **114 days** | 113 days | 113 days |
| CC#2 (Dec 15, 2024 → Jan 31, 2025) | **48 days** | 47 days | **46 days** |

The **Checklist's 46-day count for CC#2** does not correspond to any standard convention (it is neither the date-difference of 47 nor the inclusive count of 48). This produces a different total equalization interest than the Sub Agreement:

- **Checklist total equalization interest:** \$849,534.24 (total due: \$43,599,534.24)
- **Sub Agreement total equalization interest:** \$852,661.64 aggregate / \$852,657.53 per-LP sum (total due: \$43,602,661.64)

The difference (\$3,127.40 in total amounts due) is small in absolute terms but affects the precise amount each Final Closing Limited Partner must wire. Fund Counsel recommends that Oakmere confirm the controlling day-count convention (the LPA's "from and including ... through and including" language yields 114 and 48 days), reconcile the two calculations, and ensure the closing statements delivered to each Final Closing Limited Partner reflect a single agreed figure.

### E. Key Person Certification — GP Memo Addresses Only One of Two Key Persons

Section 9.4(d) of the LPA requires the General Partner to certify at each Closing that **each** Key Person continues to devote substantially all of his or her business time to the Partnership and that no Key Person Event has occurred. The Key Persons are **Marcus J. Whitmore** and **Priya S. Mehta** (LPA §9.4(a)).

The GP Memo (§VII) addresses **only Marcus J. Whitmore**, stating that he "continues to devote substantially all of his business time to the affairs of Fund VII." The GP Memo does **not** address Priya S. Mehta's status. By contrast, the Amendment (§2.2(b)) properly certifies that "**both** Marcus J. Whitmore and Priya S. Mehta ... continue to devote substantially all of their business time and professional efforts" to the Partnership.

The Final Closing Certificate must certify the status of **both** Key Persons. Fund Counsel has drafted the certificate accordingly (see Section II of the Final Closing Certificate). The General Partner should confirm that it has the factual basis to certify Ms. Mehta's continued full-time devotion to the Fund.

### F. GP Representations and Warranties — No Dedicated Article in the LPA

Condition 10 of Section 11.2(10) requires the General Partner to certify that "all representations and warranties of the General Partner contained in this Agreement are true and correct." However, the LPA does **not contain a dedicated article of General Partner representations and warranties**. Article VI is titled "Representations, Warranties, and Covenants of **Limited Partners**" (not the GP). The Source Documents cite inconsistent (and incorrect) locations for GP representations:

- The **GP Memo** (§VI, Condition 10) references "**Article VII**" (which is actually Management Fees and Expenses);
- The **Amendment** (§2.2(a)) references "**Article XII**" (which is actually Confidentiality; Side Letters; MFN).

Fund Counsel recommends that the General Partner identify the specific provisions of the LPA containing GP representations and warranties (if any), or confirm that the certification under Condition 10 is intended to cover the General Partner's representations embedded throughout the LPA (e.g., the SEC registration representation in Section 9.2, the Key Person undertakings in Section 9.4, and the GP Commitment representation in Section 3.1(e)). The Final Closing Certificate certifies Condition 10 in general terms; the General Partner should satisfy itself that this certification is supportable.

## VI. Additional Issues and Observations

### A. Advisory Committee Notification — Target Fund Size Exceeded (Not Yet Addressed)

Following the Final Closing, aggregate Capital Commitments (\$1,615,000,000) will exceed the Target Fund Size of \$1,500,000,000 by \$115,000,000. Section 3.1(d) of the LPA requires the General Partner to notify the Advisory Committee in writing of such excess, including the amount of the excess and the identities and Capital Commitments of the Partners admitted at the applicable Closing, **not later than five (5) Business Days following the Closing** at which the Target Fund Size is first exceeded. The Target Fund Size was not exceeded at the Initial or Second Closings (cumulative \$1,330,000,000), so this notification obligation is triggered at the Final Closing.

This notification is **not listed as an outstanding item in the GP Memo** (§XII) and does not appear in the Checklist. The Sub Agreement (§5.1 and Schedule I) characterizes this notification as "a condition to the acceptance of commitments above the Fund Target," which **overstates** the LPA requirement — Section 3.1(d) expressly provides that exceeding the Target Fund Size "shall not require Advisory Committee consent." Fund Counsel recommends (i) correcting the Sub Agreement's characterization and (ii) calendaring the Advisory Committee notification for delivery within five Business Days of the Final Closing. The Final Closing Certificate acknowledges this obligation in Section III(a).
