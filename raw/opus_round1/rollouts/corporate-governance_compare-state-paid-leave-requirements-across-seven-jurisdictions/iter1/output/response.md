# Multi-State Paid Leave Compliance Analysis — Deliverables Summary

**Client:** Vantage Logistics Group, Inc.
**Prepared by:** Hartwell & Cabot LLP (Daniel Ikeda, Senior Associate; Catherine Ng, Partner)
**Engagement:** Multi-State Paid Leave Compliance Review (7 jurisdictions) — April 14, 2025
**Deliverable date:** May 19, 2025

## Deliverables

### 1. `multi-state-compliance-matrix.xlsx`
A 10-sheet, color-coded compliance matrix comparing Vantage's current paid leave policies (Employee Handbook, Rev. 1/15/2022) and implemented practices against the statutory/regulatory requirements of all seven jurisdictions (NJ, NY, CT, MA, CO, OR, WA).

- **Cover & Legend** — engagement metadata, R/Y/G/N/A indicator definitions, scope & methodology notes.
- **Summary Dashboard** — overall status grid (5 leave categories × 7 states) plus dimension-level gap counts (301 dimensions scored: 162 red, 28 yellow, 87 green, 24 N/A).
- **Per-state detail tabs (NJ, NY, CT, MA, CO, OR, WA)** — dimension-by-dimension comparison: Vantage Current Policy/Practice | State Statutory Requirement | Gap Indicator | Risk Notes, across Paid Sick Leave, Paid Family Leave, Paid Medical Leave, Employer Obligations, and PTO/Vacation Payout.
- **Gap Register** — 23 consolidated material deficiencies (5 Critical, 9 High, 9 Moderate) with statutory basis, recommendation, and target date.

### 2. `gap-analysis-memo.docx`
A 13-section narrative gap analysis and remediation memorandum with: executive summary; methodology/limitations; compliance posture summary table; gap analysis organized by severity (Critical/High/Moderate); state program registration analysis with deadline table; self-funded PFL structural analysis; paid medical leave/STD misdirection analysis; manual leave-tracking operational risk; a 14-item prioritized remediation plan tied to the facility opening schedule; NJ DOL complaint resolution framework; and budget considerations.

## Key Findings (Critical)
1. **NJ Earned Sick Leave** — family-member definition and covered uses are narrower than statute; direct cause of the active NJ DOL complaint (Case No. NJ-ESL-2025-04117, *Muñoz*); 890 NJ employees exposed.
2. **CO FAMLI registration** — not registered; 3 remote CO employees hired April 2025 likely triggered the obligation; private employers cannot self-fund.
3. **MA PFML registration** — not registered; facility opens Aug 1, 2025; must register by ~June 1, 2025 to allow PinnacleHR's 6–8 week configuration lead time.
4. **Self-funded PFL structural non-compliance** — 50% AWW / $850 cap / 8 wks is below statutory minimums in all 7 states; no private-plan approval filed; employees paying into state programs the plan purportedly replaces.
