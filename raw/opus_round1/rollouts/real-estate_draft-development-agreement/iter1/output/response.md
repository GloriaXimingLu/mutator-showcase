# Development Agreement Draft — Summary of Work

## Deliverable
`output/development-agreement-draft.docx` — a draft Development Agreement for the **Lakemont Station Commons** mixed-use transit-oriented development, prepared on behalf of **Granite City Development LLC** (the "Developer") for negotiation with the **City of Lakemont, Illinois**.

The document is ~17,000 words, organized into 18 Articles plus Recitals, a signature page, and a consolidated schedule. It was generated from markdown via Pandoc and validated against the ECMA-376 WordprocessingML schema (validation passed).

## Source documents reviewed (all read in full)
1. **Negotiated Term Sheet** (Jan. 22, 2025) — principal business terms (non-binding).
2. **RFP Response Narrative** (Dec. 16, 2024) — Developer's proposal.
3. **City Council Resolution No. R-2025-017** (Feb. 18, 2025; vote 7–2) — conditional approval with 8 conditions.
4. **Construction Loan Term Sheet** (First Prairie National Bank; March 2025) — $210M facility, lender requirements re: the Development Agreement.
5. **Lakeshore Side Letter** (Feb. 10, 2025) — 25% equity investor's consent, anti-dilution, 14% Minimum IRR, 51% change-of-control threshold.
6. **Phase II ESA Summary** (Greenfield Environmental; Nov. 8, 2024) — environmental findings (BTEX + TCE on Parcel 016; metals on Parcel 017).
7. **TIF District Summary Memo** (Corporation Counsel; Mar. 5, 2025) — TIF parameters, eligible costs, increment projections, prevailing-wage analysis.

## Approach
- Drafted developer-favorable terms throughout (e.g., no reversionary interest; broad City environmental indemnity; limited/narrowed prevailing-wage scope; good-faith LEED pursuit; flexible permitted-assignment regime; robust Developer termination rights; lender-protective provisions).
- Embedded **54 bracketed drafting notes** inline, each flagging a cross-document conflict or open item with citations to the specific source documents and sections.
- Appended a **consolidated Schedule of Cross-Document Conflicts and Open Items** (54-row table) keyed to Agreement sections for ease of negotiation reference.

## Principal conflicts flagged (highest priority)
1. **Reversionary interest vs. lender prohibition (CRITICAL).** Resolution R-2025-017, Condition 8 requires a reversionary interest if Phase I is not Substantially Complete within 36 months of conveyance; the Construction Loan Term Sheet (§§4.2, 7.1, 12) states this "would make the Loan impossible to close," and the Lakeshore Side Letter (§7(d)) requires Investor consent. The draft eliminates the reversionary interest and substitutes alternative clawback mechanisms (§9.3).
2. **36-month reversion trigger vs. Sept. 30, 2028 Phase I SC deadline.** The reversion could fire ~3 months before the contractual completion deadline (§3.3 note).
3. **Prevailing-wage scope.** Term Sheet says "all construction"; Developer position limits it to statutory public-works scope (§6.3 note).
4. **Living wage / first-source hiring for permanent positions.** Required by Resolution Condition 3(c) but absent from the Term Sheet (§8.4 note).
5. **Change-of-control thresholds.** 50% (Term Sheet), [50]% (Loan), 51% (Side Letter) — must be harmonized (§11.4 note).
6. **TIF cap reachability.** TIF Memo projects only $38–42M (adjusted for abatement) vs. the $48M cap; Developer bears shortfall risk (§4.2 note).
7. **Land write-down / infrastructure funding source.** Whether charged against the $48M TIF cap (§§4.1, 4.3 notes).
8. **Equity demonstration deadline.** April 19, 2025 (Resolution) vs. 60 days after Agreement approval (Term Sheet) (§13.2 note).
9. **TCE contamination** on Parcel 016 not described in the Term Sheet (BTEX only) (§5.2 note).
10. **Insurance limits** — CGL aggregate $20M (Term Sheet) vs. $25M (Loan), plus additional coverages (§6.5 note).

The full set of 54 items is captured in the consolidated schedule at the end of the document.
