## Scope and method

## Motion to dismiss — argument map (all five counts)
- **Count V (DTPA)** — lead with the dispositive statutory exemption: Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.49(f) exempts transactions with total consideration > $500,000 unless the consumer is an individual. Arcadia is an LLC; total consideration is $17,050,000. Alternatives: not a "consumer" (*Riverside Nat'l Bank*); puffery; no producing cause (*Melody Home*).
- **Count IV (Unjust Enrichment)** — barred because an express contract (the MSLSA) governs the same subject matter (*Fortune Production*; *Excess Underwriters*). Arcadia sues *on* the contract in Count I and never alleges it is void.
- **Count II (Fraud)** — four independent grounds: (1) Rule 9(b) particularity failure — post-hoc reasoning, no "why," conclusory "on information and belief" concealment (*Benchmark*; *Flaherty*); (2) economic loss rule under both Delaware (*Brasby*; *Kuhn*) and Texas (*Sharyland*; *Chapman*) law; (3) puffery (*Pizza Hut*; *Presidio*; *Castrol*) plus the proposal's express disclaimers; (4) the § 12.1 integration/non-reliance clause bars reliance on extra-contractual statements (*Eagle Industries*; *SIGA*; *Kuhn*), and reliance was not justifiable given Arcadia's own due diligence and counsel.
- **Count III (Negligent Misrepresentation)** — no independent duty (*McCamish*); economic loss rule; benefit-of-the-bargain damages not recoverable in tort (*Sloane*); Rule 9(b) applies to claims sounding in fraud; puffery/no justifiable reliance.
- **Count I (Breach of Contract)** — Delaware law governs (§ 12.7). Primary: deemed acceptance under § 5.3 (Go-Live Jan. 15, 2023 → deadline Feb. 14, 2023; March 8 email was 22 days late) (*Simulados*, W.D. Tex.; *Precision Healthcare*); expired 90-day warranty with exclusive remedy (§ 9.1); data-migration breaches misattributed (SOW-1 § 3.2 allocates them to Arcadia/Linden Park). Alternative: consequential/punitive damages barred by § 8.2 and all damages capped by § 8.1 (*Dresser-Rand*; *Kana Software*).

## Cover memo — threshold issues flagged (in priority order)
1. **Subject-matter jurisdiction / complete-diversity defect (highest priority).** The Notice of Removal treated Arcadia as a citizen of only Texas, but the FAC (¶¶ 7–8) and Arcadia's operating agreement show Arcadia is also a citizen of **Delaware** — through Apex Medical Ventures, LP, whose general partner (Apex Medical Ventures GP, Inc.) is a Delaware corporation. Meridian is a citizen of both Delaware and Texas. Complete diversity is destroyed; the Court likely lacks § 1332(a) jurisdiction (non-waivable, *Arbaugh*). This may moot the MTD and requires a deliberate client forum-strategy decision (federal MTD vs. state-court remand).
2. Post-answer posture — Rule 12(b)(6) vs. 12(c) (defense preserved in the Answer; same standard).
3. Choice of law — Delaware (contract) vs. Texas (tort); MTD argues the tort claims fail under either.
4. DTPA — verify the exact current text of § 17.49(f); confirm the $25M-asset alternative with Arcadia's financials.
5. DTPA — possible noncompliance with the 60-day pre-suit notice (§ 17.505(a)): demand dated June 15, 2023; suit filed Aug. 7, 2023 (53 days). Remedy is abatement, not dismissal, so not a primary MTD ground.
6. § 8.1 liability cap — compute the exact amount from Meridian's billing records.
7. Count I strategy — full dismissal vs. damages limitation (dual-track framing hedges the risk).
8. Jury-trial waiver (§ 12.9) — confirm enforceability (affects trial/forum value).
9. Documents reviewable on the motion — kept within the FAC + documents referenced in and central to it (*Lormand*); avoided reliance on the Schreiber memo/negotiation emails/support-ticket report as primary authority to prevent Rule 12(d) conversion.
10. Filing mechanics — page limits, separate proposed order, certificate of conference, briefing schedule.
