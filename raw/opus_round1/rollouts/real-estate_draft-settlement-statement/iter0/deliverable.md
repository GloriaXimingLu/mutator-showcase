## 3. Discrepancies Identified Across Closing Documents

The following inconsistencies appear among the closing documents and should be reconciled or noted for the file. None is expected to delay closing, but each should be confirmed.

### D-1. Title Commitment Number Mismatch
The Purchase and Sale Agreement (§5.1) references Title Commitment **No. PCT-2025-08814**, dated April 30, 2025. The actual title commitment delivered for closing bears **Commitment No. TC-2025-07182**, effective June 18, 2025. The settlement statement uses the operative commitment (TC-2025-07182). *Recommend confirming with the Title Company that the April 30 commitment was superseded and that TC-2025-07182 is the controlling commitment.*

### D-2. Title Insurer Name Inconsistency
The title commitment header states it is issued on behalf of **"Continental Fidelity Title Insurance Company,"** but the body text and Conditions and Stipulations repeatedly refer to **"Continental Hartleigh Title Insurance Company."** The countersignature block again uses "Continental Fidelity." *Recommend confirming the correct legal name of the underwriter before the policies are issued, as this affects the policy face and Schedule A.*

### D-3. Conflicting Party Addresses
Several parties are shown at more than one address across the documents:
- **Pinnacle Abstract & Title LLC** appears at 88 Field Point Road, Greenwich, CT 06830 (PSA; title commitment); at 3100 Main Street, Suite 204, Bridgeport, CT 06604 (lien payoff cover letter); and at 1200 Post Road East, Suite 214, Westport, CT 06880 (tax lien statement request).
- **Buyer's Counsel (Ridgeline Law Group PLLC / Nora F. Kapadia)** appears at 915 Chapel Street, Suite 400, New Haven, CT 06510 (PSA; title commitment) and at 55 Railroad Avenue, Suite 300, Greenwich, CT 06830 (lien payoff cover letter).
- **Seller's Counsel (Ashford, Clement & Paige LLP)** appears at 200 Atlantic Street, 14th Floor, Stamford, CT 06901 (PSA) with phone (203) 325-6600, but the lien payoff letters and probate certificate use (203) 547-8100 / (203) 555-4100.

*Recommend confirming the correct service/mailing addresses for wire confirmations and post-closing deliveries.*

### D-5. Security Deposit Interest Not Quantified
The security deposit schedule credits **principal only ($32,400.00)**. However, the management agreement (§6.1) and the rent roll both state that residential deposits are held in an **interest-bearing** escrow account at Harborstone Federal Credit Union "per Connecticut law" (CT §47a-21, which generally requires payment of interest on residential security deposits). The amount of accrued interest is not stated in any delivered document. *The accrued residential deposit interest should be calculated by Bayshore Management Co. / Harborstone FCU and added to the $32,400.00 credit to Buyer (and corresponding debit to Seller) at or before closing. The settlement statement currently reflects principal only.*

### D-6. Earnest Money Interest Not Quantified
PSA §3.2(d) provides that the earnest money is held in an interest-bearing account and that all interest is credited to Buyer at closing. The accrued interest on the $195,000.00 (held since April 24 and May 22, 2025) is **not stated** in any document. *The Escrow Agent should confirm the accrued interest and add it as a credit to Buyer (reducing Buyer's cash required to close). The settlement statement currently reflects $195,000.00 principal only.*

### D-7. Gas / Electric Utility Accounts Not Addressed
PSA §7.5 requires proration of "water, sewer, gas, electric, and any other municipal services." Only the water/sewer (WPCA) account statement was provided. The building's gas-fired water heater and any common-area electric are not addressed. *Confirm whether gas/electric are on tenant-paid accounts or master-metered accounts requiring proration or transfer. If master-metered, final readings and prorations should be obtained and added to the statement.*

### D-8. Water/Sewer Treatment — Contract vs. Payoff Statement
PSA §7.5(a) states that where a utility billing period ends on or before the Closing Date, "the full unpaid balance shall be credited to Buyer on the settlement statement." The WPCA billing period (May 15 – July 14, 2025) ends July 14, one day before closing, so the entire $1,847.60 is Seller's. The City of Bridgeport Tax Status Certificate, however, includes this $1,847.60 in the "Total Due to City of Bridgeport" ($30,450.40) to be **paid at closing from Seller's proceeds**. The settlement statement treats the $1,847.60 as a **Seller charge paid to the City at closing** (consistent with the tax/payoff certificate), which fully protects Buyer (Buyer bears $0). *If the parties prefer the contract's literal "credit to Buyer" treatment (Buyer pays the August 1 bill and is reimbursed via credit), the entry can be reclassified without changing the net result; the Seller's proceeds are reduced by $1,847.60 either way.*

### D-9. First-Mortgage Payoff Includes Escrow Shortage and Release Fee
The Harborstone first-mortgage payoff of $687,412.33 includes an **escrow shortage/deficiency of $539.00** and a **release/reconveyance preparation fee of $75.00**. These are embedded in the payoff total and are paid from Seller's proceeds. *No separate action required, but note that the release preparation fee is distinct from the $73.00 recording fee charged to Seller for recording the release.*

### D-10. Mechanic's Lien — Settlement vs. Claimed Amount
The Northbridge mechanic's lien was filed for **$37,500.00** but is being settled for **$31,000.00** (a $6,500 reduction). The settlement is contingent on the closing occurring by August 31, 2025 (Northbridge letter). *Ensure the signed Release of Mechanic's Lien in recordable form is delivered at closing simultaneously with the $31,000.00 disbursement.*

## 6. Survival and Reproration

- **Prorations survive closing for 90 days** for reproration and final settlement (PSA §7.7, §15.1). Items most likely to require reproration: (a) FY2025–2026 property tax (when the actual bill issues); (b) any utility charges not finally determined at closing; (c) security deposit interest and earnest money interest if not finalized at closing.
- **Reproration mechanics:** Any amounts owed upon reproration are payable within 10 business days of demand with reasonable supporting documentation (PSA §7.7).
- **Repair Escrow:** Buyer has 12 months from closing to complete roof repairs and submit paid invoices with lien waivers for reimbursement; any unused balance (plus interest) returns to Seller thereafter (PSA §8.4(d)–(e)).
- **Representations and warranties** survive closing for 12 months (PSA §15.1).
