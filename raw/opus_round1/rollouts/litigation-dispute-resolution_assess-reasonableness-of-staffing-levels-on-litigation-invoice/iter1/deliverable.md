5. **Over-staffed internal meeting and deposition (§ 4.3).** Five timekeepers billed a single May 8 strategy call (cap is three) and three attorneys billed the Liu deposition without advance approval for the third. **Recommended: $3,312.50.**

6. **Non-reimbursable travel expense (§§ 6.4, 8.2, 8.3).** $2,100.00 in "travel" for two firm attorneys to attend a deposition held at the Firm's own Atlanta office is local travel and was not pre-approved. **Recommended: $2,100.00.**

7. **Budget overage without pre-approval (§ 7.3).** Attorney fees of $193,372.50 exceed the 115% threshold ($189,750.00) with no pre-approval. The violation is flagged; because the specific reductions above bring fees below the cap, no *additional* dollar reduction is required under § 7.3, though Pinnacle reserves the right to enforce the cap.

8. **Missing budget-to-actual reconciliation (§§ 7.2, 9.1(g)).** The invoice omits the required budget-to-actual reconciliation and variance narrative. The Firm should resubmit with the reconciliation.

The detailed findings, citations, and calculations supporting each reduction follow.

## V. Detailed Findings

### A. Unapproved Timekeepers (Billing Guidelines § 4.2; Staffing Plan §§ 2.3, 2.4, 4)

**Standard.** § 4.2 requires that "[a]ny additions to, or substitutions on, the Approved Staffing Plan … must be pre-approved in writing by Pinnacle's Designated In-House Counsel prior to any such timekeeper performing billable work." Staffing changes submitted after the fact "are subject to reduction or rejection of all fees attributable to the unapproved timekeeper, at Pinnacle's sole discretion." The Staffing Plan (§ 4) reiterates that the listed timekeepers are the "complete and exclusive" roster and that any new timekeeper requires "prior written approval." The Staffing Plan further provides that "only one junior associate will be assigned to and authorized to bill on this matter at any given time," and that James Pettersson "is the only mid-level associate assigned to this matter. No other mid-level associate is included on the approved staffing roster."

#### 1. Nina Barros — Mid-Level Associate (45.0 hrs / $21,375.00) — Full Rejection

Barros is not on the Approved Staffing Plan. The Staffing Plan expressly states that Pettersson is the only authorized mid-level associate. On April 28, 2025, Karen Cho emailed Rachel Dominguez requesting Barros's addition. Rachel Dominguez's reply of May 1, 2025 stated: *"Before we can move forward on adding Nina Barros, though, let me review this with Sandra and get back to you. … I'll try to circle back with you early next week."* No further approval was given. The Firm's own Rate Schedule on the invoice concedes the status: "Nina Barros … **Pending Approval — Added May 2025** … client response pending."

The Firm nonetheless billed Barros for 45.0 hours ($21,375.00) throughout May. The Status Report's assertion that Barros's involvement "was consistent with the request submitted to Ms. Dominguez on April 28, 2025" misstates the standard: a *request* is not *approval*. § 4.2 is unambiguous that "the absence of a response shall not constitute approval." No exigent-circumstance notification under § 4.2 was made (the request was a routine staffing addition, and Pinnacle expressly asked for time to review). The Firm proceeded at its own risk.

**Recommendation:** Reject all Barros fees in full — **$21,375.00**. Pinnacle may, in its discretion under § 4.2, consider retroactive approval for a portion if the Firm submits a written justification, but as billed the fees are not payable.

#### 2. Priya Sengupta — Junior Associate (27.5 hrs / $10,312.50) — Full Rejection

Sengupta is not on the Approved Staffing Plan. The Staffing Plan authorizes a single junior-associate slot, filled by Tyler Webb (2nd year), and states that assigning "more than one junior associate concurrently would constitute a staffing change requiring prior written approval under § 4.2." Sengupta (1st year) was added in May and billed concurrently with Webb for 27.5 hours ($10,312.50). Unlike Barros, there is **no record of any staffing-change request to Pinnacle** for Sengupta — she was simply "added May 2025" (per the invoice Rate Schedule) with no request, no justification, and no approval. This is a more straightforward violation than Barros: no request was even submitted.

**Recommendation:** Reject all Sengupta fees in full — **$10,312.50**.

**Combined unapproved-timekeeper reduction: $31,687.50.** (Note: several Barros and Sengupta entries are also block-billed; the full rejection for unapproved status supersedes, and no separate block-billing reduction is applied to those entries to avoid double-counting.)

The Guideline's own examples treat "confer with partner regarding strategy" and "prepare post-deposition summary" as discrete tasks separate from research, drafting, or deposition attendance. The remedy is "an automatic twenty-five percent (25%) reduction to the total fees of each block-billed entry," which "is in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other reductions." The Firm may avoid the reduction by resubmitting "as separate line items with individually allocated time within fifteen (15) days."

### D. Task/Timekeeper Appropriateness (§ 6.1)

**Standard.** § 6.1 requires that each task be assigned to "the lowest-cost timekeeper capable of competently performing the work." Document review and cite-checking by partners or senior associates are "presumptively inappropriate" and subject to reduction to the appropriate lower-level rate (§ 6.1(a), (b)). Routine first-level document review by a junior associate is reduced to the contract attorney rate (§ 6.1(a)). Administrative tasks (scheduling, coordinating with court reporters, calendaring) are "presumptively inappropriate for any attorney" and are to be written off or reduced to the paralegal rate (§ 6.1(g)–(k); § 2).

#### 1. Entry 9 — Partner document review (Hargrove, 4.5 hrs / $4,432.50)

"Review and analyze documents produced by MedCore in third production batch; prepare detailed memorandum summarizing key findings for client." This is document review by the lead partner. § 6.1(a) directs that document review by a partner be "reduced to the contract attorney rate ($200/hr cap) or the junior associate rate, depending on the nature of the review and whether the exercise of legal judgment was required." The Status Report indicates the review involved legal judgment (calibrating the review protocol and identifying key documents for trade-secret claims), so reduction to the **junior associate rate ($375/hr)** is appropriate rather than the contract-attorney rate.

**Calculation:** 4.5 × ($985 − $375) = **$2,745.00.**

#### 2. Entry 43 — Partner cite-checking (Hargrove, 2.0 hrs / $1,970.00)

"Review and revise cite-check memorandum prepared by T. Webb; finalize citations for motion to compel brief." Cite-checking is a Routine Task (§ 2) and is "presumptively inappropriate" for a partner (§ 6.1(b)), to be reduced to the junior associate rate. (Junior associates Webb and Sengupta performed the underlying cite-checking on this same brief — Entries 47 and 54 — confirming this is junior-level work.)

**Calculation:** 2.0 × ($985 − $375) = **$1,220.00.**

#### 3. Entry 15 — Administrative tasks by an attorney (Pettersson, 1.5 hrs / $712.50)

"Schedule depositions of three remaining witnesses; coordinate court reporter availability; confirm deposition locations." This is entirely administrative — scheduling (§ 6.1(g)), coordinating with a court reporter (§ 6.1(h)), and confirming locations (§ 6.1(k)). Administrative tasks "are considered overhead expenses of Outside Counsel and shall not be billed to Pinnacle at any rate." The entry should be written off in full (Pinnacle's discretion under § 6.1 to reduce to the $175/hr paralegal rate would yield $262.50; full write-off is recommended).

**Recommendation: $712.50** (full write-off).

#### 4. Entry 79 — Junior associate routine document review (Webb, 3.5 hrs / $1,312.50)

"Review third batch of MedCore production documents for relevance and privilege." This is routine first-level document review. § 6.1(a) provides that "[d]ocument review by a junior associate that constitutes routine first-level review for relevance or responsiveness → reduced to the contract attorney rate." The Status Report confirms Webb "assisted with document review" on days when volume exceeded the contract attorney's capacity — i.e., routine first-level review. Reduce to the contract attorney rate ($200/hr cap).

**Calculation:** 3.5 × ($375 − $200) = **$612.50.**

**Combined § 6.1 reduction: $5,290.00.**

### E. Conference Attendance and Deposition Staffing (§ 4.3)

**Standard.** § 4.3 limits billing timekeepers for "any single internal meeting or conference call" to **three (3)**, even for Substantive Case-Strategy Sessions; excess attendees must record time as non-billable. For depositions, two billing attorneys are permitted without approval; a third requires **advance approval** requested "no later than five (5) business days before the scheduled deposition," and "[i]n the absence of such advance approval, Pinnacle reserves the right to reduce the time of the additional timekeepers in their entirety."

#### 1. May 8 team strategy call — five billing timekeepers (excess of two)

Five timekeepers billed the same May 8 strategy call: Hargrove (3.0, Entry 22), Cho (3.0, Entry 23), Pettersson (3.0, Entry 24), Barros (2.5, Entry 25), and Webb (2.5, Entry 26). The cap is three. Two attendees are excess. Barros is already fully rejected (§ A.1). Of the four *approved* timekeepers who billed, one is excess. No advance designation of billing attendees was made. Consistent with the "lowest-cost timekeeper" principle, the three most senior/essential attendees (Hargrove, Cho, Pettersson) are retained, and the most junior excess attendee — **Webb (2.5 hrs / $937.50, Entry 26)** — is disallowed.

**Recommendation: $937.50.**

#### 2. Dr. Rebecca Liu deposition (May 19) — three billing attorneys without approval for the third

Three attorneys billed the Liu deposition: Hargrove (4.0, Entry 60), Cho (6.5, Entry 61), and Pettersson (5.0, Entry 62). Two deposition attorneys are permitted without approval; a third requires advance approval five business days before the deposition. No such approval was requested or given. Per the Status Report, Cho conducted the direct examination and Hargrove attended to observe and provide real-time strategic guidance — the two primary attorneys. Pettersson attended "to manage the exhibit presentation, take detailed notes, and prepare a comprehensive testimony summary," i.e., the additional (third) timekeeper. § 4.3 permits Pinnacle to "reduce the time of the additional timekeepers in their entirety."

Pettersson's Entry 62 ("Attend deposition of Dr. Rebecca Liu; prepare summary of testimony and identify key admissions …") is predominantly deposition-attendance time and is block-billed so that the attendance portion cannot be isolated. Because Pettersson is the unapproved third deposition attorney and the entry is chiefly deposition attendance, the entry is reduced in its entirety. (The Firm may seek retroactive approval under § 4.2/§ 10.4 or resubmit any genuinely separate post-deposition summary time as a discrete entry for reconsideration; no block-billing reduction is separately applied to Entry 62 to avoid double-counting.)

**Calculation:** 5.0 × $475 = **$2,375.00.**

**Combined § 4.3 reduction: $3,312.50.**

### F. Monthly Budget Overage Without Pre-Approval (§ 7.3)

**Standard.** § 7.3 requires pre-approval if monthly attorney fees are projected to exceed the high end of the approved range by more than 15%. The Staffing Plan (§ 3) set the approved range at **$95,000–$165,000**, so the 115% ceiling is **$165,000 × 1.15 = $189,750.00**. Fees exceeding $189,750 without pre-approval may be reduced to that ceiling.

**Finding.** As-billed attorney fees total **$193,372.50**, which exceeds the $189,750.00 ceiling by **$3,622.50**. No pre-approval was sought or given (the only staffing/budget communication in the record is the Barros email, in which Ms. Dominguez stated she wanted to "check where things stand against the current budget and staffing plan"). This is a § 7.3 violation.

**Treatment.** § 7.3 expressly provides that budget-compliance reductions "do not offset or preclude" the other Guidelines reductions. The specific reductions in Sections A–E above reduce payable attorney fees to **$123,032.49**, which is *below* the $189,750.00 ceiling. Accordingly, the § 7.3 cap is not the binding constraint, and **no additional dollar reduction is required under § 7.3** at this time. Pinnacle nonetheless **reserves the right to enforce the $189,750.00 ceiling**, and the violation should be documented and addressed: the Firm must obtain pre-approval before any future month exceeds the ceiling and should consider submitting a phase-budget adjustment request under § 7.4 if the elevated discovery volume is expected to persist.

#### Expense #2 — Travel ($2,100.00) — Disallowed

The entry seeks "Travel expenses for D. Hargrove and K. Cho to attend Dr. Rebecca Liu deposition — mileage, parking, meals." The Status Report states the Liu deposition "was taken at the offices of Hargrove & Linden LLP in Atlanta, Georgia." Hargrove and Cho are H&L attorneys based at that Atlanta office. Travel to one's own office is local travel. The expense fails on three independent grounds:

1. **Local travel (§ 6.4):** "Local travel within the Atlanta metropolitan area is not billable under any circumstances."
2. **No pre-approval (§ 8.3):** Aggregate travel exceeding $1,000 "must be pre-approved by the Designated In-House Counsel." $2,100 exceeds $1,000; no pre-approval is evidenced.
