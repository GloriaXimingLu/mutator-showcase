       Our Company  
      Crestline Software, Inc. is an enterprise software-as-a-service (    “    SaaS    ”    ) company providing an AI-driven supply chain optimization platform to Fortune 1000 manufacturers and retailers. We were incorporated in the State of Delaware on March 14, 2017. Our principal executive offices are located at 4200 Innovation Drive, Suite 800, Austin, Texas 78759. We operate through our wholly-owned subsidiary, Crestline Software UK Ltd., incorporated in England and Wales, which supports our European operations, and we maintain offices in San Francisco, California; London, United Kingdom; and Bangalore, India.  
      Our platform consists of the Crestline Nexus unified SaaS platform and the proprietary SupplyMind AI engine, which combines machine learning, advanced statistical modeling, and real-time data integration to deliver predictive and prescriptive supply chain intelligence, including demand forecasting, inventory optimization, and logistics routing. As of December 31, 2024, we served 487 customers, including 34 customers contributing annual recurring revenue (    “    ARR    ”    ) in excess of $1.0 million, and had 1,247 employees. Our intellectual property portfolio includes 47 issued U.S. patents, 12 pending U.S. patent applications, and 8 issued foreign patents. We achieved SOC 2 Type II certification in August 2024.

         RISK FACTORS  
      An investment in our Class A common stock involves a high degree of risk. Before investing, you should carefully consider the risks and uncertainties described below, together with all of the other information contained in this prospectus. The occurrence of any of the following risks could materially and adversely affect our business, financial condition, results of operations, and prospects, and the trading price of our Class A common stock could decline, and you could lose all or part of your investment.  
       Risks Related to Our Business and Industry  
          We have a history of net losses, may not achieve or sustain profitability, and may not generate sufficient cash to service our indebtedness.         We have incurred net losses since inception. We reported a net loss of $24.8 million and $27.4 million for the fiscal years ended December 31, 2024 and 2023, respectively. As of December 31, 2024, we had an accumulated deficit of $312.6 million. We may continue to incur net losses for the foreseeable future, and we may not achieve or sustain profitability.  
          Our professional services operations are currently margin-negative.         For fiscal year 2024, our professional services revenue was $18.7 million and our professional services cost of revenue was $18.8 million, resulting in a negative professional services gross margin of approximately (0.5)%. Although we expect professional services gross margins to improve as implementation processes scale and standardize, there can be no assurance that professional services will become profitable or that we will achieve our target professional services gross margins of 15% to 20%.  
          We depend on a limited number of customers for a significant portion of our revenue.         Our top 10 customers represented approximately 31.7% of our total revenue in fiscal year 2024, and our largest customer, Harmon Industries Inc., represented approximately 8.2% of our total revenue. The loss of, or a material reduction in purchases by, one or more of these customers could materially and adversely affect our revenue and results of operations.  
          We face intense competition and may lack sufficient resources to remain competitive.         The market for supply chain optimization software is competitive, with well-capitalized incumbents and point-solution vendors. We may not be able to compete effectively.  
          We are subject to intellectual property litigation that could materially and adversely affect our business.         On September 12, 2024, Optivance, Inc. filed a patent infringement lawsuit against us in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Texas (Case No. 6:24-cv-00831), alleging that certain features of our SupplyMind platform infringe U.S. Patent No. 11,284,107, relating to         “    machine-learning-based inventory forecasting.    ”         We believe the claims are without merit and intend to vigorously defend against them. However, an adverse judgment could result in damages that our outside patent counsel currently estimate could range from $5.0 million to $20.0 million, and could result in injunctive relief requiring us to redesign or cease offering certain features. In addition, an adverse judgment in excess of $5.0 million that remains undischarged for 60 days could constitute an event of default under our term loan credit agreement, which could result in the acceleration of our indebtedness. See         “    Risk Factors—Risks Related to Our Indebtedness    ”         and         “    Description of Indebtedness.    ”  
          Our intellectual property is pledged as collateral under our credit agreement.         Substantially all of our assets, including our intellectual property (consisting of our 47 issued U.S. patents, 12 pending U.S. patent applications, 8 issued foreign patents, and our registered trademarks         “    Crestline,    ”         “    Crestline Nexus,    ”         and         “    SupplyMind    ”    ), are pledged as collateral to secure our obligations under our term loan. In the event of a default, the lender could foreclose on our intellectual property, which would severely and adversely affect our business.  
          We depend on key personnel, including our co-founders.         Our success depends on the continued service of our co-founders, Arun Ramaswamy, our Chief Executive Officer, and Katerina [●] Litvak, our Chief Technology Officer. The loss of the services of either co-founder could adversely affect our business. In addition, the departure of Mr. Ramaswamy as Chief Executive Officer could constitute a         “    change of control    ”         under our term loan credit agreement unless a successor reasonably acceptable to the lender is appointed within 90 days, which could result in the acceleration of our indebtedness. See         “    Description of Indebtedness.    ”  
          If we fail to maintain effective internal control over financial reporting, we may not be able to accurately report our financial results.         As an emerging growth company, we are not required to obtain an attestation from our independent registered public accounting firm on our internal control over financial reporting. If we fail to maintain effective internal controls, we could be subject to regulatory scrutiny and a loss of investor confidence.  
          Our ability to use our net operating loss carryforwards and certain other tax attributes may be limited.         As of December 31, 2024, we had approximately $228.0 million of federal net operating loss (    “    NOL    ”    ) carryforwards and approximately $166.0 million of state NOL carryforwards. Our ability to use these NOLs may be limited if we experience an         “    ownership change    ”         as defined in Section 382 of the Internal Revenue Code, including in connection with this offering or other equity transactions. We have not yet completed a Section 382 study.  
          We have identified a related party customer relationship that requires ongoing disclosure.         Vantara Logistics, Inc., a portfolio company of Graymont Ventures (our largest pre-offering stockholder), is a customer under a SaaS license agreement entered into in April 2023 with an annual contract value of $1.2 million and a three-year term.         Risks Related to Our Class A Common Stock and This Offering  
          Because of the ten-to-one voting ratio of our Class B common stock, our co-founders will control a majority of the combined voting power of our capital stock and will be able to control all matters submitted to a vote of our stockholders.         Our co-founders will hold all of our outstanding Class B common stock, representing approximately 71.8% of the combined voting power of our outstanding capital stock immediately following this offering. As a result, our co-founders will have the ability to control the outcome of all matters submitted to a vote of our stockholders, including the election of directors, amendments to our certificate of incorporation, and approval of significant corporate transactions. This concentrated voting control will limit the ability of holders of our Class A common stock to influence corporate matters.  
          Our Class B common stock has no sunset provision.         Our Class B common stock will continue to have ten votes per share indefinitely, and will convert into Class A common stock only upon transfer to a non-permitted transferee or upon the death or incapacity of the holder. There is no time-based or ownership-based sunset. As a result, the concentration of voting control with our co-founders will continue indefinitely. Certain major stock index providers may exclude companies with multi-class voting structures from their indices, which could limit the demand for, and the market price of, our Class A common stock.  
          We are a                 “        controlled company        ”                 under Nasdaq listing rules and may rely on exemptions from certain corporate governance requirements.         Because our co-founders will control more than 50% of the voting power of our outstanding capital stock, we will be a         “    controlled company    ”         under Nasdaq listing rules. As a controlled company, we may elect to rely on exemptions from the Nasdaq requirements that a majority of our board of directors be comprised of independent directors, that we have a fully independent compensation committee, and that we have a fully independent nominating committee. As of the date of this prospectus, our Board of Directors consists of [●] members, [●] of whom are independent. If we rely on these exemptions, you will not have the same protections afforded to stockholders of companies that are subject to these corporate governance requirements.  
          Our amended and restated certificate of incorporation and bylaws contain provisions that may discourage acquisition proposals and limit the ability of stockholders to influence corporate matters.         These provisions include a classified (staggered) board of directors, the ability of the Board of Directors to fill vacancies, the elimination of the ability of holders of Class A common stock to act by written consent, limitations on the ability of stockholders to call special meetings, advance notice requirements for stockholder proposals and nominations, a requirement of a 66⅔% supermajority vote to amend certain provisions of our charter and bylaws, and director removal only for cause and upon a 66⅔% vote.  
          Our exclusive forum provisions may limit the ability of stockholders to bring claims in certain judicial forums.         Our amended and restated certificate of incorporation provides that, unless we consent in writing to the selection of an alternative forum, the Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware will be the sole and exclusive forum for certain state-law claims, and the federal district courts of the United States will be the sole and exclusive forum for claims arising under the Securities Act. These provisions may limit the ability of stockholders to bring claims in state court or in the courts of their choosing.

Events of default include cross-default to other indebtedness exceeding $2.0 million and judgments for the payment of money in excess of $5.0 million that remain undischarged for 60 days.          BUSINESS  
       Overview  
      Crestline Software, Inc. is an enterprise SaaS company providing an AI-driven supply chain optimization platform to Fortune 1000 manufacturers and retailers. Our platform, Crestline Nexus, is powered by the proprietary SupplyMind AI engine, which combines machine learning, advanced statistical modeling, and real-time data integration to deliver predictive and prescriptive supply chain intelligence.  
        Our Products  
          Crestline Nexus Platform.         A unified SaaS platform for end-to-end supply chain optimization, with real-time data ingestion from ERP, WMS, TMS, and IoT systems, pre-built connectors for over 100 enterprise data sources, and a multi-tenant, cloud-native architecture.  
          SupplyMind AI Engine.         Proprietary machine learning models for demand forecasting, inventory optimization, and logistics routing, protected by our patent portfolio.  
        Market Opportunity  
      We estimate the total addressable market for AI-driven supply chain software to be approximately $28 billion by 2028, with a serviceable addressable market of approximately $9 billion focused on Fortune 1000 manufacturers and retailers. We believe current penetration is less than 3% of the serviceable addressable market.  
        Customers  
      As of December 31, 2024, we served 487 customers across manufacturing, retail, logistics, consumer packaged goods, and other industries. Our top 10 customers represented approximately 31.7% of fiscal year 2024 revenue, and our largest customer represented approximately 8.2% of fiscal year 2024 revenue. No single customer accounted for more than 10% of total revenue in fiscal year 2024 or 2023. Our dollar-based net retention rate was 132% for fiscal year 2024.  
        Intellectual Property  
      As of December 31, 2024, our intellectual property portfolio consisted of 47 issued U.S. patents, 12 pending U.S. patent applications, and 8 issued foreign patents. Our material trademarks include         “    Crestline,    ”         “    Crestline Nexus,    ”         and         “    SupplyMind,    ”         each registered with the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office. We achieved SOC 2 Type II certification in August 2024. We are subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) through our UK subsidiary and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).  
        Employees  
      As of December 31, 2024, we had 1,247 employees.  
        Properties  
      We lease our principal executive offices in Austin, Texas (our headquarters, expiring August 2029, with annual base rent of approximately $3.8 million), and additional offices in San Francisco, California (expiring March 2027, annual base rent approximately $2.1 million); London, United Kingdom (expiring January 2028, annual base rent approximately £680,000); and Bangalore, India (expiring June 2026, annual base rent approximately INR 42,000 thousand).  
        Legal Proceedings  
      On September 12, 2024, Optivance, Inc. filed a patent infringement lawsuit against us in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Texas (Case No. 6:24-cv-00831), alleging that certain features of our SupplyMind platform infringe U.S. Patent No. 11,284,107, relating to         “    machine-learning-based inventory forecasting.    ”         We believe the claims are without merit and intend to vigorously defend against them. The lawsuit is in the early stages, and no trial date has been set. We have not recorded a loss contingency for this matter because management believes it is not probable that a loss has been incurred. Based on the advice of outside patent counsel, we estimate the potential range of loss, in the event of an adverse judgment, to be between $5.0 million and $20.0 million, although an unfavorable outcome is considered to have a low probability. An adverse judgment in excess of $5.0 million that remains undischarged for 60 days could constitute an event of default under our term loan credit agreement.

       PURPOSE AND SCOPE  
      This memorandum flags inconsistencies, internal arithmetic errors, cross-document conflicts, and disclosure risks identified during a review of the document set assembled in connection with Crestline Software, Inc.’s proposed initial public offering of Class A common stock. The documents reviewed are: (1) the audited consolidated financial statements for fiscal years 2023–2024 (Archer Pryce LLP); (2) the CFO IPO readiness memorandum dated February 14, 2025; (3) the amended and restated certificate of incorporation and bylaws; (4) the term loan summary (Kestridge West Credit Partners); (5) the underwriting term sheet and lock-up; (6) the director and officer questionnaire responses (Thorncroft & Wexler LLP); (7) the equity plan summary and grants workbook; and (8) the draft IPO roadshow presentation.  
      Each finding below states the issue, the specific evidence (with exact figures and the document in which it appears), the consequence for the registration statement or the offering, and a recommended action. Findings are grouped by category and numbered consecutively. A prioritized action list appears at the end. This memorandum is intended to be read together with the draft Form S-1 and does not itself constitute legal advice on any single item; several items require confirmation against primary records (stock ledger, credit agreement, equity plan records, and 409A valuation reports) before the registration statement is filed.  
      
        EXECUTIVE SUMMARY  
      The record supports filing a Form S-1, but it contains a substantial number of inconsistencies that must be reconciled before filing. The most serious are:  
              The headline non-GAAP metric is arithmetically wrong.         Adjusted EBITDA is stated as $22.8 million in the CFO memo and the roadshow, but the stated add-backs sum to $20.5 million. The FY2023 reconciliation line items in the CFO memo also do not match the audited financial statements. Presenting an incorrect non-GAAP reconciliation risks a Regulation G / Item 10(e) violation and an SEC comment letter.  
              The term loan is described inconsistently across every document that touches it         — closing date (December 2022 vs. June 2023), draw amounts, amortization schedule, and even the lender’s name (    “    Kestridge West Credit Partners    ”         vs.     “    Silicon Valley Credit Partners    ”    ).  
              The cash flow statement does not tie to the balance sheet         (beginning cash for FY2024 is $78.4 million but the prior year-end balance sheet shows $68.5 million), and FY2023 short-term investment purchases of $215 million cannot reconcile to the $38.5 million balance.  
              The co-founder’s legal name is inconsistent         (    “    Katerina Litvak    ”         in the charter, bylaws, and underwriting term sheet vs.     “    Katerina Litvanova    ”         in the CFO memo and D&O questionnaires), and it is unclear whether she is a director (charter says 7 directors including her; the D&O questionnaire says 6 directors excluding her).  
              The equity plan records do not reconcile to the audited financial statements         (option and RSU grants, exercises, forfeitures, and the 409A valuation history all conflict), and the equity plan ledger contains wrong director names and wrong officer titles.  
              A related-party customer (Vantara Logistics) is presented as an ordinary customer in the roadshow and CFO memo         without the required Item 404 disclosure, and the roadshow repeatedly mischaracterizes professional services as         “    high-margin    ”         when the audited statements show a negative professional services gross margin.  
      Each of these, and the additional findings below, should be resolved and reflected consistently in the S-1, the roadshow, and all offering documents.  
      
        I. FINANCIAL STATEMENT AND NON-GAAP INCONSISTENCIES  
       Finding 1 — Adjusted EBITDA is arithmetically incorrect ($22.8M stated; $20.5M correct)  
          Issue.         The Adjusted EBITDA figure of $22.8 million for FY2024 does not equal the sum of the reconciliation line items as presented.  
          Evidence.         The CFO readiness memorandum (Section 5.2) and the roadshow (Slides 3, 23, 34, and 35) each present the following bridge: Net loss $(24.8M) + D&A $8.7M + Stock-based compensation $38.2M + Interest expense $3.1M − Interest income $6.2M + Income tax provision $0.3M + Restructuring charges $1.2M. The sum of these items is $20.5 million, not $22.8 million. The roadshow’s own drafting notes acknowledge this error (Slide 23 notes:         “    Performing the arithmetic: −$24.8 + $8.7 + $38.2 + (−$3.1) + $0.3 + $1.2 = $20.5 million, NOT $22.8 million. The $2.3M discrepancy is unexplained.    ”    ). The stated Adjusted EBITDA margin of 12.2% is likewise incorrect; $20.5M / $187.3M = 10.9%.  
          Consequence.         The individual FY2024 add-backs match the audited financial statements, so the error is in the total only. Nonetheless, presenting an incorrect non-GAAP reconciliation in the S-1 would violate Regulation G and Item 10(e) of Regulation S-K and would likely draw an SEC comment letter. The same $22.8M figure is repeated in the roadshow, creating a consistency problem if the S-1 uses the correct $20.5M.  
          Recommendation.         Use $20.5 million (10.9% margin) as Adjusted EBITDA for FY2024 in the S-1 and all offering materials. Correct the roadshow. Confirm whether any additional add-back (e.g., a $2.3M item omitted from the bridge) was intended; if so, disclose it explicitly and reconcile.

       Finding 38 — Vantara Logistics related-party transaction is not consistently disclosed  
          Issue.         The Vantara Logistics customer relationship is a related-party transaction that is disclosed by one director but omitted by the CFO and presented as an ordinary customer in the roadshow and CFO memo.

Audited Note 15 and D&O questionnaire Section 3.1 (Priya Venkatesh): Vantara Logistics is a portfolio company of Graymont Ventures; Ms. Venkatesh (director) is Graymont’s Managing Partner and sits on Vantara’s board; SaaS agreement April 2023, $1.2M/year, three-year term, $3.6M total; Audit Committee approved with Ms. Venkatesh recused. D&O questionnaire Section 2.3: the CFO did not identify Vantara as a related-party transaction. D&O questionnaire Open Item 2 and roadshow Slide 14 notes: the CFO memo and roadshow treat Vantara as a standard customer/case study without related-party notation.  
          Consequence.         Item 404 of Regulation S-K requires disclosure of this related-party transaction in the S-1. Presenting Vantara as an ordinary customer case study in the roadshow (Slide 14) without the related-party disclosure is misleading and inconsistent with the S-1.  
          Recommendation.         Disclose the Vantara transaction under Item 404 in the S-1 with full detail (Graymont affiliation, Ms. Venkatesh’s dual board role, Audit Committee approval and recusal, arm’s-length determination). Remove or annotate the Vantara case study in the roadshow. Ensure all investor-facing materials consistently identify Vantara as a related party.

        Finding 46 — Emerging growth company status and two-year financial statements

          Consequence.         Two-year audited financials are permissible for an EGC, but the S-1 must disclose EGC status and the accommodations elected (including the Section 404(b) auditor attestation exemption and reduced executive compensation disclosure).  
          Recommendation.         Confirm and disclose EGC status in the S-1. State that the Company is presenting two years of audited financial statements in reliance on EGC accommodations. Disclose the accommodations the Company elects to use.

          Issue.         The audited financial statements (Note 8) do not describe the collateral, the subsidiary guarantee, or the pledge of intellectual property, all of which are material terms of the term loan.  
          Evidence.         Term loan summary Section 4: the term loan is secured by a first-priority lien on substantially all assets, including all intellectual property (the 47 issued U.S. patents, 12 pending applications, 8 foreign patents, and the registered trademarks), equipment, deposit accounts, and 65% of the voting equity of the foreign subsidiary; Crestline Software UK Ltd. provides a limited guarantee capped at £5.0 million.           Issue.         The credit agreement contains a key-person change-of-control trigger and other triggers that should be disclosed.

          Consequence.         An adverse Optivance judgment at or above $5.0 million, if not bonded, appealed, or stayed, could accelerate the $40.0 million term loan. This interplay is a material risk.  
          Recommendation.         Disclose the Optivance litigation and its potential to trigger a credit agreement default in the S-1 risk factors and legal proceedings section. Describe the judgment-default threshold in the description of indebtedness.  
        Finding 53 — 30-day IPO notice covenant

Term loan summary Section 5.4 and Section 13(2): Section 7.14 requires an         “    IPO Notice    ”         not less than 30 days before the IPO, including the anticipated date, gross proceeds, use of proceeds, and a pro forma capitalization table. Failure to deliver is an event of default under Section 8.1(c) (subject to a 10-business-day cure).  
          Recommendation.         Ensure the IPO Notice is delivered to Kestridge West at least 30 days (preferably 35 days) before pricing. Disclose the notice requirement in the S-1’s description of indebtedness and use of proceeds.
