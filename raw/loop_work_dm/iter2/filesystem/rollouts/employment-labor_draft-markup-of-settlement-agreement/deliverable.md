       I. Executive Summary  
      You asked me to review the draft Settlement Agreement and General Release of All Claims (the         “    Draft    ”    ), prepared by Mr. Delano’s counsel (Engel & Associates, P.C.), against Greenleaf’s employment records and the governing company policies referenced in the Draft (principally the Greenleaf 2021 Equity Incentive Plan and the July 1, 2022 RSU Grant Agreement). A redlined markup reflecting the revisions described below is delivered separately as              settlement-agreement-markup.docx    .  
      The Draft is workable as a framework, but it contains several defects that, if signed as written, would (1) likely render the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (    “    ADEA    ”    ) release unenforceable, leaving Greenleaf exposed to Mr. Delano’s age-discrimination claim after paying $525,000; (2) misstate the value of the equity consideration using an outdated valuation; (3) impose a non-competition covenant that is almost certainly void in part and likely unenforceable as a whole; and (4) omit provisions Greenleaf needs (governing law, dispute resolution, insurance coordination, and a cooperation clause). The Draft also understates Greenleaf’s litigation risk: Greenleaf’s own personnel records show that a replacement for Mr. Delano was hired six days after his termination, which directly contradicts the         “    elimination of position    ”         rationale and materially strengthens Mr. Delano’s retaliation and age-discrimination claims.  
      The most important points are:  
             OWBPA defects (critical).         Mr. Delano is 58 (DOB 07/14/1966), so the ADEA waiver must satisfy the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act (    “    OWBPA    ”    ). The Draft gives only             14 days         to consider the agreement (OWBPA requires at least             21         for an individual waiver) and provides             no seven-day revocation period         (OWBPA requires one). As written, the ADEA release is likely not         “    knowing and voluntary    ”         and may be void. The redline extends the period to 21 days, adds a 7-day revocation period, redefines the         “    Effective Date    ”         to occur only after the revocation period expires, and adds the required attorney-advisement.  
             RSU valuation is wrong and misstated (critical).         The Draft values the 5,000 accelerated RSUs at             $10.00/share ($50,000)         and calls that         “    the most recent independent valuation conducted by Ridgepoint Valuation Services.    ”         Greenleaf’s equity records show the most recent 409A valuation is dated             January 15, 2025, at $12.50/share    ; the $10.00 figure is the             July 1, 2024         valuation. The correct value is             5,000 × $12.50 = $62,500    , making the corrected total settlement value             $537,500    , not $525,000 (a             $12,500         understatement). The         “    most recent    ”         statement is a material misrepresentation that must be corrected.  
             Replacement hire undercuts the defense (critical risk).         Greenleaf hired             Raj Mehta         as         “    Senior Director of Supply Chain & Logistics    ”         on             March 20, 2025 — six days after terminating Mr. Delano on March 14, 2025         — at             $185,000         (versus Mr. Delano’s $218,000). This is in Greenleaf’s own HR records and contradicts the stated         “    elimination of position due to restructuring    ”         rationale. It substantially strengthens Mr. Delano’s ORS 654.062(5) whistleblower-retaliation and ADEA claims and makes it essential that the release be enforceable.  
             Non-compete is largely unenforceable.         The 18-month non-compete covers             California         (where non-competes are void), was imposed on an employee             Greenleaf terminated         (an Oregon enforceability problem under ORS 653.295), and contains a tolling clause that could extend it beyond the statutory cap. The redline removes California and Idaho, shortens the term to 12 months, conditions enforceability on ORS 653.295, and removes open-ended tolling. You should consider deleting the non-compete entirely and relying on the non-solicitation covenant.  
             EPLI carrier is not coordinated.         Greenleaf carries EPLI with             Timberline Insurance Group (Policy No. TIG-EPL-2024-00391)    . The Draft does not reference the policy or condition settlement on carrier consent. Settling without carrier coordination can breach the policy and jeopardize coverage. The redline adds an insurance condition; you should confirm policy limits and obtain consent before signing.  
             Back-pay tax allocation is indefensible.         The Draft treats             $125,000         as wages (    “    back pay    ”    ) for the period March 14 – May 5, 2025 (~52 days). At Mr. Delano’s $218,000 base salary, 52 days of base wages is only about             $31,058    ; even adding the unpaid 2024 bonus (target $43,600) yields roughly             $74,658    . The remaining ~$50,000 is non-wage damages mischaracterized as wages, exposing Greenleaf to unnecessary employer FICA and to IRS challenge of the allocation. The redline reduces the wage portion to $75,000 and reallocates $50,000 to non-wage compensatory damages (total unchanged at $537,500 after the RSU correction).  
      The redline also adds carve-outs required by law (DTSA immunity notice; preservation of the right to file agency charges and receive whistleblower awards), narrows the workers’-compensation release, addresses the unresolved 2024 bonus and 401(k), and adds governing-law, dispute-resolution, cooperation, insurance, and notices provisions.
