       I. Executive Summary  
      Respondent Darren Kwan, through counsel Raymond Fossett (Hargrove Fossett & Yates LLP), produced seven (7) document sets in response to Petitioner Melinda Torres-Kwan’s First Request for Production of Documents, Nos. 1–22, transmitted by cover email (undated) and indexed as PROD-001 through PROD-007. The production consists of: (1) Respondent’s Sworn Financial Declaration; (2) excerpts of the parties’ 2023 joint Form 1040; (3) Crestline Wealth Advisors brokerage statements; (4) Fairview Community Bank individual account statements; (5) Meridian Home Lending mortgage statements; (6) Fairview Community Bank Platinum Visa credit card statements; and (7) NovaBridge Solutions LLC expense reimbursement reports. A privilege log identifying four withheld communications (PRIV-001 through PRIV-004) was summarized in the cover letter, with a         “    formal    ”         log promised within ten business days.  
      This review catalogues each exhibit, summarizes the financial figures disclosed, and flags the inconsistencies, omissions, and dissipation indicators that emerge when the sworn declaration is cross-checked against the underlying records. The most significant findings, each developed in Part IV, are:  
             Material understatement of income.         The sworn declaration reports 2023 income of             $359,000         ($185,000 W-2 wages plus             $174,000         labeled         “    K-1 Income / Distributions    ”    ). The $174,000 figure is the             cash distribution         (Schedule K-1, Box 19a), not the K-1 ordinary business income, which was             $367,200         (K-1, Box 1). Adding the interest, dividend, capital-gain, and rental income that the return reports but the declaration omits, Respondent’s actual 2023 income was approximately             $571,740         — an understatement of roughly             $212,740    . The declaration’s sworn statement that Respondent has         “    no other sources of income … None    ”         is directly contradicted by the joint tax return.  
             Undisclosed real property and mortgage.         The declaration states Respondent         “    does not hold any ownership interest … in any other real property    ”         beyond the marital residence. The 2023 tax return (Schedule E) and the produced mortgage statements (PROD-005) show Respondent owns             1035 Elm Street, Unit C, Fairview, Columbia 20147         — a rental condominium titled solely in his name, acquired October 8, 2019 (during the marriage), subject to a Meridian Home Lending mortgage with a principal balance of approximately             $241,281         as of August 1, 2024, generating             $2,100/month         ($25,200/year) in rent. Neither the property, the mortgage, nor the rental income appears in the declaration.  
             $85,000 transfer to an undisclosed account around the date of separation.         On             June 15, 2024    , Respondent wired             $85,000         from the Crestline brokerage account (ending 9956) to             Harborstone Financial Group, account ending 2291         (reference WT-2024-061524-0087). No account at Harborstone Financial Group is disclosed in the declaration, which asserts Respondent         “    does not maintain any other financial accounts.    ”  
             Recurring undisclosed transfers to an unidentified third party.         The individual checking account shows             $14,600         in Venmo transfers to         “    Kiera M.    ”         over March–July 2024 (monthly amounts of $2,800; $3,100; $2,500; $3,200; $3,000). The recipient and purpose are not disclosed anywhere in the declaration.  
             Valuation-date misrepresentation concealing dissipation.         The declaration represents that all balances are         “    as of the date of separation … on or about June 1, 2024,    ”         but the figures used are the             August 1, 2024         statement values. The Crestline account was approximately             $468,695         on June 1, 2024, but is reported at             $387,450         (the August 1 value) — a difference of approximately             $81,245         that is almost entirely the $85,000 wire. The checking account similarly dropped from             $26,869.56         (June 1) to             $8,215.00         (August 1), a decline of approximately             $18,655         driven by a             $14,644.60         irregular 401(k) contribution and rent on the new residence.  
             Gross understatement of monthly living expenses.         The declaration budgets discretionary spending at roughly $1,100/month (dining $600; clothing $200; personal care $100; miscellaneous $200). The credit card statements show average monthly charges of             $8,517         (May–August 2024), including clothing averaging             $1,825/month    , salon/spa             $360/month    , upscale restaurant dining             $1,306/month    , and             $425/month         in country club dues that are not disclosed at all.  
             Separation-date conflict.         The sworn declaration fixes separation at             June 1, 2024    ; the cover letter and privilege log repeatedly describe separation as             August 1, 2024    . The records (credit card address change effective July; first Harbor Point rent payment July 30; all bank/brokerage/mortgage mail still addressed to the marital residence through August 1) do not cleanly support a June 1 separation.  
             Business-governance and perquisite concerns.         The NovaBridge expense reports disclose that, under Operating Agreement §5.3, Respondent — as 60% majority managing member —             self-approves his own compensation and expense reimbursements         with no co-member review. The records show a same-day, near-identical         “    Personal Devices    ”         charge on the personal credit card ($2,149.50 to Pinnacle Electronics on 5/17/2024) and on the company expense report ($2,150 to TechVault Solutions on 5/17/2024, reimbursed 5/20/2024), and overlapping use of the same vendors (Sapphire Sky Travel; The Capital Grille; Ridgeline Steakhouse) across personal and business spending.  
             Significant production gaps.         Joint accounts (FCB 4821/4822), the 401(k) (Pinnacle 3310), the marital-residence mortgage statements, the complete tax return, and all NovaBridge entity-level documents (Operating Agreement, Form 1065, financials) were not produced. The privilege log is incomplete. Two exhibits (PROD-005 mortgage; PROD-006 credit card) bear no Bates ranges, and Bates prefixes/numbering are inconsistent and non-contiguous, implying a larger document universe than produced.  
      Part VI sets out prioritized follow-up discovery, including third-party subpoenas to Harborstone Financial Group, Fairview Community Bank, Crestline Wealth Advisors, Pinnacle Retirement Services, Meridian Home Lending, Venmo, and NovaBridge Solutions LLC; a motion to compel the omitted categories; a demand for an amended sworn declaration; and a forensic-accountant workup.

         IV. Flagged Inconsistencies, Omissions, and Deficiencies  
      Each finding below states the issue, the supporting record citation, and the consequence. Findings are grouped by theme but numbered consecutively; each is a distinct issue warranting its own follow-up.  
       A. Income Misstatement  
          Finding 1 — K-1                 “        income        ”                 reported as the cash distribution; business income understated by $193,200.         The declaration (¶¶ 15–16) reports         “    K-1 Income / Distributions (NovaBridge Solutions LLC) $174,000.00    ”         and describes this as         “    K-1 distributions received by Respondent from NovaBridge during 2023.    ”         The 2023 Schedule K-1 (PROD-002, DK-PROD-0053) shows that the             $174,000 is the distribution (Box 19a)    , while Respondent’s             K-1 ordinary business income (Box 1) was $367,200    . For support and standard-of-living purposes, the relevant figure is the $367,200 of pass-through business income on which Respondent is taxed (together with the $193,200 of his share of entity retained earnings), not merely the cash he chose to distribute.             Consequence:         the declaration understates business income by $193,200 and misleads the income analysis for child support, alimony, and equitable distribution.  
          Finding 2 —                 “        No other income        ”                 representation is false.         The declaration (¶ 19) states:         “    Other than the income described … Respondent has no other sources of income, including but not limited to interest income, dividend income, rental income, royalty income, trust income, or income from any other business venture or investment.             None.    ”         The 2023 joint return reports taxable interest of             $1,840         (Line 2b), ordinary dividends of             $6,310         (Line 3b; $4,620 qualified), a net capital gain of             $8,450         (Line 7), and net rental real estate income of             $2,940         (Schedule E). Each category the declaration denies is in fact present.             Consequence:         a sworn         “    None    ”         representation contradicted by the produced tax return; potential perjury/sanction exposure and a materially incomplete income picture.  
          Finding 3 — Total 2023 income understated by approximately $212,740.         Combining Findings 1 and 2, Respondent’s actual 2023 income was approximately             $571,740         ($185,000 W-2 + $367,200 K-1 ordinary business income + $2,940 rental + $1,840 interest + $6,310 dividends + $8,450 capital gain), versus the declared             $359,000         — an understatement of roughly             $212,740         (approximately 37% of actual income).             Consequence:         every support calculation and standard-of-living analysis built on the declaration is understated.  
          Finding 4 — Vehicle allowance not disclosed.         Darren Kwan’s 2023 W-2 (PROD-002, DK-PROD-0048) reports Box 14         “    Vehicle allowance $9,600    ”         (an $800/month employer-provided vehicle stipend from NovaBridge). This perquisite is not reflected in the declaration’s income or offset against the declared auto expenses.             Consequence:         understatement of total compensation and of the business-perquisite picture relevant to valuation.  
        B. Undisclosed or Omitted Assets and Liabilities  
          Finding 5 — Undisclosed real property: 1035 Elm Street, Unit C.         The declaration (¶ 23) states:         “    Respondent does not hold any ownership interest, whether sole or joint, in any other real property.    ”         The 2023 Schedule E (PROD-002, DK-PROD-0050) lists             1035 Elm Street, Unit C, Fairview, Columbia 20147         as a single-family residential condominium rented 365 days, and states the property is         “    titled in the name of Darren Kwan.    ”         The Meridian mortgage statements (PROD-005) confirm Respondent as sole borrower (Co-Borrower: None) on loan 7842-0163-ELM, original amount $279,000, originated             October 8, 2019         — during the marriage.             Consequence:         a marital asset (presumptively marital, acquired during the marriage with marital funds) and its income are entirely omitted from the sworn declaration; potential breach of the statutory disclosure obligation and of the fiduciary duty between spouses.  
          Finding 6 — Undisclosed mortgage on 1035 Elm Street (~$241,281).         The Meridian statements (PROD-005) show a current principal balance of             $241,281.19         as of 8/1/2024 (total outstanding incl. accrued interest $241,700.42), monthly PITI             $1,680.00    , 4.875% fixed, maturing 11/1/2049. This liability is absent from the declaration’s liability schedule (¶¶ 43–48).             Consequence:         total liabilities are understated by approximately $241,281 (declared $249,000 vs. actual ~$490,281), and net worth is overstated correspondingly.  
          Finding 7 — Undisclosed rental income ($2,100/month; $25,200/year).         The FCB checking statements (PROD-004) show recurring         “    ACH Deposit — Tenant Rent Payment    ”         of             $2,100.00/month         deposited to account 7703 on 4/5, 5/3, 6/5, and 7/3/2024, consistent with the Schedule E disclosure of         “    $2,100/month received for all 12 months    ”         in 2023. This income stream is omitted from the declaration’s income section and is not offset against the undisclosed Elm Street expenses.             Consequence:         understatement of both income and the cash-flow available for support.  
          Finding 8 — Undisclosed account at Harborstone Financial Group (acct. 2291).         C. Valuation Date and Dissipation  
          Finding 11 — Valuation-date misrepresentation conceals post-separation dissipation.         The declaration (¶ 56) represents that         “    All asset values and account balances reflected herein are approximate as of the date of separation, which Respondent identifies as on or about June 1, 2024.    ”         The figures used, however, are the             August 1, 2024         statement values: the Crestline account is reported at             $387,450         (the 8/1/2024 valuation) but was approximately             $468,695         on June 1, 2024 — a difference of approximately             $81,245         accounted for almost entirely by the 6/15/2024 $85,000 wire (offset by market gains). The checking account is reported at             $8,215         (8/1/2024) but was             $26,869.56         on June 1, 2024 — a difference of approximately             $18,655    , driven by the 8/1/2024 $14,644.60 401(k) lump and the $4,750 rent payment. (The savings account, by contrast, is slightly             overstated         relative to June 1: $42,680 vs. $40,547.96.)             Consequence:         using post-dissipation August 1 values while representing them as date-of-separation values understates the marital estate by roughly $97,800 net and conceals the timing and effect of the $85,000 wire and the 401(k) lump.  
          Finding 12 — $85,000 wire to an undisclosed account around separation.         See Finding 8. The wire occurred on 6/15/2024 — two weeks after the declared June 1 separation date (and roughly six weeks before the August 1 date stated in the cover letter). The recipient account (Harborstone 2291) is undisclosed.             Consequence:         prime candidate for a dissipation/concealment claim; tracing is required.  
          Finding 13 — $14,600 in recurring transfers to an unidentified                 “        Kiera M.        ”         The FCB checking statements (PROD-004) show Venmo transfers to         “    Kiera M.    ”         of $2,800 (3/15), $3,100 (4/22), $2,500 (5/10), $3,200 (6/18), and $3,000 (7/14) — totaling             $14,600         at an average of ~$2,920/month. The recipient and purpose are not disclosed in the declaration.             Consequence:         a recurring, undisclosed diversion of marital funds to an unidentified third party; possible paramour support, repayment of an undisclosed debt, or other dissipation; identification and tracing required.  
          Finding 14 — $14,644.60 irregular 401(k) contribution on 8/1/2024.         The FCB checking statement (PROD-004, 8/1/2024 partial) shows an ACH to         “    Pinnacle Retirement Services 401(k) — Early Month    ”         of             $14,644.60    , roughly ten times the regular bi-monthly $1,437.50 contribution.
