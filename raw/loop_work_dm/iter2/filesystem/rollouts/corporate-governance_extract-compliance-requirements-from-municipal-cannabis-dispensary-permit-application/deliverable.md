        I. Executive Summary  
      This matrix audits the Greenleaf Therapeutics LLC (    “    Greenleaf    ”    ) application package — comprising the business plan summary, lease summary, community-engagement report, insurance/bonding status memo, and State conditional license — against every requirement of Evanston Municipal Code Chapter 9, Article 5 and the City’s Application Checklist and Submission Guide. Each requirement is assessed for status and paired with a specific remediation note.  
      The package is substantively strong on several core eligibility items: the entity is a properly formed Illinois LLC; the State conditional license is genuine and on file; the lease runs seven years (plus renewals) and expressly permits cannabis use with landlord consent; the proposed C1a location satisfies all buffer distances on a straight-line basis; the capitalization ($1,850,000, all equity) is robust; the community-benefit contribution is correctly computed ($32,000 for Year 1); and the social-equity ownership structure (60% qualifying interest) is valid.  
      However, the review identifies             a cluster of critical deficiencies that, if not cured before the May 15, 2024 submission, will cause the application to be deemed incomplete or will materially depress the application score    :  
              No Professional Engineer (P.E.) odor-mitigation certification         — required             at the time of submission    ; the business plan concedes the system design will be         “    finalized during the build-out engineering phase.    ”         Without it the application is incomplete and will not proceed to scoring.  
              No performance-bond / letter-of-credit commitment letter         — required at submission to evidence bonding capacity; its absence forfeits the 10-point Financial Viability bonding sub-score and risks an incompleteness finding.  
              Cannabis-specific crime/theft insurance not bound and no binding commitment letter         — under negotiation, blocked on the unfinished security plan.  
              Community-engagement Session 2 gave only 12 days’ advance notice         (published March 28, 2024 for an April 9, 2024 session) — below the 14-day minimum; the session may be deemed non-qualifying, leaving only one qualifying session where two are required (and the two-session minimum is non-waivable).  
              Buffer-distance survey uses straight-line measurement         (the application form requires pedestrian-route measurement)             and is dated December 15, 2023         (the form requires the survey within 60 days of submission).  
              State conditional-license expiration timeline risk         — the license expires September 21, 2024, while the City’s own 120–150 day processing estimate places permit issuance in late Q3/early Q4 2024, potentially             after         expiration. The business plan does not mention the available six-month State extension petition.  
      The review also surfaces             numerous internal conflicts among the City’s own governing documents         (code vs. submission guide vs. application form) on payment methods, cure periods, certificate-of-good-standing recency, the occupancy formula, buffer-measurement methodology, the list of permitted zoning districts, and statutory cross-references. These are flagged because they create ambiguity the applicant must navigate and, in several cases, the application form is             more restrictive than (or inconsistent with)         the controlling ordinance.  
      A prioritized remediation action plan appears in Section V.  
      
        II. Methodology and Status Legend  
      Every distinct requirement in the Master Checklist (Submission Guide §3, Items 1–20) and in Municipal Code §9-5-5(B) (application contents) was extracted and compared against the record. Cross-document conflicts were identified by comparing the code, the submission guide, and the application form against each other and against the applicant’s submissions. Specific figures, dates, license numbers, and tract numbers were pulled verbatim from the record.  
          Status definitions:  
                         Status  
          Meaning  
                Compliant  
          Record evidence satisfies the requirement as written.  
                Deficient  
          Requirement is not met; cure required before submission or the application will be incomplete / denied.  
                At Risk  
          Requirement is met in commitment/posture but not yet finalized; failure to close the open item before the relevant deadline will create a deficiency.  
                Not Verifiable  
          Required item is referenced but not present in the package; cannot be confirmed from the record.  
                Conflict  
          The City’s governing documents disagree with each other on the same requirement; the applicant must determine which controls.  
                Non-Issue  
          A fact that resembles a problem but is expressly excluded or not applicable; flagged to avoid a false positive.
