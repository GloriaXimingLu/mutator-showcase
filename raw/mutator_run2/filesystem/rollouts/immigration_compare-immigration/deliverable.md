## I. Executive Summary

This memorandum reviews the certified PERM application (ETA Form 9089,
Case No. A-18247-63910, filed March 15, 2025; certified May 22, 2025)
filed by Prismex Analytics, Inc. ("Prismex" or the "Employer") on behalf
of Ananya Mehrotra (the "Beneficiary") for the position of Senior
Machine Learning Engineer, and compares the minimum requirements stated
on the application (Section J.B) against the Beneficiary's supporting
credentials. The review was undertaken in connection with the Employer's
transition to the H-1B (Form I-129) and, ultimately, the Form I-140
immigrant petition stages.

The Beneficiary's credentials are strong and, with one critical
exception, satisfy the PERM's stated minimum requirements. The education
requirement (Master's degree in Computer Science) is met by her M.S.
from North Carolina State University. The experience requirement (five
years of progressive post-master's experience) is met by approximately
six years and nine months of qualifying experience at DataBridge
Solutions LLC as of the March 15, 2025 priority date. Four of the five
enumerated special skills are well documented.

**However, the review identifies one CRITICAL gap and several
moderate-to-minor gaps and documentary inconsistencies that must be
addressed before the Form I-140 is filed:**

1.  **CRITICAL --- AWS Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty
    certification.** The PERM states this certification (or an
    equivalent) is *required* (Section J.B.5). The Beneficiary does
    **not** hold it. The only AWS credential she holds --- AWS Certified
    Cloud Practitioner --- is a *foundational*-level certification that
    **expired on January 18, 2025**, before the PERM filing date, and is
    not equivalent to the specialty-level certification. Her TensorFlow
    Developer Certificate is issued by Google and is not an AWS
    equivalent. This gap is compounded by the Employer's attestation on
    the ETA 9089 (Section K.3) that the Beneficiary possesses all stated
    special skills, which is inconsistent with the documentary record.
    This creates material risk at the I-140 stage (failure to meet a
    minimum requirement as of the priority date) and potential
    integrity/revocation exposure for the certified PERM.

2.  **MODERATE --- FEIN discrepancy.** The Employer's Federal Employer
    Identification Number (FEIN) is listed as **84-3291756** on the PERM
    (Section C.7) but as **84-3291047** on the Prevailing Wage
    Determination (PWD). This must be reconciled before the I-140, where
    the FEIN anchors the ability-to-pay analysis.

3.  **MODERATE --- Missing NC State Research Assistant verification
    letter.** The PERM lists the Beneficiary's Research Assistant
    appointment at NC State (August 2016 -- May 2018) as an experience
    entry, but no third-party verification letter has been obtained (the
    supervising professor is on sabbatical). The transcript partially
    documents the appointment. This experience is not needed to satisfy
    the five-year minimum, but it should be corroborated if relied upon.

4.  **MINOR/SOFT --- R programming proficiency; supervisory scope; "Dr."
    honorific; telephone-number consistency; and experience-calculation
    precision.** Each is addressed below with targeted remedial steps.

A prioritized summary and remedial recommendations appear in Sections V
and VI. Given the Beneficiary's STEM OPT expiration on September 15,
2025, and the targeted H-1B filing date of approximately July 15, 2025,
the critical certification gap should be addressed immediately.

#### C.1 R Programming Proficiency --- Soft Gap (Minor)

The PERM requires "proficiency in Python **and** R." The Beneficiary's
Python proficiency is unambiguously established (expert; primary
language across all roles). For R, the record is comparatively thin: the
resume lists R as "intermediate," and the DataBridge experience letter
describes only "some use of R for statistical reporting and ad hoc data
analysis." The NC State transcript shows successful completion of **ST
558 --- Statistical Computing with R** (grade A--), which provides
formal academic support for R competency.

**Assessment:** "Some use" is weaker than "proficiency," but the
combination of formal coursework (A--) and documented workplace use
provides a defensible basis. This is a soft gap, not a disqualifying
one. The risk is that an adjudicator or auditor reads "some use" as
falling short of the stated "proficiency" minimum.

**Remediation:** Supplement the DataBridge experience letter (or obtain
a brief addendum) to describe the Beneficiary's R usage more concretely
--- e.g., specific statistical reporting deliverables, R packages
utilized (ggplot2, dplyr, caret, etc.), and the frequency/regularity of
R use --- so the record supports "proficiency" rather than "some use."
The transcript coursework should be cited in the I-140 brief as
corroboration.

#### C.2 AWS Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty Certification --- CRITICAL GAP

This is the most significant finding of the review.

-   **Requirement (J.B.5):** "AWS Certified Machine Learning --
    Specialty certification or equivalent required."
-   **Beneficiary's AWS credential:** AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner,
    issued January 18, 2022, **expired January 18, 2025** --- a
    *foundational*-level certification that "validates a foundational,
    high-level understanding of AWS Cloud services" and "does not
    demonstrate specialization in any specific AWS service domain,
    including machine learning" (per the certifications compilation).
-   **Beneficiary's other credential:** TensorFlow Developer Certificate
    (Google; issued March 10, 2023; active; no expiration) ---
    framework-specific to TensorFlow and "not affiliated with Amazon Web
    Services."
-   **Counsel's own verification (certifications compilation):**
    "Counsel specifically inquired whether the beneficiary holds the AWS
    Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty certification or any other
    AWS specialty-level certification; the beneficiary confirmed she
    does not hold any such certification."

**Assessment:** The Beneficiary does **not** hold the required
certification, nor any equivalent. The AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner
is not equivalent to the AWS Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty ---
it is a lower-tier, foundational credential covering general cloud
literacy rather than ML specialization on AWS. It is also **expired**,
having lapsed two months before the PERM was filed. The TensorFlow
Developer Certificate is issued by a different vendor (Google) and
validates a different skill domain (TensorFlow framework proficiency);
it is not an "equivalent" to an AWS specialty certification. The "or
equivalent" clause is therefore unsatisfied.

**Compounding issue --- attestation integrity (Section K.3).** On the
ETA 9089, the Employer attested "Yes" to K.3 ("Does the foreign worker
possess the special skills and other requirements stated in Section
J.B.5?"). Because the AWS Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty
certification is one of those stated requirements and the Beneficiary
does not hold it, the K.3 attestation is, as a factual matter,
inconsistent with the documentary record. This implicates:

-   **I-140 risk.** For employment-based preference petitions, the
    Beneficiary must establish that she met *all* minimum requirements
    stated on the ETA 9089 as of the priority date (March 15, 2025). A
    certification listed as a minimum requirement that the Beneficiary
    did not hold as of the priority date is a ground on which USCIS may
    deny the I-140. Obtaining the certification *after* the priority
    date is necessary but may not fully cure the priority-date gap.
-   **PERM integrity/revocation risk.** A certification procured through
    a material misrepresentation or willful failure to comply with the
    regulations is subject to revocation (20 CFR 656.32). While the DOL
    30-day self-review window has likely closed, revocation remains
    available where a certification was procured by fraud or willful
    misrepresentation.
-   **Counsel's disclosure obligations.** Counsel is now in possession
    of documentary evidence (the certifications compilation) that is
    inconsistent with a material attestation on the certified
    application. This requires a documented, good-faith decision about
    how to proceed.

**Remediation:** See Section VI (Recommendations 1--3). In summary: (i)
the Beneficiary should obtain the AWS Certified Machine Learning --
Specialty certification immediately; (ii) counsel and the Employer must
make a documented decision about whether the certification is a bona
fide minimum requirement and how to address the priority-date and
attestation-integrity implications; and (iii) the H-1B (I-129) filing
strategy should be calibrated to avoid over-relying on the certification
as a strict prerequisite in the near term.

#### C.3 Supervisory Scope (3--5 ML Engineers) --- Minor Observation

The position requires supervising a team of 3--5 ML engineers. The
Beneficiary's documented supervisory experience is leading a team of
**2** junior data scientists at DataBridge. The "will supervise"
language in J.B.5 describes a job duty/condition rather than a
prerequisite the Beneficiary must previously have performed at the same
scale. The Beneficiary has demonstrated supervisory capability
(mentorship, code review, project coordination), which supports her
ability to perform the duty.

**Assessment:** Not a disqualifying gap. The scale difference (2
vs. 3--5) is minor and is best characterized as a capability that is
supported, rather than a missing credential. Counsel should be prepared,
if questioned, to point to the demonstrated supervisory experience and
the senior nature of the offered role.

**Remediation:** No corrective action strictly required. Optionally, the
DataBridge letter could be supplemented to describe the scope of her
supervisory responsibilities (e.g., hiring input, performance feedback,
sprint planning) to reinforce leadership capability.

error (the PWD remains valid through November 7, 2025).

## VI. Remedial Recommendations

### Recommendation 1 --- AWS Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty Certification (Critical; Immediate)

(a) **The Beneficiary should begin preparing for and sit the AWS
    Certified Machine Learning -- Specialty examination immediately**,
    with a target of obtaining the credential before the I-140 is filed
    (and, ideally, before the H-1B is filed). The Beneficiary's
    documented AWS SageMaker production experience provides a reasonable
    foundation for the exam, but the specialty exam requires dedicated
    preparation (AWS ML services, SageMaker, model deployment, MLOps,
    security, and cost optimization). Given the STEM OPT deadline of
    September 15, 2025, preparation should commence now.

(b) **Counsel and the Employer must make a documented decision regarding
    the bona fides of the certification requirement.** Two paths should
    be evaluated:

-   **Path A --- The certification is a genuine, bona fide minimum
    requirement.** If the Employer truly requires the AWS Certified
    Machine Learning -- Specialty certification of *all* applicants for
    this role (and can so attest), then the Beneficiary must obtain it.
    Counsel must then assess the priority-date exposure: because the
    Beneficiary did not hold the certification as of March 15, 2025, the
    I-140 carries denial risk on the "minimum requirements as of
    priority date" theory. Options include proceeding with the I-140
    after she obtains the credential (accepting some risk and preparing
    a legal argument that the requirement is met), or --- if the risk is
    unacceptable --- withdrawing the certified PERM and refiling a new
    PERM after the Beneficiary obtains the certification (recognizing
    the cost and delay, and the tight STEM OPT timeline).

-   **Path B --- The certification is not a true minimum requirement
    (e.g., it is preferred, or was included in error).** If the Employer
    cannot genuinely attest that the certification is required of all
    applicants, then the PERM's stated minimum requirements are
    over-inclusive relative to the Employer's actual requirements, which
    is itself a PERM deficiency (inflated requirements invite
    business-necessity scrutiny and undermine the "clearly open to U.S.
    workers" premise). In that case, the Employer should consider filing
    a new PERM that accurately states the true minimum requirements
    (without the certification, or with it recharacterized as
    preferred). The current certified PERM should not be relied upon if
    its stated requirements do not reflect the Employer's actual
    minimums.

(c) **Document the decision.** Whichever path is selected, the basis for
    the decision --- including the Employer's confirmation of whether
    the certification is a bona fide minimum --- should be documented in
    the engagement file. This is essential given the inconsistency
    between the K.3 attestation and the documentary record.

### Recommendation 2 --- H-1B (Form I-129) Filing Strategy (Critical; Time-Sensitive)

The H-1B petition is the immediate bridge (FY2026 cap selection; STEM
OPT expires September 15, 2025; target filing \~July 15, 2025). The H-1B
requires (i) a specialty occupation requiring a bachelor's or higher and
(ii) a Beneficiary qualified to perform the specialty occupation. The
Beneficiary's M.S. in Computer Science plus approximately seven years of
relevant experience readily satisfy the H-1B qualification standard; the
AWS certification is **not** a statutory prerequisite for H-1B
classification.

**Recommendation:** File the H-1B on schedule. To minimize RFE risk
while the certification gap is being resolved, ensure the H-1B's
position description and the Beneficiary's qualification narrative do
not over-state the AWS certification as a strict prerequisite the
Beneficiary must already hold. If the Employer wishes to represent the
certification as required, the Beneficiary should obtain it before the
H-1B is filed; otherwise, frame the position's minimum as a bachelor's
(or higher) in a relevant field plus the relevant experience, which the
Beneficiary satisfies. Confirm the cap-gap extension analysis (F-1 STEM
OPT automatic extension to October 1 upon timely-filed cap-subject H-1B)
so the Beneficiary's work authorization is uninterrupted through the
transition.

### Recommendation 3 --- Address the Attestation-Integrity Issue (Critical)

Because counsel now holds evidence (the certifications compilation) that
is inconsistent with the K.3 attestation on the certified PERM, counsel
should advise the Employer of the discrepancy in writing, outline the
options in Recommendations 1(a)--(c), and obtain the Employer's
documented direction. If, upon review, the Employer confirms the
certification is a bona fide requirement that the Beneficiary did not
hold as of the priority date, counsel should evaluate whether continued
reliance on the certified PERM is appropriate or whether corrective
action (withdrawal/refiling) is warranted to avoid a
fraud/willful-misrepresentation revocation theory under 20 CFR 656.32.
This is a judgment call for counsel with the client, but it must be made

## VII. Risk Assessment and Strategic Considerations

**A. I-140 priority-date risk (certification).** The single greatest
exposure is that the Beneficiary did not hold the AWS Certified Machine
Learning -- Specialty certification as of the March 15, 2025 priority
date. Even if she obtains it before the I-140 is filed, USCIS may deny
on the theory that a stated minimum requirement was unmet as of the
priority date. The strength of this risk depends on (i) whether the
certification is a bona fide minimum requirement and (ii) how
aggressively USCIS scrutinizes the special-skills attestation. Counsel
should weigh proceeding (with the credential obtained and a supporting
legal argument) against refiling a new PERM, against the backdrop of the
STEM OPT timeline.

**B. PERM revocation/integrity risk.** The K.3 attestation inconsistency
creates a theoretical revocation exposure under 20 CFR 656.32,
particularly if the certification is a bona fide requirement the
Beneficiary never held. The exposure is mitigated if the Employer can
demonstrate the certification was not, in fact, a true minimum
requirement (supporting Path B above), but that path carries its own
PERM-deficiency implications. A documented, good-faith resolution is the
best protection.

**C. Timeline pressure.** The Beneficiary's STEM OPT expires September
15, 2025. The H-1B (I-129) is the priority filing (\~July 15, 2025) and
is not blocked by the certification gap. The I-140 follows and is where
the certification gap must be resolved. The two-track approach (file
H-1B now; resolve certification for the I-140) is the most pragmatic
path, provided the H-1B is not framed in a way that imports the
certification prerequisite.

**D. Classification note (non-credential).** The position's
Master's-degree requirement supports EB-2 classification. The additional
five-year post-master's progressive requirement exceeds the standard
EB-2 minimum; while defensible for a senior role, counsel should ensure
business-necessity rationale is documented in the audit file in the
event of an audit. This is outside the scope of a credential gap but is
noted for completeness.

**E. Ability to pay (non-credential).** The offered wage (\$142,000)
exceeds the prevailing wage (\$131,747). The Employer (312 employees; in
business since 2018) should be able to demonstrate ability to pay via
payroll/tax records (Cornerpoint Payroll Services). The FEIN
reconciliation (Recommendation 4) is a prerequisite to a clean
ability-to-pay showing.

## IX. Conclusion

The Beneficiary is well-qualified for the offered Senior Machine
Learning Engineer position: she holds the required Master's degree,
exceeds the five-year post-master's experience minimum, and documents
four of the five enumerated special skills. The education, experience,

non-essential) experience verification letter, and several minor
consistency items. The critical gap must be resolved before the Form

15, 2025 STEM OPT expiration. The H-1B (I-129) can proceed on schedule
if its qualification narrative is calibrated to avoid importing the
certification prerequisite. The recommendations in Section VI, executed
on the timeline in Section VIII, will align the Beneficiary's documented
credentials with the PERM's stated requirements and position the
