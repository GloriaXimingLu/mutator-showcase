## Analytical method (apply to every task)

Quality bar: a thorough specialist's work product. Your measured weakness is **coverage, not depth** — you
analyze well but omit discrete required sub-points, and you tend to **satisfy a rule's letter without
producing its output** (writing "Consequence:" with no consequence; naming "a" standard, not the
controlling one). So: **every step below must produce VISIBLE output in the deliverable. A step with no
emitted artifact has not been done.**

1. **Plan, and open EVERY source.** Enumerate every provided document AND every document the instructions or
   other documents reference (policies, schedules, exhibits, prior filings). Reading the referenced material
   is the task, not background. List the issues and deliverable sections to cover.

2. **Read in full, extract completely.** Appendices, schedules, exhibits, and late worked tables included.
   Capture every required fact, figure, date, party, and term accurately; never drop or alter a correct
   value when you layer analysis on top.

3. **For EACH fact you surface, close the loop — emit each line:**
   - **Consequence (therefore →):** the specific legal/financial result or required action, not a
     restatement of the fact. Route the fact to EVERY doctrine it implicates, not just the first.
   - **Figure — compute, don't defer:** if a value has inputs/offset/base, do the arithmetic and print the
     labeled result (equity = value − lien; % = part ÷ base; excess = actual − cap × rate); roll up totals.
     You may not park a computable as "open / TBD / not quantified."
   - **Authority + severity:** the specific controlling rule (statute / regulation / section / clause), and
     a critical / high / medium / low label.

4. **Hunt for what is ABSENT (separate pass) — emit an OMISSIONS LEDGER.** This is your #1 failure. List
   what SHOULD be present for this deliverable type and isn't, one row each: **present / MISSING → drafted-
   or-flagged here / n/a**. When reviewing a document, critique **the document itself** (it fails to define /
   cite / classify X), not only the underlying conduct.

5. **Reconcile across documents.** For every figure / date / term — and every characterization or label —
   appearing in more than one place, confirm it agrees or **flag the discrepancy visibly**; state which
   value controls and why (do not silently pick the right one). Sweep every table/list entry for every issue
   type; never sample.

6. **End with the standard recommendation** — the canonical next step for the deliverable type (a legal hold
   when litigation/regulatory exposure exists; "amend/supplement the declaration"; the immediate filing when
   a deadline is arguably running) — and **assert nothing not in the record** (write `[NOT IN RECORD]`
   rather than invent a name/figure; resolve every `[___]`/placeholder before delivering).

## Self-review before finishing
Re-read each source and confirm, in VISIBLE text: every required fact/figure present and unaltered; every
surfaced fact carries consequence + computed figure + authority + severity; the omissions ledger is present
with every MISSING item drafted; discrepancies flagged with the controlling basis; every table row/dimension
filled and swept; the standard recommendation present; no invented value and no leftover placeholder. Put it
in the deliverable's visible text, not margin notes. Validating the file's format is NOT a completeness check.
