## Document structure

- **Executive Summary** — organizing principle, two structural facts (near-parity contributions / asymmetric equity; complementary non-fungible contributions), and an at-a-glance table of the five landing points.
- **Five self-contained provisions**, each with: (A) ready-to-insert clause language using the JVA's defined terms; (B) a positions table (Meridian opening / Kastor opening / proposed compromise); (C) negotiation rationale explaining concessions and gains for both sides; (D) cross-references to related JVA provisions.
- **Summary Concession Matrix** — a 7-column table (provision, both openings, compromise, principal concession by each side) plus an "Overall Balance" narrative.

## The five compromises (at a glance)

1. **Governance (§ 5.01):** 3-2 board (Meridian majority); supermajority (4 of 5, incl. ≥1 Kastor) on a narrow 9-item Reserved Matters list; tiered deadlock (executive escalation → mediation → CEO casting vote on operational matters / buy-sell on Reserved Matters).
2. **Capital Calls (§ 4.03):** 75-day total period (30 + 45); penalty interest (SOFR + 500 bps or 10%); secured Cure Loan with auto-conversion; 20%/yr dilution cap with forced-sale buy-sell at 20% cumulative in 24 months.
3. **Non-Compete (§ 11.01):** 18 months (12 if exit < 24 months from formation); scope = JV product lines + substantially similar/directly competitive products; geography = U.S./Canada + $500K/24-month threshold territories; Delaware law & forum; reformation.
4. **Exit Valuation (§§ 10.03–10.04):** 7.0× trailing 12-month EBITDA (near 7.1× median); single jointly-selected independent appraiser (not the JV auditor) with AAA fallback; triggers = change of control, material breach, deadlock, involuntary transfer (with intra-group carve-outs).
5. **IP License-Back (§ 12.05):** Contributed IP reverts; no license of Meridian Contributed IP to Kastor; Meridian keeps a use license to Kastor Contributed IP; Co-Developed IP licensed to both (Meridian exclusive in NA, Kastor non-exclusive outside NA); territorial sublicensing subject to a global Restricted Competitors prohibition + 25% NA revenue covenant + audit rights; improvements revert to contributor with a restricted license to the other party.
