## Key analytical findings
- All three proposed rates/fees exceed the upper end of market ranges (A: 6.25% vs. 5.5% high; B: 5.0% vs. 4.5% high; C: ~$5.28M vs. $4.0M high).
- The Net Sales redefinition compounds the rate increase; License A's effective rate (~6.70%) approaches the §14.3 150% cap (6.75%).
- License A's foundational patent (U.S. 9,234,117, ~60% of portfolio value) expires Mar 12, 2028 mid-term — supports a royalty step-down to 2.0–3.5% post-expiry.
- License B is irreplaceable (no alternatives; $24–34M / 3–4 yrs to develop) — NovaTrait holds very high leverage; secure long term, resist 5.0%.
- License C has credible alternatives (break-even 0.7–2.5 yrs) — strongest switching leverage; reject source-code elimination and Derivative Data ownership clause.
- Critical deadline: License B standstill expires Jun 29, 2025 — extension to Dec 31, 2025 recommended as a condition of continued negotiation.
- Flagged drafting errors in NovaTrait's proposal: "worldwide" Territory for License B (actual = Western Hemisphere, §1.19); cross-reference to "§12.4" for sublicense consent (should be §3.3); misidentification of Greenfield's counsel as "Bergerac Whitlock LLP" (actual = Ashworth & Calder LLP).
