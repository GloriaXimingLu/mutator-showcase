## ARTICLE 4. PUBLIC INCENTIVES

**4.1 Land Write-Down.** The conveyance of the Site at fifty percent (50%) of the Appraised Value constitutes a land write-down incentive with a value of $9,200,000. The Parties acknowledge that the land write-down is a separate incentive and shall not be charged against, or reduce, the TIF Reimbursement cap set forth in Section 4.2.

[Drafting Note — Cross-document conflict / open item (land write-down vs. TIF cap): The TIF Memo (Section VII) recommends that the development agreement "clearly specify whether the land write-down is charged against the TIF cap or constitutes a separate incentive funded from another source." The Corporation Counsel's recommendation reflects the City's interest in preserving TIF capacity. **4.2 Tax Increment Financing.**

(a) **Pledge.** The City shall pledge up to $48,000,000 in TIF increment over the remaining life of the TIF District to reimburse Developer for eligible TIF costs incurred in connection with the Project (the "TIF Reimbursement"). Eligible TIF costs shall include, without limitation, environmental remediation costs, public infrastructure improvements, parking structure construction costs, and other eligible project costs under the TIF Act and the TIF District Redevelopment Plan.

(b) **Pay-As-You-Go.** TIF Reimbursement shall be on a pay-as-you-go basis. Developer shall front all eligible costs and shall be reimbursed annually from available TIF increment as generated by the Project and other developments within the TIF District. The City shall not issue TIF bonds or notes for the Project.

(c) **Subject to Availability.** TIF Reimbursement is subject to the availability of increment. The City does not guarantee that the full $48,000,000 cap will be reached over the remaining life of the TIF District. A shortfall in TIF Reimbursement shall not, in and of itself, constitute a default by the City under this Agreement; provided, however, that the City shall use good-faith efforts to seek an extension of the TIF District or to adopt a successor TIF district if cumulative TIF Reimbursement is tracking materially below projections as of December 31, 2038, as provided in Section 4.2(f).

(d) **Priority of Reimbursement.** TIF Reimbursement shall be made in the following priority order: (i) first, environmental remediation costs for Parcels 016 and 017; (ii) second, public infrastructure costs, including the City's off-site infrastructure commitment and Developer's eligible on-site infrastructure expenditures; (iii) third, the public parking structure, public plaza, and community center costs associated with Phase IV; and (iv) fourth, all other eligible costs.

(e) **Estimated Increment.** The estimated annual TIF increment at full Project buildout is approximately $4,200,000 per year, based on projections by the City's finance department, subject to change based on actual assessed values, tax rates, and the performance of properties within the TIF District.

(f) **Good-Faith Extension Covenant.** If, as of December 31, 2038, cumulative TIF Reimbursement to Developer is tracking materially below the projections set forth in the TIF Memo such that the $48,000,000 cap is unlikely to be reached by the TIF District expiration date, the City shall use good-faith efforts to (i) seek legislative or other authorization to extend the TIF District or (ii) adopt a successor TIF district covering the Site, in each case subject to applicable eligibility findings and approval processes. The City's obligations under this Section 4.2(f) are obligations of good-faith effort only and shall not constitute a guarantee of extension or a monetary obligation of the City.

[Drafting Note — Cross-document conflict / open item (TIF reachability): The TIF Memo projects that, under a base case without the Phase I abatement, cumulative increment through 2042 reaches approximately $54.2M (exceeding the $48M cap); but under an adjusted scenario incorporating the Phase I property tax abatement, cumulative increment is approximately $38M–$42M, potentially falling $6M–$10M short of the cap.
