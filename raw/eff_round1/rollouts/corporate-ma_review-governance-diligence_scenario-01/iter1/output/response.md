## Key Findings
- **Critical:** Stonemill holds an absolute, non-circumventable veto via the Series C separate-class vote (Restated Cert. §4.3.6(b)(i)); the drag-along is **not** a reliable path to closing over Stonemill's objection (ambiguous trigger prong + contested charter-override question).
- **Critical:** $12M Clearfield term loan appears entered without the Preferred consent required by §4.3.6(a)(v).
- **Critical / time-sensitive:** SensorLink LOI exclusivity auto-renewed on Sep 8, 2024 — live exclusivity may conflict with Hargrove signing.
- **Critical (doc gap):** 2021 Equity Incentive Plan and award agreements not received; acceleration impact unquantifiable.
- **Significant:** Sandra Okafor (Northbridge designee) on the Special Committee; RRA "Deemed Liquidation Event" undefined; board minutes' waterfall characterization incorrect as to Series C; no independent committee counsel.
- Waterfall: Series C receives only its $18.50 preference (break-even) while all other constituencies receive upside — the economic root of Stonemill's leverage.
