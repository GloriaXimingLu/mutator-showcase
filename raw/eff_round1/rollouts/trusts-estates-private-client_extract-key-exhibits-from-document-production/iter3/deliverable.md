## I. Introduction and Scope

This memorandum catalogs, summarizes, and evaluates the document production served by Respondent Darren Kwan ("Respondent") through his counsel, Raymond Fossett of Hargrove Fossett & Yates LLP, in response to Petitioner Melinda Torres-Kwan's First Request for Production of Documents, Nos. 1–22. The transmittal email (production-cover-letter.eml) indexes seven (7) produced document sets (PROD-001 through PROD-007), identifies four (4) documents withheld on privilege grounds (PRIV-001 through PRIV-004), and indicates that entity-level records responsive to Request Nos. 16–22 remain outstanding pending "coordination" with NovaBridge Solutions LLC and its co-member Jared Ostrowski.

We have reviewed each produced exhibit in full, cross-checked the figures reported in Respondent's Sworn Financial Declaration (PROD-001) against the supporting account statements and tax records, and tested the internal arithmetic of the produced records. This memorandum (a) catalogs the exhibits; (b) summarizes the key figures; (c) flags material inconsistencies, omissions, and transactions of concern; and (d) recommends follow-up discovery. A summary of priorities appears in Section VI.

**Bottom line up front:** The production reveals several material discrepancies between the Sworn Financial Declaration and the supporting records. Most significantly, the Declaration (i) omits an entire real-property asset—a rental condominium at 1035 Elm Street, Unit C—along with its mortgage liability and rental income, all of which appear on the 2023 tax return; (ii) understates Respondent's 2023 income by approximately $197,000 by reporting cash distributions ($174,000) in lieu of pass-through ordinary business income ($367,200); and (iii) does not disclose an $85,000 wire transfer out of the brokerage account on June 15, 2024 to an unlisted account at Harborstone Financial Group, nor a pattern of recurring Venmo transfers totaling $14,600 to an unidentified third party ("Kiera M."). The separation date is also internally inconsistent as between the Declaration (June 1, 2024) and Respondent's own cover-letter privilege log (August 1, 2024). These items warrant targeted follow-up discovery, as detailed below.

## IV. Flagged Inconsistencies and Concerns

The following are organized by materiality. Paragraph (¶) references are to PROD-001 unless otherwise noted.

### A. Material

**1. Omission of the 1035 Elm Street, Unit C rental property (asset, liability, and income).**
The Declaration affirmatively states Respondent holds no real property other than the marital residence (¶23) and has "no other sources of income, including … rental income" (¶19). The 2023 tax return (PROD-002, Schedule E) shows a rental condominium at 1035 Elm Street, Unit C, titled in Respondent's name, generating $25,200 in rents and $2,940 net income, with $11,280 in mortgage interest paid to Meridian Home Lending. The produced mortgage statements (PROD-005) confirm a separate Meridian loan (No. 7842-0163-ELM) on that property with a $241,281.19 balance as of 8/1/2024. The bank statements (PROD-004) confirm both the rental income ($2,100/mo tenant ACH deposits, April–July) and two monthly Meridian ACH payments ($1,842 for Birchwood + $1,680 for Elm Street). The property, its mortgage, and the rental income are entirely absent from the Declaration. This is a sworn-statement omission directly contradicted by Respondent's own produced records and tax return.

**2. Understatement of income — distributions reported in lieu of pass-through ordinary business income.**
The Declaration reports "K-1 Income / Distributions" of $174,000 (¶15–16), which is the K-1 Box 19a **cash distribution**, not income. The K-1 Box 1 **ordinary business income** is $367,200 (PROD-002). Including K-1 interest ($1,240) and net rental income ($2,940), Respondent's 2023 individual income was approximately $556,380—about $197,000 more than the declared $359,000. This bears directly on child-support and maintenance calculations.

**3. $85,000 outgoing wire transfer to an undisclosed account (June 15, 2024).**
The Crestline statement for June 2024 (PROD-003, KWAN_PROD_00152) shows an $85,000 outgoing wire to **Harborstone Financial Group, account ending 2291** (Ref. WT-2024-061524-0087), initiated by the account holder. No Harborstone account appears in the Declaration's account schedule (¶24). The transfer occurred two weeks after the Declaration's stated separation date of June 1, 2024 (and, if the cover-letter privilege log is correct, during the marriage). The brokerage balance fell from ~$468,695 (5/31) to $385,125 (6/30) on this transfer. This is a potential dissipation/transfer of marital assets to an undisclosed institution and must be traced.

**4. Recurring Venmo transfers to an unidentified third party ("Kiera M.") — $14,600.**
The individual checking statements (PROD-004) show five substantial Venmo transfers to "Kiera M." between March and July 2024: $2,800 (3/15), $3,100 (4/22), $2,500 (5/10), $3,200 (6/18), and $3,000 (7/14), totaling $14,600 (avg. ~$2,920/mo). These recurring transfers are not disclosed or explained in the Declaration and do not correspond to any listed expense or obligation. The identity of the recipient and the purpose of the payments are unknown.

**5. Internally inconsistent separation date.**
The Declaration identifies the date of separation as "on or about June 1, 2024" (¶5, ¶56) and ties all asset valuations to that date. Respondent's cover-letter privilege log, however, repeatedly describes the separation as **August 1, 2024** (PRIV-001: "following the parties' separation on August 1, 2024"; PRIV-002: "post-separation August 1, 2024"). The two-month gap is a threshold issue: it determines the close of the marital estate, the characterization of the June 15 $85,000 wire and other mid-summer transactions, and the valuation date for assets. The inconsistency appears within Respondent's own production.

### B. Significant

**6. Crestline brokerage account tax-ID mismatch.**
Every Crestline statement (PROD-003) lists the account tax ID as ending **8341**. Respondent's SSN (per the tax return) ends **4718**; the SSN ending **8341** belongs to the parties' minor child, **Ethan Kwan** (PROD-002, dependents schedule). The account is styled as Darren Kwan's "Individual Taxable Brokerage" account, and its dividends/interest were reported on the parties' joint 1040 (Schedule B references account 9956). This discrepancy must be resolved: if the account is a custodial/UTMA account for the minor, the $387,450 may not be a marital asset of Respondent at all; if it is an error, the registration must be corrected. Either outcome materially affects the asset schedule.

**7. NovaBridge expense-reimbursement self-approval and possible double-reimbursement.**
PROD-007 discloses that Operating Agreement §5.3 permits Respondent, as the 60% managing member, to **self-approve his own expense reimbursements** without co-member (Jared Ostrowski) review. Two items overlap suspiciously with personal credit-card charges in PROD-006:
- **"Personal devices," May 17, 2024:** a $2,149.50 charge at **Pinnacle Electronics** ("Personal Devices") on the personal Visa, and a $2,150.00 reimbursement from NovaBridge for **TechVault Solutions** ("Technology Equipment — Personal Devices — Tablet, smartphone, and accessories") — same date, same category, near-identical amount, different vendor.
- **Sapphire Sky Travel:** a $3,400 NovaBridge reimbursement (4/3, "client appreciation event planning") and a $6,200 personal Visa charge (7/8) at the same vendor.

Additionally, only $5,550 of the $12,662 in H1 2024 reported reimbursements is traceable to deposits in the produced checking account ($3,400 on 4/10; $2,150 on 5/20). The destination of the remaining ~$7,112 is unexplained.

**8. Joint account statements not produced.**
The Declaration lists a joint checking account (FCB 4821, $12,340) and joint savings account (FCB 4822, $28,750) (¶24–26), but PROD-004 includes only the individual accounts (7703, 7704). As joint accounts, they are within Respondent's possession, custody, and control. The Declaration's $3,500/mo "voluntary contribution" to the marital household (¶51) is not traceable in the individual checking records and may flow through the joint account. These statements should be produced.

**9. Marital-residence mortgage undocumented; only the Elm Street loan was produced.**
The Declaration reports a $218,400 Meridian mortgage on 418 Birchwood Lane at $1,842/mo (¶22, ¶44). The $1,842 ACH is visible in PROD-004, but **no statements for the Birchwood loan were produced**—PROD-005 covers only the Elm Street loan (7842-0163-ELM, balance $241,281.19). The declared $218,400 balance cannot be verified from the production. Notably, the documented Elm Street balance ($241,281) exceeds the declared Birchwood balance ($218,400).

**10. Valuation-date misrepresentation.**
The Declaration represents that "all asset values and account balances … are approximate as of the date of separation … on or about June 1, 2024" (¶56). In fact, the reported balances match the **August 1, 2024** statement values (brokerage $387,450; checking $8,215; savings $42,680)—i.e., after the $85,000 wire, the $14,644.60 401(k) lump sum, and other post-separation activity. The brokerage balance on/around June 1 was ~$468,695, roughly $81,000 higher than reported.

**11. Credit-card creditor misidentified; "revolving balance" characterization inaccurate.**
The Declaration identifies the card issuer as "First National Card Services" with an "~$8,500 revolving balance" (¶43, ¶46). The produced statements are a **Fairview Community Bank** Platinum Visa (acct 8316) that is **paid in full every month** (finance charges $0.00; payments equal the prior balance). The $8,500 figure matches no statement balance (May $7,038; Jun $8,124; Jul $12,487; Aug $6,419). The card is better characterized as a monthly expense conduit than a revolving debt.

### C. Minor / Internal-Arithmetic

**12. Recurring $1,000 discrepancy in credit-card statements (June–August 2024).**
In each of June, July, and August 2024, the stated "Total New Charges" exceeds the sum of the itemized transaction lines by exactly $1,000 (June: $8,124.30 stated vs. $7,124.30 itemized; July: $12,486.75 vs. $11,486.75; August: $6,418.50 vs. $5,418.50). May 2024 reconciles exactly. This consistent gap suggests an unitemized recurring charge or a statement error and should be clarified.

**13. Internal inconsistency in Crestline holdings (Columbia Balanced Income Fund).**
In the June, July, and August 1 Crestline statements, the listed market value of the Columbia Balanced Income Fund does not equal shares × stated price (June: 1,759.49 sh × $86.40 = ~$152,020 vs. listed $118,400; July: ~$154,262 vs. $119,240; Aug 1: ~$154,368 vs. $118,680—a ~$33,600–$35,700 gap each month). May reconciles within rounding. The account totals foot to the listed (lower) values, but the per-holding figures are internally inconsistent.

**14. 401(k) contribution pattern irregular.**
The checking account shows monthly ACH 401(k) contributions of $1,437.50 (Mar–Jul) and an anomalous **$14,644.60** lump sum on 8/1/2024 ("Early Month"). The monthly figure annualizes to $17,250, which does not equal the $22,500 elective deferral reported on the 2023 W-2 (Box 12a). The August 1 lump sum—on the heels of the June wire and during the disputed separation window—warrants explanation, as does the 401(k) balance ($412,800), for which no statements were produced.

**15. Filing-date inconsistency.**
The cover letter states the Financial Declaration was "filed with the Court on or about October 4, 2024," while the Declaration itself bears a filed date and notarization of **September 20, 2024** (Certificate of Service dated 9/20/2024).

**16. Address / contact lag.**
The Crestline (PROD-003) and FCB bank (PROD-004) statements still show the marital address (418 Birchwood Lane) as of 8/1/2024, notwithstanding the declared 6/1/2024 move to 2200 Harbor Point Drive. The Visa statement address was updated effective the July 2024 cycle.

**17. Production-metadata errors.**
PROD-004's production stamp misstates the case number (2024-DR-00847 vs. 2024-DR-01847) and caption (*In re Marriage of Kwan* vs. *Torres-Kwan v. Kwan*); the cover-letter email has a blank Date field; and Bates prefixes/ranges are inconsistent across sets (see Section II).
