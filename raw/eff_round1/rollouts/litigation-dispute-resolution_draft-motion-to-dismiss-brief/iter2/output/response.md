## Motion — argument summary (per count)

- **Count V (DTPA):** (1) Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.49(f) exempts transactions over $500,000 where the consumer is not an individual — dispositive on the face of the FAC ($17.05M; Arcadia is an LLC); (2) the alleged statements are non-actionable puffery (*Pizza Hut*, *Castrol*, *Presidio*); (3) Arcadia's own conduct (45-day PM gap, 75-day API delay, Linden Park data-migration errors) breaks producing cause (*Melody Home*).
- **Count IV (Unjust Enrichment):** barred because the MSLSA is an express contract governing the same subject matter; Arcadia sues on it in Count I (*Fortune Production*, *Excess Underwriters*, *Nemec*); the LOL/warranty limits do not create a "gap" for unjust enrichment to fill.
- **Count II (Fraud):** fails Rule 9(b) (no "why"/scienter — *Flaherty*; lumping — *Dorsey*); puffery; economic loss doctrine (Del. *Brasby*/*Kuhn*; Tex. *Sharyland*/*Chapman*); § 12.1 integration clause + anti-reliance (*Eagle Industries*, *SIGA*) and Arcadia's own due diligence defeat justifiable reliance (*Presidio*, *Hollcroft*); no duty to disclose.
- **Count III (Negligent Misrepresentation):** no independent duty (*McCamish*); economic loss doctrine; benefit-of-bargain damages not recoverable in tort (*Sloane*); Rule 9(b); no justifiable reliance.
- **Count I (Breach of Contract):** deemed acceptance under § 5.3 (no Nonconformity Notice by Feb. 14, 2023; March 8 email was 22 days late and not a notice — *Simulados*, *Precision Healthcare*); data-migration allegations state no breach by Meridian (SOW-1 § 3.2 / Ex. C); § 8.2 bars the consequential/lost-profits/reputational/punitive damages (*Dresser-Rand*, *Kana Software*); § 8.1 cap and § 8.3/§ 9.1 exclusive remedy. Primary: dismiss; alternative: limit damages to direct damages within the cap.

The motion is captioned "12(b)(6), or, in the alternative, 12(c)" to hedge the Standing Order § 3.4 / Scheduling Order conflict, and includes a binding/persuasive authority-status note per Standing Order § 1.4. The table of authorities page references were verified against the rendered pagination.

## Cover memo — threshold issues flagged (in priority order)

1. **Subject-matter jurisdiction (HIGHEST PRIORITY):** The Notice of Removal treated Arcadia as a Texas-only citizen, but the operating agreement shows Arcadia is also a Delaware citizen through Apex Medical Ventures GP, Inc. (a Delaware corporation). Meridian is also a Delaware citizen → complete diversity destroyed → the Court likely lacks jurisdiction and may raise it sua sponte (*Arbaugh*). Flagged with strategic options (verify; do nothing vs. address proactively; federal-vs.-state-court tradeoffs; voluntary-invocation/estoppel concern since Meridian removed).
2. **12(b)(6) vs. 12(c):** Standing Order says treat as 12(b)(6); Scheduling Order says treat as 12(c); same standard; motion captioned to hedge; preservation confirmed in the Answer.
3. **DTPA § 17.49(f) asset-threshold dispute:** Arcadia's counsel argues the exemption is "conjunctive" and requires $25M consumer assets, asserting $23.8M (below threshold). Our research indicates the $25M test lives in § 17.45(4)/§ 17.49(b), not § 17.49(f); burden is on Meridian (*Cameron*); the $23.8M figure is unverified and may require factual development — obtain audited financials and investigate the "owned by a person with $25M+" prong.
4. **DTPA pre-suit notice:** demand dated June 15, 2023; original petition filed Aug. 7, 2023 (~53 days, short of the 60-day § 17.505(a) period); date discrepancy and FAC-timing complications; remedy may be limited to treble damages under § 17.505(d) — raised only as a secondary alternative.
5. **Choice of law** (Delaware contract / Texas torts / Texas DTPA); 6. **deemed-acceptance & warranty dates** to confirm; 7. **§ 8.1 cap "event" ambiguity** (obtain billing records); 8. **with-prejudice vs. leave to amend** risk; 9. **housekeeping** (split certificate of conference into a separate docket entry; update "Dkt. No. [__]" in the order; bar-number discrepancy between the Notice of Removal and the Answer; jury-waiver enforceability on remand); 10. **bottom line.**
