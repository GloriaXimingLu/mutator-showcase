# Deliverables: Rule 12(b)(6) Motion to Dismiss Package

**Matter:** *Arcadia Health Systems, LLC v. Meridian Cloud Solutions, Inc.*, No. 1:23-cv-00847-CMA (W.D. Tex., Austin Division) — Hon. Catherine M. Alvarez.

Three documents were produced in `output/`, all validated (ZIP/XML integrity, relationship consistency) and formatted to Judge Alvarez's Standing Order (12pt Times New Roman, double-spaced, 1" margins, centered page numbers, §-bracket caption).

## 1. `motion-to-dismiss.docx` (23 pages)

Defendant Meridian's Rule 12(b)(6) motion to dismiss all five counts of the First Amended Complaint with prejudice. Includes court caption, **Table of Authorities** (25 cases + statutes/rules, with computed page numbers and dot leaders), certificate of conference, and certificate of service.

**Argument by count:**
- **Count V (DTPA)** — Dispositive: the transaction is exempt under Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.49(f) because total consideration ($17.05M) exceeds $500,000 and Arcadia (an LLC) is not an "individual." Alternative: puffery / no producing cause.
- **Count IV (Unjust Enrichment)** — Barred because the MSLSA is a valid express contract governing the same subject matter (*Fortune Production*; *Excess Underwriters*); Arcadia sues on the contract in Count I, so the claims are irreconcilably inconsistent.
- **Count II (Fraud)** — Four independent grounds: (1) Rule 9(b) failure (no "why"/scienter; post-hoc reasoning per *Flaherty*; lumping per *Dorsey*); (2) puffery (*Pizza Hut*; *Presidio*); (3) integration/anti-reliance clause § 12.1 (*Eagle Industries*; *SIGA*; *Kuhn*); (4) economic-loss doctrine + no justifiable reliance (*Brasby*; *Chapman*; *Sharyland*).
- **Count III (Negligent Misrepresentation)** — No independent duty (*McCamish*); economic-loss doctrine; benefit-of-the-bargain damages not recoverable in tort (*Sloane*); Rule 9(b).
- **Count I (Breach of Contract)** — Deemed acceptance (§ 5.3; Go-Live Jan 15 → window closed Feb 14; first written complaint Mar 8, 22 days late — *Simulados*; *Precision Healthcare*); expired 90-day limited warranty with exclusive remedy (§ 9.1); consequential-damages waiver (§ 8.2) bars $18.4M lost profits / $6.7M operating costs / $5.0M reputational harm; liability cap (§ 8.1); data-migration defects caused by Linden Park per CO-004 § 5.1. Alternative: damages limited to the contractual cap.

Relies only on the FAC and documents referenced in/central to it (MSLSA, SOW-1, Change Orders, Dec. 9, 2021 proposal) to avoid conversion to summary judgment.
