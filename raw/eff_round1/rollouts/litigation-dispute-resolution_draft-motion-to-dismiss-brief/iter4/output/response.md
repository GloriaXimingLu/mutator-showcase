# Deliverables: Rule 12(b)(6) Motion to Dismiss — *Arcadia Health Systems, LLC v. Meridian Cloud Solutions, Inc.*, No. 1:23-cv-00847-CMA (W.D. Tex.)

Three documents were produced for Defendant Meridian Cloud Solutions, Inc. (Stonebridge & Calloway LLP), all formatted to comply with Judge Alvarez's Standing Order on Motion Practice (12-pt Times New Roman, double-spaced, 1-inch margins, page numbers at bottom center, proper caption) and validated as well-formed .docx files.

## 1. `motion-to-dismiss.docx` (25 pp.; ~20 pp. substantive — within the 25-page limit)

A Rule 12(b)(6) motion to dismiss **all five counts** of the First Amended Complaint with prejudice, filed as a post-answer motion under Standing Order § 3.4 (defenses preserved in Meridian's Answer, Dkt. No. 15). Includes a certificate of conference and certificate of service.

**Argument by count:**
- **Count V (DTPA)** — Dispositive: Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.49(f) exempts transactions over $500,000 where the consumer is not an individual. Arcadia is an LLC; total consideration is $17,050,000. Alternative: puffery and no producing cause (Arcadia's own conduct).
- **Count IV (Unjust Enrichment)** — Barred because an express contract (the MSLSA) governs the same subject matter. *Fortune Production*; *Excess Underwriters* (contractual limits don't create a "gap" for equity to fill).
- **Count II (Fraud)** — Four independent grounds: (1) Rule 9(b) failure (no "why" — post-hoc performance issues don't show pre-sale knowledge; *Benchmark*, *Dorsey*, *Flaherty*); (2) economic loss rule (Delaware: *Brasby*, *Kuhn*; Texas: *Sharyland*, *Chapman*); (3) puffery / no justifiable reliance (*Pizza Hut*, *Castrol*, *Presidio*; Schreiber's due diligence and the Dec. 2021 disclaimers); (4) integration clause / anti-reliance (*Eagle Industries*, *SIGA*; Arcadia withdrew its fraud carve-out).
- **Count III (Negligent Misrepresentation)** — No independent duty (*McCamish*); benefit-of-bargain damages not recoverable in tort (*Sloane*); economic loss rule; Rule 9(b) applies (*Benchmark*).
- **Count I (Breach of Contract)** — Deemed acceptance under § 5.3 (Go-Live 1/15/23; deadline 2/14/23; first complaint 3/8/23 — 22 days late; *Simulados*, *Precision Healthcare*); 90-day warranty expired 4/15/23; exclusive remedies (§ 8.3); consequential damages waived (§ 8.2); liability cap (§ 8.1) (*Dresser-Rand*, *Kana Software*); data migration was Arcadia/Linden Park's responsibility (SOW-1 § 3.2). Primary: full dismissal; Alternative: damages capped.

No argumentative recitals.

## 3. `cover-memo.docx` (9 pp.) — Privileged & Confidential / Attorney Work Product

Internal memo flagging **nine threshold issues**, in order of urgency:
1. **Diversity jurisdiction defect (CRITICAL)** — Complete diversity likely does *not* exist. The Notice of Removal considered only the managing member's citizenship and ignored Apex Medical Ventures, LP, whose general partner (Apex Medical Ventures GP, Inc.) is a Delaware corporation. Both parties are therefore citizens of Delaware and Texas. SMJ is lacking and can be raised sua sponte (*Arbaugh*). May supersede the 12(b)(6) motion; requires a client forum-strategy decision.
2. **Post-answer procedural posture** — Direct conflict between the Standing Order (treat as 12(b)(6)) and the Scheduling Order (treat as 12(c)); same standard applies either way.
3. **Choice of law** — Delaware governs the contract (§ 12.7); Texas likely governs the torts; motion addresses both in the alternative.
4. **DTPA pre-suit demand timing** — Suit filed 53 days after the § 17.505(a) demand letter (60-day requirement); secondary ground, pending receipt-date confirmation.
5. **Bar-number inconsistency** — Our Notice of Removal and Answer list different State Bar numbers for the same attorneys; verify before filing.
6. **Jury waiver vs. demand** — § 12.9 waiver vs. FAC's jury demand; relevant to the forum decision.
7. **Standing Order compliance** — Certificate of conference must be docketed separately; proposed order docket number to be updated within one business day.
8. **Count I strategy** — Full dismissal vs. damages limitation; alternative still eliminates ~$30M of the $47.3M demand.
9. **Summary and recommended next steps.**
