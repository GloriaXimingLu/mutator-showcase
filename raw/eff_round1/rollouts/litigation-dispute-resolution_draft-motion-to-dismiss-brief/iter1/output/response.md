## Motion — argument summary (each count dismissed on independent grounds)

- **Count V (DTPA)** — Dispositive statutory exemption: Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.49(f) bars DTPA claims where total consideration exceeds $500,000 and the consumer is not an individual. Total consideration here was $17,050,000 (MSLSA $14.7M + Change Orders $2.35M); Arcadia is an LLC. Asset-threshold (> $25M) argued in the alternative.
- **Count IV (Unjust Enrichment)** — Barred because an express, valid contract (the MSLSA) governs the identical subject matter; Arcadia sues on the contract and does not allege it is void. *Fortune Production*; *Excess Underwriters*.
- **Count II (Fraud)** — Five independent grounds: (1) Rule 9(b) (no "why false when made"; post-hoc failure ≠ scienter); (2) puffery ("industry-leading," "30–40% improvement"); (3) economic loss doctrine (Delaware *Brasby*; Texas *Sharyland*); (4) integration clause / Delaware anti-reliance (*Eagle Industries*, *SIGA*); (5) no justifiable reliance (Schreiber's due-diligence memo, proposal disclaimers, counsel-negotiated terms).
- **Count III (Negligent Misrepresentation)** — Four independent grounds: no duty independent of the MSLSA (*McCamish*); economic loss doctrine; benefit-of-the-bargain (not reliance) damages (*Sloane*); sounds in fraud → Rule 9(b).
- **Count I (Breach of Contract)** — Deemed acceptance under § 5.3 (first complaint 22 days after the 30-day window closed); 90-day warranty expired (§ 9.1) with exclusive remedy (§ 8.3); data migration was Arcadia's sole responsibility (SOW-1 § 3.2); consequential damages waived (§ 8.2) and liability capped (§ 8.1). **Alternative relief** requested: strike consequential damages and cap recovery.

The motion is a post-answer 12(b)(6) — the defense was preserved in Meridian's Answer (First Affirmative Defense and § III reservation) and the November 8, 2023 notice-of-intent letter, satisfying Standing Order § 3.4; filed ahead of the January 15, 2024 deadline. It notes the Scheduling Order's treatment as 12(c) under the same Twombly/Iqbal standard.

## Cover memo — threshold issues flagged

1. **Subject-matter jurisdiction (CRITICAL).** Complete diversity appears **destroyed**: Meridian is a citizen of Delaware *and* Texas; Arcadia (a Texas LLC) is also a citizen of Delaware because one of its members, Apex Medical Ventures, LP, has a Delaware-corporation general partner (Apex Medical Ventures GP, Inc.). The Notice of Removal analyzed only Arcadia's managing member. The defect is on the face of the FAC (¶¶ 7–8), is non-waivable, and could trigger sua sponte remand — mooting the motion. Memo recommends confirming the citizenship facts (Apex partnership agreement; trust documents; reconcile the "irrevocable"/"revocable" discrepancy) and making a partner-level call on whether to surface it, weighing federal-forum benefits (rigorous pleading standards, bench trial per the § 12.9 jury waiver) against remand risk.
2. Post-answer 12(b)(6) posture (Standing Order vs. Scheduling Order 12(b)(6)/12(c) tension; certificate of conference; deadline).
3. Choice of law on the torts (Delaware for contract; Texas likely for torts — brief argues both).
4. DTPA § 17.505 pre-suit-notice timing (demand dated June 15/26, 2023; suit filed Aug. 7, 2023 — possibly < 60 days) plus the § 17.49(f) exemption strength.
5. Jury-trial waiver (§ 12.9) vs. the FAC's jury demand (resolved to bench per Scheduling Order, but watch for a challenge).
6. Count I dismissal risk — most aggressive ask; alternative damages limitation is the high-probability fallback (~$32.6M of the $47.3M demand eliminated).
7. Limits of the 12(b)(6) record — Arcadia-fault facts (PM gap, API delay, Linden Park errors, Poletti's notes) reserved for summary judgment.
8. § 8.1 damages-cap calculation needs Meridian billing data.
