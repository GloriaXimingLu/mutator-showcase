# Multi-State Paid Leave Compliance Analysis — Deliverables Summary

**Client:** Vantage Logistics Group, Inc.
**Prepared by:** Hartwell & Cabot LLP (Daniel Ikeda, Senior Associate; supervised by Catherine Ng, Partner)
**Engagement:** Multi-State Paid Leave Compliance Review (engagement dated April 14, 2025; deliverables due May 19, 2025)
**Jurisdictions:** New Jersey, New York, Connecticut (current); Massachusetts, Colorado, Oregon, Washington (expansion)

## Deliverables

### 1. `multi-state-compliance-matrix.xlsx`
A color-coded compliance matrix comparing Vantage's current paid leave policies (Employee Handbook Rev. Jan 15, 2022) and practices against the statutory/regulatory requirements of all seven jurisdictions. Eight worksheets:

- **Cover & Legend** — engagement metadata, R/Y/G gap-indicator legend, severity framework, contents.
- **Summary Dashboard** — at-a-glance grid of overall gap indicators by state × leave category, with critical flags.
- **Sick Leave – Detail** — 9 dimensions × 7 states (accrual rate, cap, waiting period, carryover, covered uses, family-member definition, documentation, posting/notice, payout).
- **Family Leave – Detail** — 9 dimensions × 7 states (eligibility, duration, wage replacement, qualifying reasons, family member, notice, job protection, anti-retaliation, private-plan status).
- **Medical Leave – Detail** — 5 dimensions × 7 states (availability, duration, wage, STD interaction, claims routing).
- **Employer Obligations – Detail** — 6 dimensions × 7 states (registration, employer/employee contribution rates, withholding, recordkeeping, posting/notice).
- **PTO Payout – Detail** — 4 dimensions × 7 states (payout, forfeiture, carryover cap, waiting-time penalties).
- **Gap Register** — 22 ranked deficiencies (5 Critical, 11 High, 6 Moderate) with statutory basis, risk, recommended action, and target deadline.

### 2. `gap-analysis-memo.docx`
A privileged narrative memorandum (13 sections) identifying all material compliance deficiencies by severity, with a prioritized remediation plan tied to the facility-opening schedule and the July 1, 2025 revised-Handbook target:

- I. Introduction & Scope · II. Executive Summary · III. Methodology & Sources · IV. Summary of Findings
- V. Critical Deficiencies (C1–C5) · VI. High-Severity (H1–H11) · VII. Moderate-Severity (M1–M6)
- VIII. Self-Funded PFL Equivalency Analysis (state-by-state; 3 structural options)
- IX. Operational Risks — Manual Spreadsheet Tracking
- X. Prioritized Remediation Plan (immediate / by July 1, 2025 / by each opening / longer-term)
- XI. NJ DOL Complaint Resolution Framework · XII. Budget Considerations · XIII. Limitations & Next Steps

## Key Findings

**Critical (5):**
1. Narrow family-member definition & covered-uses scope in paid sick leave — subject of active NJ DOL complaint (Muñoz, Case No. NJ-ESL-2025-04117); systemic across all 7 states.
2. Self-funded PFL plan (50% / $850 wk / 8 wks) below statutory minimums in all 7 states; no private-plan approvals filed; not a compliant substitute anywhere (CO permits no self-funding).
3. No employer-funded paid medical leave for employee's own condition; HR misdirection to employee-paid voluntary STD instead of state TDI/DBL/PL programs (7+ affected employees).
4. MA PFML registration not complete — deadline ~June 1, 2025 for Aug 1, 2025 opening.
5. CO FAMLI registration not complete — 3 April 2025 remote hires may have already triggered the obligation.

**High (11):** sick leave accrual rate (1/40 vs 1/30); PT cap (24 vs 40) & WA accrual cap; 120-day waiting period; no carryover; NY 56-hr cap; PTO payout (MA/CO/WA); WA sick-leave payout; military exigency omission; notice carve-out; OR/WA registrations; PFL eligibility over-restrictive.

**Moderate (6):** manual spreadsheet tracking; documentation threshold; postings/notices; OR PTO language; recordkeeping/audit trail; CO carryover-cap forfeiture risk.

Both files passed schema/structure validation.
