## Issues identified (14)
- **Critical (3):** (1) pathological arbitration agreement from conflicting forum-selection + hierarchy clauses (SA §14.1/15.8 vs IPLA §9.1/9.3/10.3 vs QA §7/8.1); (2) fragmented architecture — 3 institutions / 2 seats / 3 laws; (3) consequential-damages waiver (SA §14.11) + liability cap (§11.1) + exclusivity + self-defeating QA §6.6.
- **High (4):** (4) no joinder mechanism for non-signatory co-manufacturers; (5) appeal-on-law to Singapore High Court (SA §14.6) likely unenforceable under the IAA/Model Law and undermines finality; (6) one-year limitation period (SA §14.9) at the UCC §2-725 floor and in tension with latent-defect provisions; (7) blanket discovery bar (SA §14.5) cripples quality/recall claims and may be unenforceable.
- **Medium (4):** (8) expert-determination "challenge" escape hatch (QA §7.2); (9) inconsistent cost allocation; (10) sole-arbitrator default / inconsistent tribunal size; (11) "general principles of international commercial law" gap-filler (SA §14.4).
- **Low (3):** (12) no negotiation/mediation tier; (13) inconsistent confidentiality-of-proceedings; (14) overbroad interim-relief forum / no Ridgeway intervention mechanism.

## Key recommendations
Adopt a single master dispute-resolution regime in the SA (ICC, Singapore seat, London venue only, NY law) governing all Transaction Documents; delete IPLA §9.3; reconcile the three integration clauses; carve out recall/lost-profit/third-party damages from the waiver (or substitute a cap) and fix QA §6.6; add a co-manufacturer joinder/consent mechanism or court carve-out; delete the appeal-on-law clause; extend the limitation period to ≥2–4 years aligned with latent-defect triggers; replace the discovery bar with IBA-style limited production + protective order; narrow the expert escalation trigger; and harmonize costs, tribunal size, and the governing-law gap-filler.
