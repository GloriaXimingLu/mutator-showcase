## Highest-value cross-document insights
1. The Toledo consultant failed to classify the off-site PCE plume as a REC, contrary to the Lender's explicit requirement — both an environmental and a lender-compliance problem.
2. The Toledo seller's §4.12/§4.13 representations are contradicted by the documented 1988 spill, 2003 NOV, and two Sanborn-documented USTs omitted from Schedule 4.13.
3. The Dayton Phase I ESA's HREC classifications lean on a 2011 VAP NFA letter whose scope (Parcel A; "diesel UST area") likely does **not** cover the waste-oil Tank 3 or the Building 3 Central Valley Chemical operations — so those "closed" conditions may be open.
4. The 2004 UST Closure Report shows Tank 3 was never closed out (results "pending," no supplemental report, no closure letter).
