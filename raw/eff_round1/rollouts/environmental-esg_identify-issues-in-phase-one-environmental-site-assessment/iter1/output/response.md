# Environmental Issues Memo — Work Summary

## Deliverable
`output/environmental-issues-memo.docx` — a privileged environmental issues memorandum with severity ratings for the proposed acquisition of **4820 Lakeshore Industrial Drive, Toledo, Ohio** (Breckenridge Manufacturing Co. → Cornerstone Industrial Partners LLC; $8,750,000 purchase price; $6,125,000 Great Lakes Capital Bank loan). Validated against ECMA-376 schema (exit 0).

## Scoping decision
The due-diligence file contained materials for **two** properties. The purchase agreement and lender requirements letter unambiguously tie "the proposed acquisition" to the **Toledo** property (Breckenridge). The **Dayton** materials (2850 Millrace Road — Terravista Phase I ESA, GeoSearch summary, 2004 Redland UST closure report) pertain to a separate, unrelated transaction (different seller, price, counsel, and timeline) and were excluded from the analysis, with a scope note in Section 10.

## Documents reviewed (Toledo)
1. Greenfield Phase I ESA (Oct 25, 2024, ASTM E1527-21)
2. EDR database summary (Sept 15, 2024)
3. Ohio EPA records-response letter (Sept 28, 2024)
4. Great Lakes Capital Bank environmental due-diligence requirements letter (Sept 10, 2024)
5. Asset Purchase Agreement excerpt (Sept 1, 2024)

## Key findings
- **3 Critical:** (E-1) TCE vapor degreasing + actively leaking waste-TCE drum (PID 42.3 ppm); (E-2) 39 years of hexavalent-chromium electroplating with no disposal records and a 2003 NOV; (E-3) off-site PCE plume from Lake Erie Dry Cleaning (87 µg/L, 17.4× standard) migrating onto the Property — which the ESA **failed to classify as a REC**, contrary to the lender's express requirement.
- **8 High:** unregistered 2,000-gal waste-solvent UST; former outdoor drum-storage area with stressed vegetation; vapor-intrusion risk; Seller rep breaches (§§ 4.12/4.13); insufficient indemnity cap ($1.75M)/3-yr survival; unsatisfied lender conditional requirements; ESA under-classification/lender non-compliance; Seller cooperation/fraud indicators.
- **10 Medium / 4 Low:** data gaps (MW-1, missing 1965–2003 waste records, missing 1961–1990 corporate records, archived consent order), GMZ restrictions, ACM/LBP, RCRA SQG, active diesel UST, adjacent listings, AAI/continuing obligations, § 6.3(b) termination right, Whitfield & Crane conflict of interest.

## Recommendation
Do not close on current terms; commission a Phase II ESA + vapor-intrusion assessment immediately; renegotiate the APA (correct Schedules 4.12/4.13, strengthen/extend the environmental indemnity, require Seller to fund the Phase II and produce withheld records); satisfy the lender's conditional items; and evaluate the § 6.3(b) termination right before the Oct 31, 2024 deadline.
