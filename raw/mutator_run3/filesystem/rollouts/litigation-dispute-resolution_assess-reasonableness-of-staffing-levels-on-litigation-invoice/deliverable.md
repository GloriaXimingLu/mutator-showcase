## Executive Summary

Hargrove & Linden LLP ("H&L" or the "Firm") submitted Invoice
No. HL-2025-05-PIN, dated June 5, 2025, for legal services rendered
during May 2025. The invoice **requests payment of \$201,822.50**,
consisting of \$193,372.50 in attorney fees and \$8,450.00 in expenses
(per the Summary page).

This review identifies **multiple material billing-guideline
violations** and one **critical internal reconciliation defect**. The
invoice **cannot be paid as submitted**. After applying the reductions
required by Pinnacle's Outside Counsel Billing Guidelines (January 2023)
(the "Billing Guidelines") and the Approved Discovery-Phase Staffing
Plan (Feb. 10, 2025), the **recommended payable amount is \$148,993.12**
--- a reduction of **\$52,829.38 (26.2%)** from the amount requested.

The principal findings are:

1.  **Internal reconciliation defect (§9.1).** The supporting Time
    Detail contains 117 line items totaling **\$215,680.00** in attorney
    fees, which is **\$22,307.50 more** than the \$193,372.50 stated on
    the Summary page and in the Time Detail's own SUBTOTAL row. The
    per-timekeeper figures on the Summary likewise do not foot to the
    line items. The Firm must submit a corrected, reconciled invoice.

2.  **Two unauthorized timekeepers (§4.2) --- \$33,400.00.** Nina Barros
    (mid-level associate, \$23,275.00) and Priya Sengupta (junior
    associate, \$10,125.00) billed the matter without the prior written
    approval required by §4.2. The April 28, 2025 staffing request for
    Ms. Barros was expressly *not* approved (Ms. Dominguez responded on
    May 1 that she would review and "circle back"), and no request was
    ever made for Ms. Sengupta, who is an impermissible *second*
    concurrent junior associate.

3.  **Contract attorney over-rate (§5.1, §4.4) --- \$3,862.50.** Marcus
    Tate was billed at \$210/hr, exceeding both the \$185/hr
    pass-through rate approved in the Staffing Plan and the \$200/hr
    §5.1 cap.

4.  **Excess timekeepers at a meeting and a deposition (§4.3) ---
    \$3,312.50.** Five timekeepers billed a single May 8 internal
    strategy call (cap: 3) and three timekeepers billed the May 19 Liu
    deposition without the advance approval required for more than two
    (cap: 2 absent pre-approval).

5.  **Pervasive block billing (§5.4) --- \$29,864.38.** Fifty-two
    entries combine two or more discrete tasks; the mandatory 25%
    reduction applies.

6.  **Task-to-timekeeper and administrative-task violations (§6.1) ---
    \$2,597.50.** A partner performed cite-checking; a junior associate
    performed routine first-level document review; and a mid-level
    associate billed scheduling/logistics as attorney time.

7.  **Budget overage without pre-approval (§7.3) and missing
    budget-to-actual reconciliation (§7.2, §9.1(g)).** The fees as
    submitted exceed the §7.3 ceiling (\$189,750) without the required
    written pre-approval, and the invoice omits the budget-to-actual
    reconciliation required by §7.2/§9.1(g).

8.  **Non-reimbursable travel expense (§6.4, §8.2, §8.3) ---
    \$2,100.00.** The deposition was held at H&L's own Atlanta office,
    so the "travel" (mileage, parking, meals) for two Atlanta-based H&L
    attorneys is local travel and non-reimbursable overhead; the \$2,100
    aggregate also exceeded the \$1,000 pre-approval threshold.

The detailed analysis and reduction calculations follow.

**Assessment.** An invoice whose supporting detail does not reconcile to

## III. Findings and Recommended Reductions

### A. Unauthorized Timekeepers --- §4.2 --- \$33,400.00 (full rejection)

The Approved Staffing Plan is the "complete and exclusive" roster of
authorized timekeepers. §4.2 requires prior *written* approval before
any new timekeeper performs billable work, and provides that
after-the-fact additions "are subject to reduction or rejection of all
fees attributable to the unapproved timekeeper, at Pinnacle's sole
discretion." Two timekeepers on this invoice were not approved.

**1. Nina Barros --- 49.0 hours / \$23,275.00 --- full rejection.**

The Staffing Plan expressly states that "James Pettersson is the only
mid-level associate assigned to this matter. No other mid-level
associate is included on the approved staffing roster." On April 28,
2025, Karen Cho emailed Ms. Dominguez requesting that Ms. Barros be
added. Ms. Dominguez replied on May 1, 2025: *"Before we can move
forward on adding Nina Barros, though, let me review this with Sandra
and get back to you... I'll try to circle back with you early next
week."* **No approval was ever given.** The Firm's own Rate Schedule
annotates Ms. Barros as "Pending Approval --- Added May 2025," and the
June 1 Case Status Report concedes her involvement was "consistent with
the request submitted" --- a request is not an approval. No
exigent-circumstance notification was provided within 24 hours as §4.2
permits. All \$23,275.00 is rejected.

**2. Priya Sengupta --- 27.0 hours / \$10,125.00 --- full rejection.**

The Staffing Plan authorizes only **one** junior associate at a time
(the "Rotating Pool"), with Tyler Webb as the current assignment, and
states that "any proposal to assign more than one junior associate
concurrently would constitute a staffing change requiring prior written
approval under §4.2." The invoice bills **two** junior associates
concurrently (Webb and Sengupta) throughout May. No staffing-change
request for Ms. Sengupta appears anywhere in the record; the Rate
Schedule merely notes "Added May 2025." Because Tyler Webb is the
approved junior associate, Ms. Sengupta is the impermissible second
junior associate, and all \$10,125.00 is rejected.

> **Subtotal --- §4.2: \$33,400.00.** (Note: rejecting these timekeepers
> also moots the block-billing, task-level, and meeting-cap issues that
> would otherwise apply to their entries.)

### B. Contract Attorney Rate Exceeds Approved Rate and Cap --- §5.1, §4.4 --- \$3,862.50

Marcus Tate (contract attorney via Redwood Document Solutions) was
billed at **\$210/hr**. The Staffing Plan approved a **\$185/hr**
pass-through rate (no markup), and §5.1 caps contract attorneys at
**\$200/hr**. The billed rate exceeds both. Per §5.1, rates above the
cap "will be automatically reduced to the applicable cap"; the approved
pass-through rate of \$185/hr controls as the lower approved rate. The
rate is reduced to \$185/hr across all 154.5 hours billed.

> **Reduction: 154.5 hrs × (\$210 − \$185) = \$3,862.50.**

### C. Excess Timekeepers --- Internal Meeting and Deposition --- §4.3 --- \$3,312.50

§4.3 caps billing timekeepers for any single internal meeting or
conference call at **three (3)**, even for Substantive Case-Strategy
Sessions, and caps deposition billing at **two (2)** absent advance
approval requested no later than five business days before the
deposition. No advance approval was requested for either event below.

**1. May 8 team strategy conference call --- five timekeepers billed.**
Entries 22 (Hargrove), 23 (Cho), 24 (Pettersson), 25 (Barros), and 26
(Webb) all bill the same internal call. After removing the unauthorized
Ms. Barros, four approved timekeepers remain --- one more than the three
permitted. The most junior attendee, Tyler Webb (2.5 hrs / \$937.50), is
reduced in full as the excess timekeeper.

**2. May 19 deposition of Dr. Rebecca Liu --- three timekeepers
billed.** Entries 60 (Hargrove), 61 (Cho), and 62 (Pettersson) bill the
deposition. Two attorneys (the lead partner observing/strategizing and
the senior associate who conducted the direct examination) are
reasonable without approval; the **third** timekeeper requires advance
approval, which was not requested. James Pettersson (5.0 hrs /
\$2,375.00), who managed exhibits and took notes, is reduced in full as
the excess timekeeper. (His exhibit/summary work could have been billed
separately and at a more appropriate level, but was impermissibly
block-billed with attendance --- see §D.)

> **Subtotal --- §4.3: \$937.50 + \$2,375.00 = \$3,312.50.**

### D. Block Billing --- §5.4 --- \$29,864.38

§5.4 prohibits combining two or more discrete tasks in a single line
item and mandates an **automatic 25% reduction** to the fees of each
block-billed entry. Fifty-two entries on this invoice combine distinct
activity types (e.g., drafting + research; review/revise + client
conference; attend deposition + post-deposition summary + team debrief;
finalize brief + prepare order + coordinate filing). Several mirror the
Guidelines' own examples almost verbatim (e.g., Entry 61: "Prepare for
and attend deposition of Dr. Rebecca Liu ...; post-deposition analysis
and team debrief"; Entry 92: "Prepare for and conduct strategy session
...; draft outline for expert discovery plan. Review and edit
interrogatory responses ...").

The 25% reduction is applied to each block-billed entry among the
approved timekeepers (entries already fully rejected under §4.2 or §4.3
are excluded to avoid double-counting). The 52 affected entries
aggregate \$119,457.50 in fees.

> **Reduction: \$119,457.50 × 25% = \$29,864.38.** A complete
> entry-by-entry schedule appears in Appendix A.

### E. Task-to-Timekeeper Appropriateness --- §6.1 --- \$1,885.00

§6.1 requires each task to be staffed to the lowest-cost competent
timekeeper and specifies rate reductions where a senior timekeeper
performs junior-level work.

**1. Partner cite-checking --- Entry 43 --- \$1,220.00.** David Hargrove
(partner, \$985/hr) billed 2.0 hours to "Review and revise cite-check
memorandum prepared by T. Webb; finalize citations for motion to compel
brief." Cite-checking and citation verification are presumptively
inappropriate for partners under §6.1(b) (appropriate timekeeper: junior
associate or paralegal). The entry is reduced to the junior-associate
rate of \$375/hr. Reduction: 2.0 × (\$985 − \$375) = **\$1,220.00**.

**2. Junior associate performing routine first-level document review ---
Entry 79 --- \$665.00.** Tyler Webb (junior associate, \$375/hr) billed
3.5 hours to "Review third batch of MedCore production documents for
relevance and privilege" --- routine first-level review that §6.1(a)
assigns to the contract attorney. The Case Status Report confirms
Mr. Webb "assisted with document review on days of elevated volume." The
entry is reduced to the contract-attorney rate of \$185/hr. Reduction:
3.5 × (\$375 − \$185) = **\$665.00**. (The narrative is also thin --- no
Bates range or coding detail --- though the §6.1 reduction is the
operative remedy.)

> **Subtotal --- §6.1 task-level: \$1,220.00 + \$665.00 = \$1,885.00.**

*Additional §6.1 flags (noted, addressed via block-billing reduction
where applicable).* Entry 9 (Hargrove, 4.5 hrs) combines partner-level
document review with preparation of a client memorandum; the
document-review component is presumptively inappropriate for a partner
under §6.1(a) and should, on resubmission, be billed at the appropriate
lower rate. Several block-billed entries (e.g., 51, 77, 94, 113, 114)
embed administrative "coordinate logistics / filing / scheduling" or
billing-summary components; because those entries are block-billed, the
25% reduction is applied and the administrative portion is flagged for
separation on resubmission. Entry 56 (Pettersson) reflects a mid-level
associate preparing deposition exhibit binders --- work §6.1(c) assigns
to a paralegal or junior associate; while §6.1's mandatory rate
reduction targets partners/senior associates, this over-leveling is an
efficiency concern to monitor.

### F. Administrative Tasks Billed at Attorney Rates --- §6.1(g)--(k), §2 --- \$712.50

Entry 15 (Pettersson, 1.5 hrs / \$712.50): "Schedule depositions of
three remaining witnesses; coordinate court reporter availability;
confirm deposition locations." Scheduling depositions and coordinating
court reporters are Administrative Tasks that §2 defines as
non-compensable overhead and that §6.1(g)--(h) renders presumptively
inappropriate for *any* attorney. The entry is written off in full.

> **Subtotal --- administrative: \$712.50.**

### G. Budget Overage Without Pre-Approval; Missing Reconciliation --- §7.2, §7.3, §9.1

**§7.3 overage.** The Approved Staffing Plan set a monthly fee range of
**\$95,000--\$165,000**, so the maximum payable without pre-approval is
115% of the high end, or **\$189,750**. The fees as submitted
(\$193,372.50 per the Summary; \$215,680.00 per the Detail) **exceed
that ceiling without the written pre-approval §7.3 requires**. No
pre-approval was requested or given. This is a compliance violation.
Because the guideline-compliance reductions in Parts A--F bring the
compliant fees to \$142,643.12 --- *below* the \$189,750 ceiling --- no
*additional* dollar reduction under §7.3 is necessary; the specific
reductions are more stringent than the cap. The §7.3 violation is
nonetheless documented and should be addressed with the Firm.

**§7.2 / §9.1(g) --- missing budget-to-actual reconciliation.** The
invoice contains no budget-to-actual reconciliation (approved range,
actual fees/expenses, cumulative figures, percentage of phase budget
consumed, and narrative of variances \>10%). This is a required invoice
component. The Firm must supply it with the corrected invoice.

**§9.1(a) --- matter number.** The invoice identifies the matter by name
but does not state a Pinnacle matter number. Minor; to be corrected on
resubmission.

**Expense 2 --- Travel (\$2,100.00) disallowed.** The Case Status Report
states the Liu deposition was taken "at the offices of Hargrove & Linden
LLP in Atlanta, Georgia." The "travel" for two Atlanta-based H&L
attorneys to their own firm's office is **local travel within the
Atlanta metropolitan area**, which §6.4 renders "not billable under any
circumstances." The meals component is likewise non-reimbursable: §8.2
allows meals only during approved out-of-town travel or working sessions
past 8:00 p.m., neither of which is indicated. Independently, the
\$2,100 aggregate exceeds the \$1,000 pre-approval threshold of §8.3,
and no pre-approval was obtained. The full \$2,100.00 is disallowed.

**Expense 1 --- Court reporting (\$3,200.00) --- flag, pending
documentation.** Grayson is an approved vendor (Appendix B) and
court-reporter/transcript fees are reimbursable under §8.1, but §8.1
limits transcript reimbursement to "one copy per deposition, at standard
rates." The description references "expedited delivery," a non-standard
premium for which no pre-approval was obtained. The Firm should furnish
an itemized vendor invoice separating the standard
original-plus-one-copy charges from the expedited premium; any expedited
premium is recommended for disallowance absent pre-approval. No dollar
reduction is taken in this memo pending that breakdown.

**Expense 3 --- E-discovery (\$2,650.00) --- allowed.** Tripoint is the
approved e-discovery vendor and the monthly hosting charge is reasonable
for an \~85,000-document production. §8.1 makes e-discovery charges
"subject to pre-approval"; the Firm should retain the pre-approval
documentation and confirm it for future invoices.

**Expense 4 --- Courier (\$500.00) --- allowed.** Messenger/courier
services for time-sensitive service and filing are reimbursable under
§8.1. (Note: to the extent any portion relates to electronic filing that
did not require courier service, it should be separated on future
invoices.)

> **Expense reduction: \$2,100.00. Compliant expenses: \$6,350.00**
> (subject to the court-reporting adjustment once itemized).

## VI. Recommendations and Next Steps

1.  **Do not pay the invoice as submitted.** Return Invoice
    No. HL-2025-05-PIN with the reductions identified above. Provisional
    recommended payable: **\$148,993.12**, contingent on resolution of
    the items below.

2.  **Require a corrected, reconciled invoice (§9.1, §7.2).** The Firm
    must resubmit with (a) a Time Detail that foots to the Summary and
    to the per-timekeeper totals; (b) the omitted budget-to-actual
    reconciliation; and (c) the Pinnacle matter number. The \$22,307.50
    discrepancy must be explained.

3.  **Reject all fees of Nina Barros and Priya Sengupta (§4.2).** If the
    Firm wishes to add either timekeeper prospectively, it must submit a
    written §4.2 staffing-change request (name, role, rate, reason,
    anticipated hours, and budget impact) and obtain written approval
    *before* further work. Note that adding Ms. Barros would require
    amending the "single mid-level associate" term of the Staffing Plan,
    and adding Ms. Sengupta would require amending the "one junior
    associate at a time" term.

4.  **Adjust the contract-attorney rate to \$185/hr** prospectively and
    confirm the Redwood pass-through rate in writing.

5.  **Require advance approval for future depositions exceeding two
    billing timekeepers** (§4.3), requested no later than five business
    days in advance, and limit internal meetings to three billing
    timekeepers.

6.  **Require compliance with §5.4 (no block billing).** The Firm may,
    within 15 days of notice, resubmit the block-billed entries as
    separate, individually timed line items to avoid the 25% reduction;
    resubmitted entries must reasonably account for the total time
    originally reported and must reflect appropriate task-to-timekeeper
    staffing.

7.  **Address the §7.3 budget overage.** Obtain written pre-approval
    before any future month is expected to exceed \$189,750, and require
    the Firm to manage staffing to the approved range.

8.  **Disallow the \$2,100 travel expense** and require an itemized
    court-reporting invoice (Expense 1) to substantiate the
    standard-rate portion and isolate any expedited premium.

9.  **Resubmission deadline.** Request the corrected invoice and any
    §5.4/§9.2 resubmissions within 15 days, consistent with the
    Guidelines' cure periods.
