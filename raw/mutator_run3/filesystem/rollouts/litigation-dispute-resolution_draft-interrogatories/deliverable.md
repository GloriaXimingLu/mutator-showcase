## Drafting approach
- **Directed solely to Veridian**, focusing on its own conduct and knowledge (recruitment, SynapticEdge development, use of CMT information, solicitation of CMT engineers, economics) rather than Oshiro's individual acts.
- **Compliance with the 25-interrogatory cap:** 24 interrogatories served. Interrogatories that touch multiple items (e.g., the five SynapticEdge features in No. 6; the three recruited engineers in No. 15) are phrased as single, unified inquiries with inline lists rather than discrete lettered subparts, consistent with the Court's subpart-counting standard.
- **Proportionality/relevance:** every interrogatory is tied to the identified trade secrets (NeuralPath Algorithm, PrecisionDrive System, PathPlanner Module) or to the claims/defenses, avoiding general business operations or unrelated product lines as the Scheduling Order cautions.
- **Mix of fact and contention interrogatories:** development timeline/personnel (Nos. 1–4, 6, 7), independent-development and trade-secret-status contentions (Nos. 5, 19), knowledge/use of CMT information and the USB drive (Nos. 8–10), recruitment and NDA-awareness (Nos. 11–13), information barriers (No. 14), solicitation of CMT engineers (Nos. 15–16), funding/economics (Nos. 17–18), defenses (No. 20), commercialization (No. 21), return/destruction and spoliation (Nos. 22, 24), and persons with knowledge (No. 23).
- **Formal features:** full caption (Case No. 1:24-cv-03841-RLH, Judge Hightower), definitions, instructions (scope, continuing obligation, objections, privilege/Rule 26(b)(5), protective-order confidentiality), signature block for Hargrove, Pell & Sutton LLP, and a certificate of service on Veridian's counsel (Stephen Osei, Drummond & Wakefield LLP) and Oshiro's counsel (Patricia Dominguez, Barlow & Crane PLLC).
