## 1. `motion-to-dismiss.docx` (19 pages; within the 25-page limit)

A Rule 12(b)(6) motion to dismiss all five counts of the First Amended Complaint, filed as a preserved post-answer motion (Answer, Dkt. No. 14, First Affirmative Defense + Part III reservation). It moves to dismiss each count on independent grounds drawn from the record:

- **Count V (DTPA)** — five grounds: (1) Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.49(f) exemption (total consideration $17.05M ≫ $500,000; $25M asset threshold supported by the FAC's $30M-investment allegation and Arcadia's own $41M damages-report figure); (2) non-actionable puffery (*Pizza Hut*, *Castrol*, *Presidio*) plus the written proposal's express disclaimer of "seamless" integration and the sales presentation's "subject to proper configuration" qualifier; (3) no producing cause (*Melody Home*) — delay caused by Arcadia and Linden Park; (4) Arcadia is not a "consumer" within the DTPA's protective scope (*Riverside Nat'l Bank*); and (5) the § 17.50(a)(2) breach-of-warranty prong is defeated by § 9.1's limited/exclusive warranty and § 9.4's disclaimer, and the § 17.50(a)(3) unconscionability prong is foreclosed by Arcadia's sophistication, counsel, and due diligence.
- **Count IV (Unjust Enrichment)** — barred by the express MSLSA covering the same subject matter (*Fortune Production*; *Excess Underwriters*); the LoL/consequential-damages waiver creates no "gap" equity may fill.
- **Count II (Fraud)** — five independent grounds: Rule 9(b) failure on the "why" prong (*Benchmark*, *Dorsey*) plus the "lumping" defect (*Dorsey*, 540 F.3d at 340) and baseless "on information and belief" concealment (*Flaherty*); economic loss doctrine under both Delaware (*Brasby*, *Kuhn*) and Texas (*Sharyland*, *Chapman*); puffery / no justifiable reliance (*Pizza Hut*, *Presidio*, *Hollcroft*); and the integration clause / anti-reliance doctrine (*Eagle Industries*, *SIGA*), reinforced by Arcadia's withdrawal of its fraud carve-out.
- **Count III (Negligent Misrepresentation)** — no independent duty (*McCamish*); benefit-of-the-bargain damages recoverable only in contract (*Sloane*); economic loss rule; Rule 9(b) applies to claims sounding in fraud; and no justifiable reliance given Arcadia's sophistication, counsel, and due diligence.
- **Count I (Breach of Contract)** — deemed acceptance under § 5.3 (first written complaint 22 days after the Feb. 14, 2023 window closed; *Simulados*, *Precision Healthcare*) plus the independent § 5.3(d) production-use basis; expired 90-day warranty and exclusive remedies (§§ 9.1, 8.3); data-migration responsibility allocated to Arcadia (SOW-1 § 3.2; CO-004's acknowledgment that Linden Park caused the errors); delay caused by Arcadia (45-day PM gap, 75-day-late API specs) and Linden Park. **Alternative:** damages capped by § 8.1 and consequential damages waived by § 8.2 (*Dresser-Rand*, *Kana Software*), with the lost-profits and reputational-harm components independently speculative and unforeseeable (two unnamed prospective clients; Arcadia's own expert concedes the figures are "inherently uncertain" and "inherently more speculative"), eliminating ~$30M of the $47.3M demand.

Includes a certificate of conference and certificate of service per the Standing Order.
