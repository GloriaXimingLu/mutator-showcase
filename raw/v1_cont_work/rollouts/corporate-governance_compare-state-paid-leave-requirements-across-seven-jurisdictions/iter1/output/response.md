# Multi-State Paid Leave Compliance Analysis — Deliverables Summary

**Client:** Vantage Logistics Group, Inc.
**Prepared by:** Hartwell & Cabot LLP (Catherine Ng, Partner; Daniel Ikeda, Senior Associate)
**Engagement:** Multi-State Paid Leave Compliance Review (engagement dated April 14, 2025)
**Deliverable date:** May 19, 2025
**Jurisdictions:** New Jersey, New York, Connecticut (current); Massachusetts, Colorado, Oregon, Washington (expansion)

## Deliverables

### 1. `multi-state-compliance-matrix.xlsx` (8 sheets)
Compares Vantage's current leave policies (Employee Handbook, Rev. Jan 15, 2022) and practices against the statutory/regulatory requirements of all seven jurisdictions, with color-coded gap indicators (red = non-compliant, yellow = at risk/verify, green = compliant). All 7 states appear as columns in every matrix sheet.

- **Cover & Legend** — purpose, sources, legend, reading instructions.
- **Paid Sick Leave** (16 elements) — accrual rate, FT/PT caps, waiting period, carryover, covered uses (own illness/preventive, family care, safe leave/DV-SA-stalking, public health emergency, school-related, bereavement), family-member definition, documentation threshold, payout, posting, recordkeeping.
- **Paid Family Leave** (15 elements) — program/private-plan status, eligibility, duration, wage replacement, weekly cap, qualifying reasons (bonding, caregiving, military exigency, additional reasons), family-member definition, foreseeable/unforeseeable notice, job protection, anti-retaliation, contribution funding.
- **Paid Medical Leave** (5 elements) — availability for own serious health condition, duration, wage replacement/cap, interaction with employer STD (Keystone), employee access/claims routing.
- **Employer Obligations** (8 elements) — registration status/deadlines, employee & employer contribution rates/wage bases, PinnacleHR withholding configuration, recordkeeping, posting/notice, private-plan approval status.
- **Contribution Summary** — estimated annual employee/employer/total contributions per state (NJ $228,830; NY $232,000; CT $144,500; MA ~$68,640; CO ~$45,900; OR ~$62,000; WA ~$33,300; current subtotal $605,330; expansion ~$209,840; grand total ~$815,170), with wage bases and the PinnacleHR capability note.
- **PTO Payout on Separation** (4 elements) — payout requirement, forfeit permissibility, carryover cap, waiting-time penalties.
- **Gap Summary** — consolidated, severity-sorted list of all 263 red/yellow findings (83 Critical, 113 High, 67 Moderate).

### 2. `gap-analysis-memo.docx`
A narrative memorandum identifying all material compliance deficiencies, categorized by severity — **6 Critical, 15 High, 14 Moderate findings** — with legal-risk explanations, three cross-cutting structural analyses, and a prioritized remediation plan of **20 action items** with owners and deadlines tied to the facility-opening schedule (MA 8/1/25, CO 11/1/25, OR 2/1/26, WA 5/1/26) and the July 1, 2025 revised-Handbook target.

**Critical (6):** (1) NJ Earned Sick Leave narrow family-member definition/covered uses — active NJ DOL complaint *Muñoz* (Case No. NJ-ESL-2025-04117), incl. the "own health-related needs"/"to the extent required by law" framing; (2) self-funded PFL structurally non-compliant in all 7 states (below statutory on rate/cap/duration; no private-plan approval; CO no opt-out; incl. the §6.3.7 "intended to satisfy state mandate" conflict and dual-structure confusion); (3) paid medical leave gap with documented HR misdirection to employee-paid Keystone STD (7 inquiries across CT/NJ/NY); (4) MA PFML registration deadline ~June 1, 2025; (5) CO FAMLI registration potentially already overdue (3 April remote hires — ops manager + 2 coordinators; no opt-out); (6) MA PTO payout non-compliance (Wage Act treble damages) before Aug 1, 2025 opening.

**High (15):** sick leave accrual rate (1/40 vs. 1/30 in 5 states); PT cap (24 hrs in all 7); FT cap below statute (NY 56/CO 48/WA no-cap); no-carryover (all 7); 120-day waiting period (5 states); covered-uses/family-member definition (all states, incl. CO HFWA 80-hr supplemental PHE leave); PFL notice (no unforeseeable distinction + 5-business-day response review); PFL qualifying reasons (military exigency/organ donation/safe leave); PFL family-member definition; PFL eligibility; PTO payout CO/OR/WA; OR/CO registration deadlines; manual spreadsheet tracking risk; **PFL AWW calculation methodology** (8-week lookback, base pay only, excludes OT/bonuses/commissions); **PFL intermittent-leave restriction** (full-day increments, supervisor approval, operational-disruption denial).

**Moderate (14):** documentation threshold (2 vs. 3 days) + pattern-of-abuse review; posting/notice; recordkeeping method; NJ/NY PTO payout at risk; PTO carryover cap at risk; bereavement (verify); employee confusion; inconsistent application; outdated handbook + facility-address discrepancies (Newark 1275 Raymond vs 78 Doremus; Queens 56-10 vs 55-20; White Plains 200 Hamilton vs 1200 Westchester; Bridgeport 825 East Main vs 300 Seaview); WA registration timing; waiting-time penalties; key-employee reinstatement exception; Keystone STD extension to expansion states; PinnacleHR configuration dependency (sole blocker is registration).
