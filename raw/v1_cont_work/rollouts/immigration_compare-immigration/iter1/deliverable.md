The beneficiary's **education** (M.S. in Computer Science, NC State, May 2018) and **core post-master's experience** at DataBridge Solutions LLC satisfy the PERM's primary education and 5-year progressive post-master's experience minimums, and the **offered wage ($142,000) exceeds the prevailing wage ($131,747)** with the PWD within its validity period. ## IV. Detailed Gap Analysis and Remedial Recommendations

### A. Certification Requirement (Critical) — Issues 1–4, 16

**1. The required AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification is not held by the beneficiary.**

PERM Section J.B.5 states that the position requires the "AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification or equivalent." Section K.3 of the certified PERM attests that the beneficiary "possess[es] the special skills and other requirements stated in Section J.B.5." The compiled certifications, however, contain only two credentials, and the Certification Verification Notes expressly state that "counsel specifically inquired whether the beneficiary holds the AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification or any other AWS specialty-level certification; the beneficiary confirmed she does not hold any such certification." The beneficiary therefore does not meet this minimum special requirement as attested.

**2. Neither held certification satisfies the "or equivalent" language.**

- **AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner** (Validation No. AWS-CCP-2022-0418-MEHROTRA): This is a **foundational-level** credential that, per the compilation, "does not demonstrate specialization in any specific AWS service domain, including machine learning." It is **not** equivalent to the AWS ML – Specialty certification. Moreover, it **expired on January 18, 2025** — approximately two months **before** the PERM filing date of March 15, 2025 — and the beneficiary has not renewed it. It was therefore expired at the time of filing and at certification.
- **TensorFlow Developer Certificate** (Validation No. TF-DEV-2023-0310-AM, issued March 10, 2023, no expiration): This is issued by **Google**, is **framework-specific to TensorFlow**, and "is not affiliated with Amazon Web Services (AWS)." It validates TensorFlow model-building skill, not AWS-based ML deployment or the breadth of the AWS ML – Specialty scope. It is not an "equivalent."

Accordingly, the "or equivalent" alternative is not satisfied by either credential.

**3. The K.3 attestation is inconsistent with the credential record.**

Because J.B.5 includes the AWS ML – Specialty certification as a required special skill and K.3 attests that the beneficiary possesses all such special skills, the K.3 attestation is inconsistent with the documented credentials. Under 20 CFR Part 656, material misstatements in a PERM application can subject a certification to revocation and the employer (and beneficiary) to further consequences; the alien declaration on the form itself references potential penalties under 18 U.S.C. § 1546. This is the most significant exposure in the file.

**4. The certification requirement may not be "normal to the occupation."**

Under 20 CFR 656.17(h), a requirement that is not normal to the occupation must be justified by business necessity (i.e., job-relatedness and consistency with business necessity). A specific vendor specialty certification is arguably not normal to the Data Scientist occupation (SOC 15-2051). If the application is audited, DOL may challenge whether the certification requirement is normal and business-necessary — a vulnerability that is compounded by the fact that the beneficiary does not hold the credential.

**5. The recruitment did not screen applicants for the required certification (Issue 16).** The PERM recruitment report (Section G.3) states that three (3) U.S. applicants were received and that all three were found unqualified — two (2) for "lacking the required educational credentials" and one (1) for "lacking the required minimum years of experience." None was rejected for lacking the AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification, even though that certification is stated as a minimum special requirement in J.B.5. If the certification is a genuine minimum requirement, it should have been applied as a screening criterion against U.S. applicants; the report's silence on certification-based rejection raises a "requirements not as stated" exposure under 20 CFR 656.17 and is a distinct audit vulnerability from the beneficiary's own lack of the credential. We further note that the full underlying recruitment documentation (the SWA job order TX-8247163, the two Austin American-Statesman Sunday advertisements of December 8 and 22, 2024, and the additional recruitment steps) is not in the transmitted package; the HR email states it is on file with the employer and Broadleaf Staffing Advisors and available on request.

**Remedial recommendations (Priority 1):**

1. **Counsel the employer on the attestation discrepancy.** We will advise Prismex in writing of the inconsistency between K.3 and the credential record and the resulting audit, revocation, and I-140 exposure, and document the employer's informed decision on how to proceed.
2. **Obtain the AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification for the beneficiary as quickly as practicable.** *Caution on timing:* for PERM purposes the beneficiary must have met the minimum requirements **at the time of filing** (March 15, 2025), and USCIS evaluates I-140 qualification as of the PERM filing date. A certification obtained after filing does not retroactively cure the K.3 attestation as of the filing date and may not cure the I-140 qualification analysis. We will assess whether the certification can be earned before I-140 filing/adjudication and how best to present it.
3. **Assemble business-necessity documentation** for the certification requirement (job-relatedness to the healthcare/fintech ML-deployment duties and business necessity), in anticipation of audit.
4. **Review the recruitment record for consistency with the certification requirement (Issue 16).** Confirm whether the AWS ML – Specialty certification was actually applied as a screening criterion against U.S. applicants, and evaluate any "requirements not as stated" exposure given that the recruitment report (G.3) cites only education- and experience-based rejections.
5. **Evaluate post-certification correction options.** DOL permits only limited amendments after certification; a substantive change to the minimum requirements generally cannot be made post-certification and would require refiling (new PWD and recruitment), which is in tension with the September 15, 2025 STEM OPT deadline. We will weigh refiling, proceeding with documented audit risk, and obtaining the certification, and recommend a path.
6. **Do not file the I-140** until the certification issue is resolved or a documented, informed risk decision is made by the employer.
7. **Request and review the full underlying recruitment file** (SWA job order TX-8247163; the two Austin American-Statesman Sunday advertisements of December 8 and 22, 2024; and the additional recruitment steps) from the employer and Broadleaf Staffing Advisors — this documentation is not in the transmitted package but is on file and available on request (per the HR email) — and reconcile it with the J.B.5 certification requirement (Issue 16).

### B. R Programming Proficiency (Material) — Issue 5

PERM Section J.B.5(e) and the job description (H.10) require "proficiency in Python and R programming languages." The record documents **expert** Python proficiency but characterizes R only as **"intermediate"** (résumé) and **"some use of R for statistical reporting and ad hoc data analysis"** (DataBridge letter). "Some use"/"intermediate" may not rise to "proficiency" and could be challenged as insufficient to meet the attested minimum. We note, however, that the record does contain some academic support for R competency: the NC State transcript shows the beneficiary completed **ST 558, "Statistical Computing with R" (grade A-, Spring 2017)**, and the résumé lists "Statistical Computing with R" among her relevant coursework. This formal coursework provides partial support for R competency, but because J.B.5(e) frames the requirement as experience-based ("Experience must include… proficiency in Python and R"), academic coursework alone may not satisfy it, and the professional-experience evidence of R remains thin.

**Remedial recommendations (Priority 2):**

1. Obtain **supplemental evidence of R proficiency** — e.g., an amended or supplemental experience letter from DataBridge detailing specific R-based deliverables, packages, and models; R-based project artifacts; or R training/certification.
2. If the beneficiary's R skills are genuinely proficient, document them with specifics (statistical reporting products, R packages used, models built in R).
3. If R proficiency cannot be substantiated at the "proficiency" level, assess whether "intermediate"/"some use" is defensible (likely insufficient) and whether the requirement should be revisited for audit-defense purposes. Note that R is more normal to the Data Scientist occupation than the AWS certification, so the requirement itself is more defensible; the issue is the level of supporting evidence.

Because the PWD was issued (November 8, 2024) before the PERM filing (March 15, 2025), and the PERM is certified under FEIN 84-3291756, this discrepancy must be reconciled before the I-140 is filed, as USCIS will verify the petitioner's identity against the certified PERM.

**Remedial recommendations (Priority 2):**

1. Obtain the employer's **IRS FEIN confirmation** (e.g., CP 575 notice or a recent tax filing/payroll record from Cornerpoint Payroll Services) to establish the correct FEIN.
2. **Reconcile** the PERM and PWD FEINs. If the PERM FEIN (84-3291756) is correct, document the PWD discrepancy (the PWD is employer-input-based; the certified PERM FEIN governs for the I-140). If the PERM FEIN is erroneous, assess correction/explanation options for the I-140 and the audit exposure.
3. Ensure the **I-140 petitioner FEIN matches the certified PERM**; prepare an explanation of the PWD discrepancy for the record.

### G. Experience Calculation and Characterization (Low) — Issues 10–11

**Issue 10 — "Approximately 7 years" precision.** Section K.2 states the beneficiary has "approximately 7 years of post-master's experience at DataBridge Solutions LLC (June 2018 – present)." Measured precisely, the DataBridge tenure is approximately **6 years and 9 months** as of the March 15, 2025 filing date, and approximately **6 years and 11.5 months** as of the May 22, 2025 certification date. Both figures exceed the 5-year minimum, but "approximately 7 years" slightly overstates the figure as of filing. We recommend using precise figures in the petition narrative.

**Issue 11 — Source of the post-master's experience.** The 5-year progressive *post-master's* requirement is met **solely** by the DataBridge experience (June 4, 2018 – present). The Evalpoint experience (July 2013 – July 2016) is **pre-master's** and is, per the Evalpoint letter, primarily backend software engineering in which ML exposure was "ancillary and secondary." The NC State RA experience (August 2016 – May 2018) is **pre-master's and part-time (20 hrs/week)**. Neither counts toward the post-master's requirement. The petition narrative should make clear that the qualifying post-master's experience is the DataBridge tenure only, and should not conflate the pre-master's roles with the post-master's qualifying experience.

**Remedial recommendations (Priority 4):** Use precise post-master's figures; clearly attribute the 5-year qualifying experience to DataBridge alone; avoid relying on (or implying reliance on) the pre-master's, part-time, or non-ML roles to satisfy the post-master's minimum.

### H. Supervisory Scope (Low / Observation) — Issue 12

The position requires supervising "a team of 3–5 ML engineers" (J.B.5; H.10). The beneficiary's documented supervisory experience is leading "a team of 2 junior data scientists" at DataBridge. The supervision of 3–5 engineers is phrased in J.B.5 as a forward-looking job condition ("Will supervise a team of 3–5 ML engineers") rather than as a prior-experience prerequisite ("Experience must include…"), so it is most likely **not** a credential gap. We flag it only so that counsel can confirm the employer's intent.

**Remedial recommendations (Priority 4):** Confirm that supervision of 3–5 engineers is a job condition (not a prior-experience requirement). If so, no further action is required beyond ensuring the I-140 job description matches the certified PERM. If the employer intended prior supervisory experience over 3–5 as a requirement, note that the documented scope (2) is below 3–5 and assess audit exposure.

**Remedial recommendations (Priority 4):** Reconcile the phone number; note the letter timing (acceptable); distinguish the résumé's total-experience figure from the PERM's post-master's figure in the petition narrative; reconcile the DataBridge Data Scientist I end date to match the official "January 3, 2021" (Issue 17); and assemble the ability-to-pay documentation from Cornerpoint Payroll Services before I-140 filing (Issue 18).
