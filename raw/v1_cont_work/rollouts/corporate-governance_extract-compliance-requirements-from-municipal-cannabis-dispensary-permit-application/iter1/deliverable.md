## 1. Executive Summary

This matrix assesses the Greenleaf Therapeutics LLC dispensary permit application package against every distinct requirement of Article 5 of the Evanston Municipal Code and the City's Application Checklist and Submission Guide. Each requirement is scored on its own line with a status, the evidentiary basis in the record, and a remediation note.

**Overarching finding — the application form itself is unexecuted.** The *Cannabis Dispensary Permit Application* (Form v3.2) included in the package is a blank template: every field in Sections 1–14 is uncompleted, the certification block (Section 14) is unsigned, and the notarization block is unexecuted. Although the supporting documents (business plan, lease summary, insurance/bonding memo, community-engagement report, and State conditional license) contain much of the underlying substance, the Code (§9-5-5(B)(1)) and the Guide (Item 1) require a *completed, signed, and notarized* application form. Until the Form is filled in, signed, and notarized, the package cannot be deemed complete. This single gap propagates across nearly every line item below and is reflected in the "Form blank" notes throughout.

**Critical substantive deficiencies (would render the application incomplete or materially weaken it):**

1. **Odor Mitigation Plan — no P.E. certification and no engineered specifications.** The Form (Q16) and Guide (Item 10) require a Professional Engineer's certification, signed and sealed by an Illinois-licensed P.E., *at the time of submission*, and the Form expressly states the application "will be deemed incomplete and will not proceed to scoring" without it. The business plan instead defers all HVAC/carbon-filtration specifications to "the build-out engineering phase" and provides no P.E. certification. *(Non-compliant.)*
2. **Security Plan not finalized.** The insurance/bonding memo confirms the Pinnacle Compliance Advisors security plan is "still being finalized … and has not yet been delivered." The business plan contains only an overview. A finalized security plan is a required submission item (Item 9) and is a prerequisite to binding the cannabis crime/theft policy. *(Non-compliant.)*
3. **Cannabis-Specific Crime/Theft insurance — not bound, no binder/commitment letter.** The Guide (Item 16) permits a binding commitment letter at application stage; only a "preliminary indication" from Canopy Risk Underwriters exists, and underwriting is blocked pending the security plan. *(Non-compliant at application; blocking for permit issuance.)*
4. **No evidence of bonding capacity.** Only a "preliminary inquiry" with Thornfield Surety Group; no commitment letter. The Form's scoring rubric awards 10 of 30 Financial Viability points for secured bonding capacity and states applicants who do not provide evidence "will receive zero (0) points in this sub-category." Guide Item 17 also requires evidence of bonding capacity at submission. *(Non-compliant; scoring penalty.)*
5. **Community-engagement Session 2 notice was only 12 days.** Notice for the April 9, 2024 session was published March 28, 2024 — 12 calendar days, below the 14-day minimum of Code §9-5-8(B). The engagement report itself records "12 days" and omits the compliance statement it supplied for Session 1. Code §9-5-8(F) renders non-compliant notice a completeness defect and bars waiver of the two-session minimum. *(Non-compliant; a third, properly-noticed session is advisable.)*
6. **Buffer-distance certification is stale and uses the wrong methodology.** The Meridian survey is dated Dec. 15, 2023 (the Form requires surveys dated within 60 days of submission; a May 15, 2024 filing makes it ~5 months old) and measures *straight-line* distances, while the Form (Q6/Q7) expressly requires *nearest-pedestrian-route* measurement and rejects straight-line. The underlying distances (school 1,180 ft; daycare 890 ft; dispensary 2,100 ft) all satisfy the Code minimums, but the certification as submitted does not satisfy the Form. *(Non-compliant.)*
7. **State conditional-license expiration timeline risk.** The conditional license expires Sept. 21, 2024. The City's own 120–150 day estimate from a May 15, 2024 submission places City Council action as late as mid-October 2024 — *after* the conditional license expires — and the 180-day conversion clock cannot start until local permit issuance. No State extension petition (available up to 6 months if filed ≥60 days before expiration, i.e., by ~July 23, 2024) is documented. *(Critical timeline risk.)*
8. **No standalone Diversity & Inclusion Plan or Environmental Sustainability Plan.** Both are required as standalone submission items (Guide Items 14, 15; Form Q32/Q33). Only business-plan narrative exists; the social-equity hiring pipeline identifies no formalized workforce-development partners, no timeline, and no contingency plan as required by Code §9-5-20(C). *(Non-compliant / partial.)*
9. **Social-equity supporting documentation not submitted.** R3 residency proof (with census-tract verification and 24-month duration), the expungement court order, and the signed 50%-R3-hiring and 2-year-mentorship commitments (Guide Item 18) are not in the package; the Form's social-equity sections (Q19–Q21) are blank. The 24-month continuous R3 residency required by Code §9-5-19(B)(1) is not documented. *(Not submitted.)*

**Cross-cutting issue — internal inconsistencies among the Code, the Guide, and the Form.** The three governing documents conflict on several points that directly affect preparation: the deficiency cure period (Code 30 days vs. Guide/Form 14 days); acceptable fee instruments; the Certificate of Good Standing freshness window (60 vs. 30 days); the list of qualifying zoning districts (Code: C1a/C2/oC1 vs. Form: C1a/C1/B2); the maximum-occupancy calculation base (Code: retail floor area vs. Form: total dispensary area); the operating-hours citation (Form cites §9-5-17; the rule is §9-5-13(F)); Employer's Liability limits (Code $1M vs. lease $500K vs. Guide/Form silent); the SSN disclosure (Code: full vs. Form: last 4); Agent-in-Charge availability (Code: 30-minute return vs. Form: by telephone); the social-equity workforce definition; and the felony lookback period (City 10 years vs. State 5 years); an internal Code conflict on the insurance-lapse immediate-suspension threshold (§9-5-16(E): 14 days vs. §9-5-25(C)(3): 48 hours); and a citation error in the Form (Q22 cites §9-5-15, the community-benefit section, for the community-engagement requirement, which is actually §9-5-8). These are catalogued in Section 5 and Section 6 and should be reconciled with the Cannabis Licensing Coordinator before filing.

**Data inconsistencies within the package** (registered-agent name, applicant telephone/email, parking-space count, City contact coordinates) are catalogued in Section 7 and must be reconciled across documents before submission.

## 2. Scope and Methodology

- **Records reviewed:** (1) Evanston Municipal Code Ch. 9, Art. 5; (2) Application Checklist and Submission Guide; (3) Cannabis Dispensary Permit Application Form v3.2; (4) Greenleaf Business Plan Summary; (5) Community Engagement Sessions Report; (6) Insurance & Bonding Status Memo; (7) Lease Summary — Key Terms; (8) State Conditional Adult-Use Dispensing Organization License ADC-285-0147.
- **Unit of analysis:** each distinct statutory/guide requirement is assessed on its own line. Where the Code, Guide, and Form impose overlapping or differing versions of a requirement, each material variant is captured separately so no graded point is bundled.
- **Status vocabulary:** **COMPLIANT** (requirement met, evidence in record); **PARTIAL** (some elements met, gaps remain); **NON-COMPLIANT** (requirement violated or not met); **NOT SUBMITTED** (required item absent from package); **NOT CONFIRMED** (record does not evidence the required fact); **PENDING** (obligation arises at/after permit issuance; acknowledged but not yet due); **DISCREPANCY** (conflict among governing documents); **N/A** (not applicable at application stage).
- **Citations:** "Code §9-5-X" = Municipal Code; "Guide Item N" = Submission Guide Master Checklist; "Form QN" = Application Form question. Supporting-document references use the document name and section.
