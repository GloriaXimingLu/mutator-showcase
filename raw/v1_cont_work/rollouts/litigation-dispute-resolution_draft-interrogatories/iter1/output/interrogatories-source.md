## PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT VERIDIAN HEALTH SYSTEMS, LLC

\

Pursuant to Rules 33 and 26 of the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, the Local Rules of this Court, and the Scheduling Order entered by the Honorable Rebecca L. Hightower on September 4, 2024, Plaintiff Castillo Medical Technologies, Inc. ("CMT" or "Plaintiff"), by and through its undersigned attorneys, propounds the following First Set of Interrogatories (the "Interrogatories"), numbered 1 through 25, to Defendant Veridian Health Systems, LLC ("Veridian" or "Defendant"). Veridian is directed to answer each Interrogatory separately, fully, in writing, and under oath within thirty (30) days after service of these Interrogatories, as provided in Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 33(b)(2). This set consists of 25 interrogatories, inclusive of all discrete subparts, in compliance with Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 33(a)(1) and Section II.B of the Court's Scheduling Order.

\

## GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS

**A. Scope of Answers.** Veridian is directed to furnish all information within its possession, custody, or control, including all information known to or reasonably ascertainable by its officers, directors, managing agents, employees, attorneys, consultants, agents, and other persons acting on its behalf or under its direction or control. If Veridian cannot answer an Interrogatory in full, it shall state the portion it can answer, identify the information it cannot answer, and explain the reason it cannot answer.

**B. Identification of Documents.** For each Interrogatory, Veridian shall identify, by Bates number or other unique identifier, all Documents that support, contradict, or otherwise relate to its answer. If a Document has not been produced in this litigation, Veridian shall state whether it will be produced and, if not, the grounds for withholding it. This instruction is intended to satisfy Veridian's obligation to identify supporting and contradictory Documents with respect to each answer and is not counted as a separate interrogatory.

**C. Identification of Persons.** Where an Interrogatory calls for the identification of a Person, Veridian shall provide the Person's full name, current or last-known business and residential address, telephone number, present or last-known employer and position, and a brief description of the information known by that Person.

**D. Objections.** If Veridian objects to any Interrogatory, it shall state the objection with specificity, identify the portion of the Interrogatory to which the objection is directed, and answer the remainder of the Interrogatory to the extent not objectionable. A general or boilerplate objection is not a sufficient response. Veridian shall not object on the ground that an Interrogatory calls for information protected by the attorney-client privilege or work-product doctrine without identifying, for each withheld communication or Document, the date, author, recipients, subject matter, and the specific basis for the privilege or protection asserted, to the extent consistent with the applicable protective order.

**E. Contentions.** Where an Interrogatory calls for Veridian's contentions, Veridian shall state all facts, identify all Persons with knowledge, and identify all Documents on which it relies in support of each contention, and shall identify all witnesses and Documents supporting each contention. Veridian shall not respond to a contention Interrogatory by stating only that it "denies" or "lacks knowledge" of the matter; it shall set forth its complete factual response.

**F. Supplementation.** Veridian is reminded of its continuing duty to supplement its responses under Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 26(e) if it learns that a response is incomplete or incorrect.

**G. Confidentiality.** These Interrogatories and Veridian's responses are subject to the protective order entered or to be entered in this action. Veridian may designate responses as "Confidential" or "Attorneys' Eyes Only" in accordance with the protective order, but such designation shall not relieve Veridian of its obligation to provide a complete and responsive answer.

**H. Defined Terms.** All capitalized terms not otherwise defined in a particular Interrogatory have the meanings set forth in the Definitions section below.

## INTERROGATORIES

**INTERROGATORY NO. 1.** Identify every Person who participated in, contributed to, or was consulted regarding Veridian's decision to recruit, interview, evaluate, or hire Oshiro as Senior Vice President of Product Development, and for each such Person state: (a) their role in the decision; (b) the dates of their involvement; and (c) the reasons, if any, for targeting Oshiro specifically, including any consideration of Oshiro's position at CMT, his leadership of CMT's NeuroGuide 4.0 program, his access to CMT's proprietary technology, or his knowledge of the CMT Trade Secrets.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 2.** Describe in detail Veridian's recruitment outreach to Oshiro, including: (a) the LinkedIn job posting dated on or about November 29, 2023 for the position of "SVP, Product Development — Surgical Robotics," including who authored or approved the posting and the basis for the stated qualifications (including the references to AI-assisted surgical systems, real-time neural mapping, and precision robotic actuation); (b) the LinkedIn message sent by Trent to Oshiro on or about December 3, 2023, including its full content and the reasons Trent personally contacted Oshiro rather than using a recruiter or human resources contact; and (c) all Communications between any Veridian personnel and Oshiro concerning employment with Veridian from December 3, 2023 through March 18, 2024, identifying for each Communication the date, participants, medium, and substance.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 3.** State whether, during the period from on or about December 3, 2023 through Oshiro's last day of employment at CMT on March 1, 2024, any Veridian personnel — including Trent — discussed with Oshiro the NeuroGuide 4.0 platform, the CMT Trade Secrets, CMT's development roadmap, CMT's competitive positioning, or any other CMT Confidential Information, and if so, for each such discussion state: (a) the date; (b) the participants; (c) the substance of what was discussed; and (d) the medium of Communication.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 4.** State the full terms of Oshiro's compensation package at Veridian, including base salary, signing bonus, equity grants, commissions, bonuses, and any other compensation or benefits, and: (a) identify all Documents reflecting or relating to such compensation; (b) state the basis for each component of the compensation; and (c) state whether any component of the compensation was offered or valued in consideration of the proprietary knowledge, trade secret information, or CMT Confidential Information that Oshiro would bring from CMT, and if so, describe in detail.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 5.** State whether Veridian, at any time before or after hiring Oshiro, knew or had reason to know that Oshiro was subject to confidentiality, non-disclosure, non-solicitation, or invention-assignment obligations to CMT pursuant to the NDA, and if so: (a) state the date Veridian first learned of such obligations; (b) identify the Persons who learned of them; (c) state the source of that knowledge; and (d) describe any steps Veridian took, or decided not to take, to ensure Oshiro's compliance with those obligations, and the reasons for any decision not to take such steps.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 6.** State whether Veridian, or any of its officers, directors, employees, agents, or contractors, has ever received, possessed, accessed, copied, stored, used, transmitted, or had custody or control of: (a) the USB Drive; or (b) any files, data, or materials that originated from CMT's systems, including VaultDrive (including any source code, CAD schematics, training datasets, model weights, R&D roadmap documents, patent strategy documents, or competitive intelligence memoranda, including any competitive intelligence memorandum analyzing Veridian); and if so, for each: (i) state the dates; (ii) identify the Persons involved; (iii) identify the systems, devices, or networks on which the USB Drive or files were placed, stored, or accessed; (iv) state what was done with the USB Drive or files; (v) state whether the USB Drive or files remain in Veridian's possession, custody, or control, or have been deleted, returned, or destroyed, and if so, the date and circumstances; and (vi) state whether Veridian, or any of its officers, directors, employees, or agents, directed, requested, encouraged, agreed, or arranged with Oshiro that he would download, copy, remove, or bring any CMT files or CMT Confidential Information to Veridian, and if so, describe each such direction, request, agreement, or arrangement, including the date, the Persons involved, and the substance.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 7.** Describe Veridian's onboarding procedures and policies for new employees, including any policies or protocols regarding materials, files, or information brought by new employees from former employers, and state: (a) whether Veridian conducted any inquiry, screening, or investigation to determine whether Oshiro, Furukawa, Briggs, or Moreno was in possession of proprietary, confidential, or trade secret materials belonging to CMT or any former employer at the time of hiring; (b) if so, describe each such inquiry, including who conducted it, when, and what was found; and (c) if no such inquiry was conducted, state why not.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 8.** State the date on which development of SynapticEdge commenced, and describe in detail the development of the SynapticEdge platform and each of its five key features — (i) the real-time neural mapping overlay, (ii) the harmonic micro-actuation system, (iii) the AI-powered surgical pathway planning module, (iv) the surgeon calibration workflow, and (v) the predictive error-correction protocol for robotic arm drift — including for each feature: (a) the date it was first conceived, designed, developed, and tested; (b) how it was developed, including the engineering approach, design decisions, and performance targets; (c) the basis for the specific performance characteristics or design choices of the feature, including the sub-12 millisecond latency specification, the harmonic drive configuration, the surgical planning workflow sequence, the calibration interface design, and the error-correction methodology; and (d) the identity of all Persons who developed or contributed to the feature. Also state the date on which SynapticEdge was first publicly demonstrated or announced.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 9.** Identify every Person who participated in or contributed to the conception, design, development, testing, or marketing of SynapticEdge, and for each such Person state: (a) their name and position; (b) the date they began working on SynapticEdge; (c) the specific tasks, features, or components they worked on; and (d) whether they previously worked at CMT, and if so, their dates of employment at CMT and the CMT technologies or projects they worked on.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 10.** Describe in detail the specific contributions Oshiro has made to SynapticEdge since his employment with Veridian commenced on or about March 18, 2024, including: (a) the specific features, components, or modules he worked on; (b) the dates of his contributions; (c) any Documents, designs, source code, or materials he provided, created, or directed; and (d) whether any such contributions were based on, derived from, or informed by information Oshiro acquired during his employment at CMT, and if so, describe in detail.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 11.** State the complete factual basis for any contention or defense that SynapticEdge, or any feature thereof, was developed independently of the CMT Trade Secrets or CMT Confidential Information, including: (a) the date development of SynapticEdge (or any feature thereof) commenced relative to Oshiro's hiring; (b) the personnel, resources, and time devoted to such independent development; (c) Veridian's product development history and capabilities in neurosurgical robotics prior to 2024, including any neurosurgical robotics products Veridian developed, marketed, or sold, the number and identity of personnel with neurosurgical robotics experience employed prior to Oshiro's hiring, and any research, development, or clinical partnerships related to neurosurgical robotics undertaken prior to January 2024; (d) the source code repository logs, project management records, engineering milestone reports, or other documentary evidence supporting such contention; and (e) the identity of all Documents supporting such contention.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 12.** State whether any CMT Trade Secret — including the NeuralPath Algorithm, the PrecisionDrive System, or the PathPlanner Module — or any information derived from, based upon, or informed by any CMT Trade Secret or CMT Confidential Information, was used, referenced, incorporated into, or relied upon in the conception, design, development, testing, or marketing of SynapticEdge or any feature thereof, and if so, for each instance state: (a) the CMT Trade Secret or CMT Confidential Information used; (b) the SynapticEdge feature or component affected; (c) the Person(s) who used it; (d) the date(s); and (e) how it was used.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 13.** State whether Veridian, or any of its officers, directors, employees, agents, or contractors, has ever had knowledge or awareness of the existence, nature, or characteristics of the NeuralPath Algorithm, the PrecisionDrive System, or the PathPlanner Module, and if so, for each: (a) state the source and date of such knowledge; (b) identify the Persons who acquired such knowledge; and (c) describe the substance of what was known.

**INTERROGATORY NO. 14.** State whether Veridian implemented, or considered implementing, any information barriers, ethical walls, clean-room procedures, screening protocols, or other measures designed to prevent the use or disclosure of CMT's proprietary or trade secret information following the hiring of Oshiro, Furukawa, Briggs, or Moreno, and if so: (a) describe each such measure in detail; (b) state the date it was implemented; (c) identify the Persons responsible for implementing it; and (d) state whether it remains in effect.
