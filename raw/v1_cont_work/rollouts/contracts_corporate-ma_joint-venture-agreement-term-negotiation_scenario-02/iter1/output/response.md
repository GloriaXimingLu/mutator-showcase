## Completeness Audit (performed after first draft)

A mandatory completeness audit was run against the source record. The audit re-listed every distinct issue/requirement the source documents raise and cross-checked the deliverable by content. The following gaps were identified and filled (each is a distinct, record-supported point that the first draft had omitted or only implied):

1. **Forum-selection clause (Provision 3)** — the enforceability memo recommends a *mandatory* forum-selection clause directing non-compete enforcement to the Delaware Court of Chancery or Harris County, Texas courts. First draft mentioned it only in rationale; **added as ready-to-insert clause language** (new Section 14.04(b)).
2. **Early-exit scaling provision (Provision 3)** — the memo recommends scaling the restraint to 12 months for exits within the first 24 months after formation (protectable interest is commensurate with JV operating duration). **Added as clause language** (new Section 14.04(a)).
3. **Hybrid revenue-threshold geography (Provision 3)** — the memo specifically recommends "US, Canada, + any jurisdiction where MKPoly generated >$500,000 in the 24 months preceding exit" to satisfy California §16602.5. **Added as an optional refinement** (Section 14.04(d)) the parties may adopt.
4. **Choice-of-law confirmation (Provision 3)** — memo recommends confirming Delaware law governs the non-compete. **Added** (Section 14.04(c)).
5. **Case authority (Provision 3 rationale)** — added the specific supporting citations the memo provides: *Peat Marwick v. Haass* (18-mo, TX), *Boulden v. Stilwell* (18-mo LLC, DE), *Marsh USA v. Cook* (2-yr, TX), *EDIX Media v. Mahani* (2-yr, DE), *Edwards v. Arthur Andersen* (CA voids), *Fillpoint v. Maas* and *Kelton v. Stravinski* (CA exceptions).
6. **Reformation/blue-pencil doctrines by jurisdiction (Provision 3 rationale)** — added the jurisdiction-specific differences (DE discretionary modification; TX mandatory §15.51 with §15.51(c) fee-shifting; NY blue-pencil; CA no reformation/void entirely).
7. **California preemptive declaratory-judgment risk (Provision 3 rationale)** — added the memo's specific risk that a member could file a preemptive CA action and the forum-selection clause mitigates but does not eliminate this.
8. **Restricted Competitors list discrepancy (Provision 5)** — flagged that the v.4 draft Schedule 12.05 lists global coatings majors (PPG, Sherwin-Williams, Akzo Nobel, etc.) while a separately proposed list identifies North American specialists (Polaris, Endurance, Trident, etc.); these must be reconciled by the principals. **Added as an explicit open item** in both Provision 3 and Provision 5 cross-references.
9. **Implied dollar premium figure (foundational context)** — added the specific $4,785,000 figure from the capital contribution schedule Note 1 (Kastor contributes 50.52% of capital but holds 45%).
