# Closing Deliverables — Whitmore Capital Partners VII, LP (Final Closing)

## Deliverables produced

1. **`final-closing-certificate.docx`** — Final Closing Certificate of the General Partner (Whitmore Capital Management LLC), dated January 31, 2025, certifying each of the ten (10) conditions precedent under Section 11.2 of the Amended and Restated LPA (as amended by Amendment No. 1), plus the Key Person certification required by Section 9.4(d), the GP representations bring-down under Section 11.2(10), and additional certifications (GP Commitment, Investment Company Act 3(c)(7) exemption, SEC registration, service providers). Includes Exhibit 1 — an updated Schedule A listing all 33 Limited Partners and the General Partner across the three closings, with commitment reconciliation, hard-cap compliance, and the ERISA/Benefit Plan Investor summary.

2. **`closing-issues-memo.docx`** — Closing Issues Memorandum from Fund Counsel (Bridgeford Hollis LLP) flagging 36 distinct discrepancies and unresolved issues found across the eight source documents, organized into (III) outstanding/unsupported conditions precedent, (IV) material discrepancies among source documents, and (V) post-closing action items, with recommendations.

## Source documents reviewed

LPA (A&R, dated Sept 15, 2024); Amendment No. 1 (dated Nov 30, 2024); Subscription Agreement template (Final Closing); Capital Call Notice No. 1 (Oct 10, 2024); Capital Call Notice No. 2 (Dec 15, 2024); GP Internal Closing Memorandum (Jan 28, 2025); AML/KYC completion email (Jan 24, 2025); Admin Closing Checklist (with Commitment Schedule and Equalization Calc tabs).

## Highest-priority issues flagged in the memo

- **ERISA / Benefit Plan Investor percentage (Item 1):** three different figures appear (19.8% in the GP Memo; 22.00% in the Subscription Agreement/Checklist ERISA Analysis; 28.80% implied by the Checklist's own "ERISA Plan Investor" Y-flags). The 28.80% figure would *exceed* the 24.99% cap in Section 6.8 — a non-waivable condition. The treatment of governmental plans is inconsistent between the LPA's Article I definition and the Subscription Agreement's Box C.
- **AML/KYC coverage (Item 2):** the AML/KYC completion email lists only 5 of 6 Final Closing LPs and omits Cascade Re Insurance Ltd. (Bermuda) — a non-waivable condition.
- **Distribution waterfall (Item 3):** Amendment No. 1's "correction" to Section 8.3(b) does not match the actual LPA text (8.3(b) is the Preferred Return tier; the catch-up is 8.3(c), which already contains the corrected language).
- **Outstanding conditions (Items 4–6):** escrow funding, legal opinions, and the updated Schedule A were not finalized as of the Jan 30 checklist update.
- **Key Person support gap (Item 34):** the GP Memo (support for Condition 6) certifies only Marcus J. Whitmore and is silent as to Priya S. Mehta, the second Key Person required to be certified under Section 9.4(d)/11.2(6).
- **Side letter status (Item 35):** the Subscription Agreement says Meridian's and Sentry's side letters are "Expected" and Northbridge's is "To be determined," while the Admin Checklist flags Meridian and Northbridge as executed and Sentry as none.
- **Individual LP commitment discrepancies (Items 7–8):** Silvermark, Commonwealth, and Graystone (the three Advisory Committee member LPs) and Birchwood Insurance Co. have inconsistent stated commitments across the documents.
