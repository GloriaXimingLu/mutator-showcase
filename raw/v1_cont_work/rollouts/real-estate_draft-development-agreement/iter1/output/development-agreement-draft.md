## ARTICLE 3. THE PROJECT

### Section 3.1 Project Description.

The Project consists of a mixed-use, transit-oriented development organized in four phases, with a total gross building area of approximately 1,620,000 square feet, comprising the program elements set forth in this Article 3 and in Exhibit [__] (Project Description and Phasing Plan). The Project is designed as a cohesive, master-planned transit-oriented development serving as a catalyst for revitalization of the Lakemont Central station area.

### Section 3.2 Phase I — Residential Tower A and Retail Podium.

Phase I comprises: (a) three hundred twenty (320) residential dwelling units, consisting of two hundred fifty-six (256) market-rate units and sixty-four (64) Affordable Units; (b) forty-five thousand (45,000) square feet of ground-floor retail space; and (c) a four hundred eighty (480)-space structured parking facility. Phase I comprises approximately four hundred twenty thousand (420,000) square feet of gross building area, with an estimated hard construction cost of $105,000,000. Phase I groundbreaking shall occur no later than March 1, 2026, with Substantial Completion by September 30, 2028 (approximately thirty (30) months of construction).

### Section 3.3 Phase II — Office Building and Retail.

Phase II comprises: (a) two hundred eighty thousand (280,000) square feet of Class A office space; (b) eighteen thousand (18,000) square feet of retail space; and (c) a three hundred fifty (350)-space structured parking facility. Phase II comprises approximately three hundred eighty thousand (380,000) square feet of gross building area, with an estimated hard construction cost of $76,000,000. Phase II shall commence within twelve (12) months of Phase I Substantial Completion, with Substantial Completion within twenty-four (24) months of Phase II commencement.

### Section 3.4 Phase III — Residential Tower B and Hotel.

Phase III comprises: (a) two hundred ten (210) residential dwelling units, consisting of one hundred sixty-eight (168) market-rate units and forty-two (42) Affordable Units; and (b) a one hundred fifty (150)-room select-service hotel. Phase III comprises approximately four hundred forty thousand (440,000) square feet of gross building area, with an estimated hard construction cost of $88,000,000. Phase III shall commence within twelve (12) months of Phase II Substantial Completion, with Substantial Completion within thirty (30) months of Phase III commencement.

### Section 5.2 Tax Increment Financing.

(a) **Pledge.** The City shall pledge up to $48,000,000 in TIF increment over the remaining life of the TIF District to reimburse Developer for eligible TIF costs incurred in connection with the Project. Eligible TIF costs shall include, without limitation: public infrastructure improvements, environmental remediation costs, parking structure construction costs, the public plaza and community center, and other eligible project costs under the TIF Act and the Lakemont Central TIF District Redevelopment Plan and Project adopted by Ordinance No. 2019-42.

(b) **Pay-As-You-Go.** TIF reimbursement shall be on a pay-as-you-go basis. Developer shall front all eligible costs and shall be reimbursed from available TIF increment as generated by the Project and other developments within the TIF District. The City shall not issue TIF bonds or notes for the Project.

## ARTICLE 14. COMPLETION ASSURANCE (ALTERNATIVE TO REVERSIONARY INTEREST)

### Section 14.1 No Reversionary Interest.

The parties acknowledge that the Construction Lender has advised that any reversionary interest, right of re-entry, possibility of reverter, or other defeasible fee estate in favor of the City would render Developer's fee simple title uninsurable, subordinate the Construction Lender's first-priority mortgage, prevent foreclosure, and render the Construction Loan unsecurable. Developer's fee simple title to the Property shall remain absolute and indefeasible, subject only to Permitted Exceptions.

### Section 14.2 Alternative Completion-Assurance Mechanism.

In lieu of the reversionary interest contemplated by Resolution Condition #8(h), the parties adopt the following alternative mechanism to satisfy the City's policy objective of ensuring timely Phase I completion while preserving Developer's fee simple absolute title and the Construction Lender's first-priority security interest:

(a) **Phase I Completion Deadline.** Developer shall achieve Substantial Completion of Phase I by September 30, 2028, as such deadline may be extended by Force Majeure under Section 12.3 and by the Construction Lender-approved extension periods.

(b) **Cure Periods.** If Developer fails to achieve Phase I Substantial Completion by the deadline (as extended), the City shall provide written notice of such failure. Developer shall have the cure periods set forth in Section 13.2 (90 days, plus additional time if diligently pursuing cure), followed by the Construction Lender's additional cure rights under Article 16 (not less than 60 days beyond Developer's cure period for non-monetary defaults, with extension if the Construction Lender is diligently pursuing a cure, including time necessary to obtain possession through foreclosure or a receiver).

(c) **Contractual Repurchase Option.** If, after expiration of all cure periods (including the Construction Lender's extended cure period), Developer has failed to achieve Phase I Substantial Completion and the Construction Lender has not cured or completed Phase I, the City shall have a contractual option to repurchase the Property, exercisable only upon (i) Developer's failure to achieve Phase I Substantial Completion within the agreed timeframe, (ii) expiration of all cure periods (including the Construction Lender's extended cure period), and (iii) the Construction Lender's prior written consent or full satisfaction of the outstanding Construction Loan balance. The repurchase price shall be the lesser of (A) the original purchase price paid by Developer ($9,200,000) or (B) the then-current fair market value of the Property as determined by an independent MAI appraiser mutually selected by the parties. The repurchase option shall be subordinate to the Construction Lender's mortgage and shall not be exercisable until the Construction Lender's mortgage is satisfied or the Construction Lender consents.

(d) **Enhanced Performance Security.** The $15,000,000 Phase I performance security under Article 8 shall be available to the City as liquidated security for Developer's Phase I completion obligation, in addition to the repurchase option. The performance security shall be the City's primary financial remedy for a Phase I completion failure, to be drawn upon only after expiration of all cure periods.

(e) **No Automatic Reversion.** Title to the Property shall not automatically revert to the City under any circumstance. The City's remedies for a Phase I completion failure are limited to the contractual remedies set forth in this Article 14 (the repurchase option and the performance security) and the termination and damage remedies under Article 13, in each case subject to the Construction Lender's rights under Article 16.
