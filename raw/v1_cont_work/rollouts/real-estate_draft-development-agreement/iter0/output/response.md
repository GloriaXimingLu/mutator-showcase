## Developer-favorable positions taken
- **No reversionary interest / defeasible fee** (Art. 2.3, Art. 9): substitutes liquidated damages + a repurchase option exercisable only after the Construction Lender's mortgage is satisfied + an enhanced completion guaranty — resolving the central conflict between Resolution #8 and the Lender's strict prohibition.
- **Environmental remediation cost and overruns allocated to the City** (Art. 5.2), with first-priority TIF reimbursement, reflecting the pre-existing nature of the contamination.
- **TIF protections** (Art. 5.2): priority reimbursement, City good-faith effort to extend the TIF, shortfall not a City default, and land write-down/infrastructure not charged against the $48M cap.
- **Assignment/Change of Control** (Art. 11): Key Person provision as alternative to rigid percentage thresholds, 60-day cure, carve-outs for internal transfers and approved capital raises.
- **Local hiring as a good-faith goal** (not a default trigger); living wage limited to Developer's own employees (Art. 7.4).
- **Prevailing wage limited to statutory "public works"** (Art. 7.2).
- **Expanded Developer termination rights** and deadline extensions for City-caused/regulatory delays excluded from the force majeure cap (Art. 3.3, 12.4, 13.3).
- **Full Lender protections** incorporated (Art. 14) to keep the construction loan viable.
