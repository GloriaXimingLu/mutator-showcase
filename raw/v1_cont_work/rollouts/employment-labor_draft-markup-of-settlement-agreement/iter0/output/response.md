## How the redline was produced

The draft was prepared by Delano's counsel (Engel & Associates), so the review was conducted from Greenleaf's perspective. Tracked changes were authored directly in the unpacked `document.xml` (not via the generic diff redliner, which would have stripped formatting), then packed and validated. Comments were anchored to the stable section-header runs.

## Key findings

**Critical (would impair enforceability / create exposure):**
- **OWBPA/ADEA release likely invalid** — only 14 days to consider (needs 21), no 7-day revocation period, no attorney-consultation advisory, and an Effective Date fixed pre-signature. Redlined to 21 days + new 7-day revocation (§5(g)) + redefined Effective Date + payments after revocation.
- **RSU valuation error** — draft uses the stale July 1, 2024 409A value ($10.00); the current Jan. 15, 2025 valuation is $12.50. Corrected value $62,500; aggregate corrected from $525,000 to **$537,500**. Added Compensation Committee approval condition and sell-to-cover withholding.
- **No government-agency/whistleblower carve-outs** — release, confidentiality, and non-disparagement could be read to bar cooperation with the **pending Oregon OSHA investigation**. Added carve-outs (§4, §6(v), §7).
- **Restrictive covenants void in 2 of 4 states** — California (B&P §16600) and Washington (RCW 49.62.020 layoff rule). Removed CA/WA from the non-compete; added a non-solicit savings clause and blue-pencil reformation.

**Significant:** workers' comp release limited to ORS 656.236 / Board approval; tax-allocation risk ($275k 1099 vs. $125k W-2) flagged for tax counsel; EPLI carrier (Timberline) consent added as a condition precedent; 2024-bonus wage representation flagged for verification.

**Moderate/concessions:** mandated positive reference narrowed to neutral; perpetual non-disparagement reduced to 3 years; re-employment/no-do-not-rehire flag flagged as a business decision.

**Added (were missing):** §19 Governing Law & Venue (Oregon/Multnomah County/jury waiver), §20 Notices, §15(a)(iv) return of property, §15(a)(v) trade-secret confidentiality with DTSA carve-out, and an integration-clause carve-out preserving the Equity Plan/RSU Grant.

**Litigation risk noted in memo:** the March 20, 2025 replacement hire (Raj Mehta, 6 days after termination) is strong pretext evidence that undermines the "reorganization" defense and supports the settlement value.
