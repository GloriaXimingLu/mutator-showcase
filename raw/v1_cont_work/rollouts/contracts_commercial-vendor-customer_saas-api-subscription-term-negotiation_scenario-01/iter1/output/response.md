## Structure (per Sutter's required 4-component format)

Each of the 7 terms includes: **(i)** proposed contract clause language (block-quoted, redline-ready), **(ii)** negotiation rationale and fallback ladder (with positions/red-lines tables), **(iii)** acceptability analysis anticipating Sandra Hu and Rafael Bowen objections, and **(iv)** regulatory/compliance constraints (HIPAA, NJ AI Act, FDA CDS guidance).

Plus cross-cutting sections: insurance gap ($25M/$50M → $30M/$60M with binding increase commitment), Board Risk Committee Tier 1 flag, regulatory backdrop, comparable-deal intelligence, concession sequencing/package strategy, and a GC-escalation table flagging the two Playbook deviations (graduated ETF on Term 3; 45-day opt-out on Term 4).

## Calibration logic applied

- **Term 1 (patient-safety, priority #1):** general cap conceded to 1.5× ACV (~$14.4M, Vendor's counter + Meridian floor + comparable-deal precedent); patient-safety super-cap held at the non-negotiable 3× ACV floor (~$28.8M); HIPAA/willful-misconduct uncapped.
- **Term 2 (audit, #2):** Langford Tier 2 annual baseline + Tier 3 secure-enclave escalation for cause; framed as NJ Act compliance baseline.
- **Term 3 (termination, #3):** graduated ETF 50%/25%/0% (endorsed by Delgado/Sutter as workable; flagged as Playbook deviation requiring GC approval); 150-day notice; 12-month transition at cost+5%; 45-day FHIR R4/HL7 v2 data return.
- **Term 4 (data usage, #4):** deployed as concession currency — 45-day opt-out + retraining-without-Meridian-data alternative for incorporated data (flagged as exceeding the 30-day red line, requiring GC approval).
- **Term 5 (SLA, #5):** Playbook fallback — binding benchmarks, 5%/10%/15% credits, 3-miss termination trigger, 20% aggregate cap.
- **Term 6 (IP, #6):** Playbook final fallback — Vendor ownership + irrevocable perpetual license + portability (endorsed by Delgado given CTO "hard no").
- **Term 7 (indemnification, #7):** Playbook fallback — shared defense, Vendor primary for AI-output claims; scope red lines (patient-harm + IP + regulatory + data breach) preserved; linked to Term 1 super-cap.
