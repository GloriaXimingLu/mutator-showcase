        I. Introduction and Scope  
      This memorandum analyzes the certified Application for Permanent Employment Certification (ETA Form 9089, PERM Case No. A-18247-63910, filed March 15, 2025; certified May 22, 2025) filed by Prismex Analytics, Inc. (    “    Prismex    ”         or the         “    Employer    ”    ) on behalf of Dr. Ananya Mehrotra (the         “    Beneficiary    ”    ) for the position of Senior Machine Learning Engineer. The purpose of this review is to identify every gap, discrepancy, and nonconformance between (a) the minimum requirements and attestations set forth on the ETA-9089 and (b) the Beneficiary’s supporting credentials as assembled in the petition package transmitted by the Employer’s HR Director on June 4, 2025.  
      The PERM has been certified; however, certification is not a finding that the Beneficiary in fact possesses every minimum requirement stated on the form. At the Form I-140 stage, U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) will independently adjudicate whether the Beneficiary met             all         minimum requirements — education, experience, special skills, and certifications —             as of the PERM priority date (March 15, 2025)    . Any unsupported attestation also exposes the certified PERM to audit, reconsideration, or revocation by the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) under 20 CFR 656.17 and 656.24. The gaps below therefore bear on both the integrity of the certified PERM and the viability of the downstream I-140 immigrant petition (and, to a lesser extent, the concurrently pursued H-1B nonimmigrant petition).

         VI. Remedial Recommendations  
      Recommendations are keyed to the gap items above and ranked by priority.             Priority 1         items must be resolved before the I-140 is filed;             Priority 2         items should be resolved before filing and documented in the audit file;             Priority 3         items are consistency/hygiene matters.  
       Priority 1 — Critical (resolve before I-140 filing)  
          R-1 (Items 8, 10) — Resolve the AWS Certified Machine Learning – Specialty certification gap.         This is the single most consequential issue. Options, in order of preference:  
             Assess the                 “        or equivalent        ”                 argument rigorously.         Determine whether the TensorFlow Developer Certificate (active as of the March 15, 2025 priority date), combined with the documented AWS SageMaker production-deployment experience at DataBridge, can be defended as         “    equivalent    ”         to the AWS ML – Specialty certification. This argument is weak because the two credentials test different competency domains (framework-specific ML development vs. AWS ML services/deployment). If pursued, support it with a detailed expert opinion letter from a qualified ML practitioner equating the competencies and documenting the Beneficiary’s AWS ML deployment work. Flag the denial risk to the client candidly.  
             Determine whether the certification is a bona fide minimum requirement.         If the AWS ML – Specialty certification was not, in fact, a genuine minimum requirement for the position (i.e., the Employer would not actually have required it of all similarly situated U.S. applicants), its inclusion raises a         “    tailoring    ”         vulnerability under 20 CFR 656.17(b). If it is not bona fide, the certified PERM is at risk and counsel should evaluate whether a corrected/refiled PERM is warranted — weighing the cost and priority-date reset against the Beneficiary’s September 15, 2025 STEM-OPT expiration and the parallel H-1B track.  
             Do not file the I-140 until this gap is resolved.         Filing an I-140 on a PERM whose stated certification requirement the Beneficiary cannot document as of the priority date invites a denial and potential PERM revocation exposure. The H-1B (I-129) nonimmigrant petition — which requires only a bachelor’s-degree-level specialty occupation, satisfied by the B.Tech + M.S. — is the immediate priority and is less directly affected by this gap; protect the H-1B timeline (target filing on or before July 15, 2025) and the cap-gap/October 1 continuation of status separately.  
          R-2 (Item 10) — Reconcile the K.3/K.4 attestations with the evidence.         Until and unless the certification gap is resolved on the merits, the         “    Yes    ”         attestations in Sections K.3 and K.4 are not supportable. Do not represent to USCIS or DOL that the Beneficiary meets all J.B.5 requirements unless and until the certification issue is resolved. Document the basis for any         “    equivalent    ”         determination in the audit file.  
        Priority 2 — Moderate (resolve before filing; document in audit file)  
          R-3 (Item 7) — Cure the R-proficiency gap.         Either (a) obtain a supplemental, detailed letter from DataBridge (Dr. Okonkwo) specifying the projects, tools, duration, and complexity of the Beneficiary’s R usage sufficient to support a         “    proficiency    ”         characterization (and reconcile the ETA-9089’s         “    some use of R    ”         language); or (b) if R proficiency is not a genuine minimum requirement, evaluate whether the requirement is overstated (tailoring risk) and whether it should have been stated differently. The internal inconsistency between the J.B.5 requirement (    “    proficiency in … R    ”    ) and the J.C experience narrative (    “    some use of R    ”    ) should be eliminated.  
          R-4 (Item 9) — Clarify the supervisory requirement.         Determine whether         “    supervise a team of 3–5 ML engineers    ”         is a bona fide             prerequisite         or a             job duty    . If a duty, reframe it as a duty (not a special-skill prerequisite) so that the Beneficiary’s 2-person supervisory history is not measured against it. If a prerequisite, either obtain evidence of broader leadership/coordination experience or reconsider whether the 3–5 headcount is a genuine minimum. Address the         “    data scientists    ”         vs.     “    ML engineers    ”         terminology as well.  
          R-5 (Items 11, 12) — Clean up the experience presentation.         (a) Expressly confirm — in a cover memorandum and in any audit response — that only the DataBridge experience (June 4, 2018 – present, ~6.8 years post-master’s, progressive, closely related) is relied upon to satisfy the 5-year minimum. (b) Either remove the Evalpoint (pre-master’s, non-ML) and NC State RA (during-degree, part-time, thesis) entries from the         “    qualifying experience    ”         presentation, or clearly mark them as non-qualifying/contextual so no adjudicator infers improper aggregation. (c) Because the RA cannot count (during qualifying education, thesis research), the missing NC State RA letter (Item 12) need not be pursued             if         the RA is not relied upon; if any reliance is intended, obtain a letter from NC State HR or the department administrator (Prof. Tsai being on sabbatical).  
          R-6 (Item 13) — Reconcile the FEIN.         Confirm the correct FEIN for Prismex Analytics, Inc. (via IRS notice or Cornerpoint Payroll Services payroll records).
